### Background Facts and Law

*(Note: all of the following is fictional.)*

Manufactured Interests, Inc. ("MIP") is a corporation incorporated in Delaware with its principal place of business in New York. MIP manufactures and sells a product called the BAM. The BAM is a small handheld device that releases a burst of air from a canister that MIP claims reduces pain and distress. The BAM is marketed as effective for treating anxiety and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), among other things. In 2009, MIP began selling the BAM nationally.

Outrage, Inc. ("Outrage") is a corporation incorporated and with its principal place of business in Delaware. Outrage's sole business is investigating and publicizing what it considers fraudulent corporate conduct. Outrage investigates companies through a combination of gathering publicly available information and surreptitiously installing recording and transmission devices in corporate offices.

Outrage began investigating MIP in 2012. Over the course of its investigation, Outrage gathered publicly available materials suggesting that the BAM was medically ineffective and that its release had been preceded by a corrupt approval process involving bribed FDA officials. Outrage also installed a recording device in MIP's board room.

In 2013, Outrage published its findings in a lengthy pamphlet entitled "The BAM Scam." The pamphlet relied on publicly available materials, the recording device evidence, and an expert analysis. The expert stated that, in her opinion, the BAM was medically ineffective, by which she meant that the BAM would not work for more than 50% of those who tried it (the "expert statement"). Outrage sent the pamphlet to several news organizations and relevant government officials. None of the news organizations or government officials reported on or responded to the pamphlet.

<!-- page: 3 -->

MIP claims that the BAM is effective in about 70% of users. You may assume that the true effectiveness rate for the BAM is an open question. MIP's own internal documents suggest that the BAM was effective in about 70% of users in clinical trials, but those clinical trials have never been peer reviewed. The expert's statement that the BAM is effective in fewer than 50% of users was based on her reading of MIP's clinical trial documents, which she obtained via FOIA request.

In late 2014, MIP filed suit in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York (SDNY) against Outrage. MIP pleaded causes of action for (1) misappropriation of trade secrets (based on the recording device evidence), (2) trespass (also based on the recording device), and (3) defamation (based on the expert statement in the pamphlet regarding the effectiveness of the BAM). The parties agreed that New York law governs all claims in the suit. Discovery ensued for eighteen months.

After discovery, MIP and Outrage cross-moved for summary judgment on all three claims. The trial judge, citing lengthy submitted declarations, entered summary judgment for Outrage on the trade secrets and trespass claims. In so doing, the judge stated that, while she had seen "lots of evidence" suggesting Outrage had installed recording devices, she "just didn't find it convincing." With respect to the defamation claim, the judge ruled that there were genuine issues of material fact, denying both parties' motions for summary judgment.

The defamation claim went to trial before a jury. After hearing the evidence, the jury returned a verdict for Outrage on the defamation claim. After the jury announced its verdict, MIP made a motion for judgment as a matter of law (JAMOL). The judge denied the JAMOL. MIP has now moved for a new trial on the defamation claim.

### Assignment Questions

1. After the jury returned its verdict for Outrage, was the judge correct to deny MIP's JAMOL?
2. Should the judge grant MIP's new trial motion on the defamation claim?
3. If the judge grants MIP's new trial motion, can Outrage immediately appeal the grant of a new trial?
4. Does the use of a jury in adjudicating MIP's claims raise any concerns? Any benefits?
