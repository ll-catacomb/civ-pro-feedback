### Background Facts and Law

*(Note: all of the following is fictional.)*

Virginia uses the First Restatement for horizontal choice of law. West Virginia uses the Second Restatement. Recall that, as stated several times in class, the choice of law analysis for a federal court in state A is the same as that for a state court in state A. Thus, an Eastern District of Virginia court facing a horizontal choice of law problem would use the First Restatement.

Under Virginia law, a plaintiff can recover for personal injuries sustained in a drone crash only if the drone operator was reckless. Virginia defines recklessness as an operator intentionally taking an action that was highly likely to cause injury. Under West Virginia law, a plaintiff can recover for personal injuries in a drone crash if the drone operator was negligent. West Virginia defines negligence as a failure to exercise the due care that a reasonable drone operator would exercise under the circumstances.

The Chudley Cannons are a drone racing team. Their manager is Ludo Bagman. The team owns about forty drones and maintains them in workshops located in Wheeling, West Virginia, and in Roanoke, Virginia. The team is incorporated in West Virginia and has its principal place of business in Wheeling, WV (that is where all its employees work, and where it holds its races). The team's board meetings are held in Charleston, WV.

Oliver Wood, a citizen of West Virginia, is a drone racer. He races for the Chudley Cannons and does so as an independent contractor. Oliver was performing a test run in Roanoke, Virginia with a Chudley Cannons drone called the Tornado 360. The drone crashed into Kingsley Shacklebolt, a citizen of Virginia, injuring him seriously. Kingsley was a spectator at the test run. The Tornado 360 turned out to be defective; it had a steering malfunction that caused many crashes. The malfunction had been reported to the Chudley Cannons by three previous operators, including Oliver himself. The Chudley Cannons had done nothing to fix the malfunction.

Kingsley retained an attorney and filed suit in the Eastern District of Virginia (EDVA) against Oliver Wood and the Chudley Cannons. Kingsley's complaint alleged (1) negligent operation of a drone against Oliver Wood, (2) negligent maintenance of a drone against the Chudley Cannons, and (3) reckless maintenance of a drone against the Chudley Cannons. Kingsley alleged damages of $300,000 in each count.

Both Oliver Wood and the Chudley Cannons have moved to dismiss the complaint in its entirety for failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted. In the alternative, both defendants move to transfer the case from the EDVA to the Southern District of West Virginia (SDWV).

### Assignment Questions

You are an associate in the firm representing Kingsley Shacklebolt. The partner in charge of the case asks you for a memo addressing the following:

1. How should the EDVA judge rule on the defendants' motion to dismiss? In particular:
   a. How should the EDVA judge rule on the motion to dismiss the negligent operation count against Oliver Wood?
   b. How should the EDVA judge rule on the motions to dismiss the two counts (negligent and reckless maintenance) against the Chudley Cannons?

2. How should the EDVA judge rule on the defendants' motion to transfer? In particular:
   a. Should the EDVA judge transfer the case, and if so, why?
   b. If the EDVA judge does transfer the case, what law will the SDWV apply to Kingsley's claims?

**Note:** You may assume that EDVA has proper personal jurisdiction over both defendants and that venue in EDVA is proper.
