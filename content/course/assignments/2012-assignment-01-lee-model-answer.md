## Staying out of federal court

Arthur Weasley (AW) can stay in state court because there is no federal subject matter jurisdiction (SMJ) in this case. AW's negligence claim is a garden-variety tort so there is no federal SMJ through arising under or the *Smith*/*Grable* exception.

Moreover, suing Ludo Bagman (LB) along with Crabbe Corporation (CC) will destroy complete diversity and keep the case in state courts (*Strawbridge*). Diversity jurisdiction demands complete diversity and meeting the $75,000 amount in controversy (AIC) requirement. AIC is met because the Nimbus was worth $200,000, but there is no complete diversity as AW and LB are both domiciled in North Carolina (NC). AW is a NC domicile since he lives and works in Durham without any intention to move to another state. Similarly, LB is a NC domicile as he lives in Charlotte and does not plan to leave. CC is domiciled in Delaware (DE), its place of incorporation, and Virginia (VA), its nerve center (*Hertz*), but again, that does not matter because § 1332 requires complete diversity. AW will want to bring a suit against both LB and CC since suing LB, who is "completely broke," will not get him damages.

If AW only sued CC, he should do so in VA or DE to prevent CC's removal to federal court for diversity. AW and CC have complete diversity and AIC is met, so in order to box CC in state courts, AW will have to file in CC's home courts of VA or DE (§ 1441(b)(2)). As explained below, VA and DE are not ideal courts for AW anyway so he should not file there.

## State courts

There are various state courts AW can choose from: NC, SC, GA, VA, DE.

GA, VA, and DE use the Second Restatement, which means they will apply NC law since NC has the most significant relationship to the case (*Babcock*). NC has strong interest since both AW and LB are from NC, CC does lots of business in NC, and the repair services and contract signing occurred in NC. NC does not follow respondeat superior, so AW cannot sue CC for LB's negligence. This is unfavorable because AW needs to sue CC to get any sort of damages (again, LB is penniless). Thus, AW should not file in GA, VA, and DE.

NC and SC apply the First Restatement, which in this case would apply GA law as that is where the blood was shed (*lex loci delictus*). GA applies the doctrine of respondeat superior, which would allow AW to sue CC for LB's negligence.

Between NC and SC, AW should file in NC because SC has no personal jurisdiction (IPJ) over either defendant. Neither LB nor CC has minimum contacts with SC (*International Shoe*). LB does not appear to have ever traveled to SC. CC does not sell its services in SC nor does it target or avail itself to the state in any way (*Volkswagen*, *Asahi*, *Nicastro*).

In contrast, NC state court would have IPJ over both LB and CC. LB is a NC domicile, so NC has general IPJ over him. Through its fifteen stores in NC, CC's contact with NC is arguably continuous and systematic (*Helicopteros*), but it is rare that a state exert general IPJ over an out-of-state defendant. However, NC certainly has specific IPJ over CC. CC has minimum contacts (*International Shoe*) and purposefully availed itself to NC (*McGee*). CC should reasonably foresee being haled into NC court (*Volkswagen*).

## Conclusion

NC state court is the best forum for AW to sue both LB and CC for negligence.
