# Assignment 1 Model Answer

## A) Grounds to Dismiss/Remand

The defendants could attempt to dismiss under 12(b)(1) for lack of subject matter jurisdiction. However, there is 1332 diversity jurisdiction. Groucho is domiciled in Texas (*Mas*). Sylvania is domiciled in Oklahoma (PPOB, *Hertz*) and Delaware (incorporated). Zeppo is domiciled in Delaware. There is therefore complete diversity under *Strawbridge*. The AIC requirements are met both in the claim against Zeppo (250k > 75k) and also against Sylvania (150k > 75k) so a 12(b)(1) motion to dismiss will not work.

The defendants could also attempt to dismiss under 12(b)(2) for lack of IPJ. 4(k)(1)(a) means that district court can piggyback off IPJ of state court, and Texas has a long-arm statute that goes to constitutional limits.

**Sylvania:** The minimal contacts for Sylvania (*Int'l Shoe*) are likely insufficient. While Sylvania clearly accepts TX domiciliaries like Groucho as customers, this might not rise to the level of purposeful availment since Sylvania does not target TX customers specifically, and may not even have known that Groucho was a TX domiciliary (making this more similar to *Hanson* than *McGee*). Groucho showed Zeppo DIS on Sylvania's website in Texas, but this seems to be too minimal. F&R? Contacts are very minimal, but there is some important evidence there, e.g. Chico witnessing Zeppo's enthralment with DIS. Unlikely that TX law applies to issues as between Groucho and Sylvania, so likely not F&R (R2 states allow for separation of issues). Court could dismiss the claim against Sylvania for lack of IPJ.

**Zeppo:** Minimal contacts for Zeppo are likely sufficient. TX is the center of gravity for the relationship between Groucho and Zeppo: this was where they interacted and the unfortunate chain started, where Zeppo first encountered DIS, and if Zeppo stole the password, he likely did so in TX. Engaging in this type of illegal conduct should have put Zeppo on notice for being brought to court in TX. F&R: TX might be convenient for Zeppo as mobile and been there before, but only stayed there overnight and now in CA; Chico's testimony is in TX and TX might have an interest in regulating illegal activity (e.g. theft of passwords) connected to its borders. Court will likely not dismiss claims against Zeppo.

## B) Contributory Negligence / Comparative Fault

If a federal court sitting in diversity, apply the forum state's HCoL rules (*Klaxon*). TX = 2R state, TX applies *lex loci delicti* unless § 6 factors strongly suggest something different.

Q becomes: Where was DIS located at the time of the theft?

It is unclear where a digital item is "located," if it existed in servers split between MA and SC. However, since basing location of digital theft on server location could implicate as many states as there are servers, this is probably an unwise policy choice.

If the item follows the owner as in *Harris v. Balk*, then it was located in Texas at the time of theft. And if it follows the location of theft, then it is located in CA. Either one of these categorizations will enable ascertainability, but it would also mean that the property is always moving.

If the item were located in South Dakota, where Groucho made his account and where the item was first taken out of his exclusive control, this would raise similar problems for a court deciding on the HCOL question as the location could be random, unconnected either to the owner of the item or the storer of it.

It might be easiest to say it is located in Oklahoma or Delaware, if we think of DIS as information that Sylvania "owes" to Groucho as in *Balk* and which would therefore follow Sylvania's location. Oklahoma might make most sense as PPOB, given that policy decisions regarding the security policies of the company were likely made there. Either categorization will be ascertainable and efficient.

If the Court does apply Delaware or Oklahoma law, then Sylvania will not be able to make its contributory negligence claims. However, if Court decides that digital items follow their owners, then this would be Texas, which has a contributory negligence system.

If the place of injury is Oklahoma, § 6 factors might change the forum here to Texas. Texas is the center of gravity of the parties' relationship (although less so vis-a-vis Sylvania). Conduct linked to the injury happened in TX (Groucho showed Zeppo DIS, where Groucho and Zeppo met, and Zeppo likely stole the password here). Texas does have a strong interest in regulating conduct with strong links to its borders, and Texas courts will be good at applying Texas law. The other two parties have no connections with Oklahoma. If the Court agrees to use TX law, then Sylvania will be able to assert contributory negligence as a complete defense.
