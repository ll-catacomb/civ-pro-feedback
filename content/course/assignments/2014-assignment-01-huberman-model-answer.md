# Assignment 1: Model Answer

Tonks (NT) is domiciled in Oregon. She lives in Oregon and we have no indication that she is only living there temporarily and has a permanent home elsewhere. WWW is a citizen both in Delaware (where it is incorporated) and in California, which is where its nerve center is (where they make all of their major decisions, have board meetings, and design their prototypes, *Hertz*).

NT could file in CA state court, and WWW will not be able to remove; WWW is a CA citizen. Because WWW is a citizen of CA, the CA court will have IPJ through general jurisdiction. CA is risky in terms of the damage cap because they are a 2nd Restatement state. If the CA damage cap is procedural then it will apply in CA state court and the damages will be capped at $20K, which is not something NT will want. If it is substantive, 2RST calls for a more in depth analysis which will weigh various factors from the Restatement and allow different state law to cover different issues. The damages limit will likely be seen as substantive, and the CA court would probably apply OR damages law since NT's injury was felt there, NT is a citizen of Oregon, and WWW has a large production facility in OR, so OR has an interest in both protecting its citizens and whether companies with a large presence in OR are creating products which spy on its consumers (many of whom are likely from OR). Also, the data was stolen and transferred from OR. However, CA could also argue it has an interest in protecting companies domiciled in their state from massive liabilities through the cap, and the party was in CA and the device was designed in CA. So while CA is nice because WWW cannot remove, NT cannot be sure that there won't be a $20K damage limit on her award.

If she files in OR state court, NT could face a problem with establishing IPJ over WWW. However, WWW produces all of its products in OR and employs over 2000 people there, so the proportion of its business conducted there compared to anywhere else could establish general IPJ. *Daimler*. If not, the court could have specific jurisdiction based on minimum contacts between WWW and OR. *International Shoe*. WWW has purposefully availed itself to OR by conducting business there, and the tort was completed in OR (because this was where NT felt the emotional distress). WWW could counter that the prototypes were not made in OR, WWW did not know where NT lived (her email address did not give a location) and that NT took the prototype to OR through unilateral third party action. *Hanson*. However, the difficulties in establishing IPJ might be worth it to guarantee that OR law (meaning no damage cap) will apply. OR is 1st Restatement, so HCOL analysis is "where is the blood?" The tort began (stealing and transmission of data) and ended (NT feels distress) in OR, so OR law will apply. A major disadvantage to filing in OR state court is that WWW could remove the lawsuit; there is diversity SMJ, see immediately below, and no bar to removal.

WWW cannot remove from CA state court and there is no federal cause of action or embedded federal question (*Smith*; *Grable*), but NT could file in CA federal court. If she does, there will be complete diversity (see above, *Strawbridge*) but the AiC requirement will only be met if the CA damage law doesn't apply. IPJ will apply through 4(k)(1)(a) (piggy back on state). Venue is proper because WWW is a citizen of CA. For COL, the federal court will use the HCOL rules of the state in which it sits to determine substantive law. From the analysis above, this means that NT could still possibly be

limited to a $20K damage award in CA federal court, plus there is still problem of federal courts not being receptive to outrage claims.

With OR federal court, similar to above, could only come in through diversity. Complete diversity is still met and this time there would be no problem with AIC because, as mentioned above, federal court will do HCOL analysis based on rules of state it sits. That would mean the first restatement, so likely OR law, which is good. Venue might be harder to lay given that WWW is not a citizen of OR but considering most of the tortious events occurred in OR, it shouldn't be a problem. IPJ will carry up through 4(k)(1)(a), but OR federal court will have same disadvantage as CA federal court.

Finally, NT could file in DE state court. Since WWW is incorporated in DE, it is a citizen there and the court would have IPJ over WWW. Like CA state court, WWW could not remove. To know which state's law would apply, we would need to know if DE is a first or second Restatement state (law would either be CA or OR law, DE law would not apply). If R1, analysis is like OR state court, above; if R2, analysis is like CA state court, above. Given the distance between DE and OR, NT might find filing in DE to be inconvenient. DE federal court would also be like OR or CA federal court, depending on HCOL, as per DE state court.

Thus NT should file in CA state court because WWW will not be able to remove and there is a good chance that the CA damage limit will not apply.
