## (1)

**§ 1331** — No federally created cause of action — negligence = state law claim & not created by federal law (*Mottley*). *Smith-Grable* exception might apply since key issue — compliance with federal statute (Willow Act) — is embedded within the negligence claim, and it is actually disputed and substantial. The federal government has a strong interest in adjudicating claims with important federal issues, here, preserving pedestrian safety. But it might bring too many cases to FCs (disturbing balance of federal and state responsibilities) — every time a defendant breaks this federal law, plaintiffs can bring federal suit despite a state COA. Also, question is likely very fact heavy. Arguably not very embedded/necessary to federal law — could prove negligence by not using "per se" theory. Maybe *Smith-Grable*, maybe not.

**§ 1332** — AIC sufficient against both parties (>$75k). So, there is complete diversity if Buffy is a Pennsylvania domiciliary (*Buffy [Penn] v. XH [DE & Maryland] and Faith [NC & SC]*), but no DJ if she's not. (*Strawbridge*). Place of domicile is the last place you lived w/o intention to leave at a particular time. Maybe Buffy's domiciled in Charlotte — moving to a different place for school doesn't change domicile and she might have an intention to return back there. More likely, she is domiciled in Pennsylvania. Buffy "decided she really liked Philadelphia," took a gap year, and applied to school here. She hasn't expressed an intention to return anywhere.

## (2)

### Where she CAN sue

**SC/NC** — Though these states have general IPJ (GIPJ) over Faith (PPoB and place of incorporation), neither has IPJ over XH, so can't sue here. No GIPJ over XH because XH is not domiciled in either state (not really at home — *Daimler*). No minimal contacts (no sales activity nor advertisements here) so no SIPJ.

**MD/DE** — Though both have GIPJ over XH (PPoB and incorporation), neither has IPJ over Faith, so can't sue here. No GIPJ over Faith because not domiciled in either state (not really at home — *Daimler*). No SIPJ because no minimal contacts (no business conducted in either state).

**PA:**

- **Faith** — No GIPJ (not domiciled/essentially at home). But likely SIPJ — PA has a long-arm statute that goes to constitutional limits, federal courts piggy back off state courts (4k1a). Need minimal contacts (*Intl Shoe*) among the defendant, forum, and subject matter of litigation. Faith conducts TV & newspaper ads in Philly and sells the vehicles at storage facility in Pennsylvania. Good case of purposefully availing & foreseeability of being in court (*McGee*). The fact that the ads are electronic isn't consequential. Faith targeted the forum (*Griffin*) — they rented parking facility in Philly. Fairness/justice? PA = good location for witnesses/evidence, accident occurred here, good for plaintiff's convenience as a PA resident, PA has an interest in protecting plaintiffs from harm

  occurring in-state and in people purposely availing themselves via doing business in-state, could maybe use Pennsylvania COL. Probably IPJ.

- **XH** — No GIPJ (same as above reason). SIPJ (have LAS and federal courts can piggy back off state courts via 4k1a) governed by minimal contacts. XH conducts no sales activity to consumers/business and no ads in PA; sells exclusively in VA. Arguably hasn't purposely availed to PA. Maybe it's sufficient that XH could expect its products to end up in Penn (stream of commerce) since it sells to various distributors who then sell to customers. But it's probably not — likely wouldn't foresee being hailed to court (*J. McIntyre*, *WW Volkswagen*) because of no ads/sales. Fairness/justice? — Same as above but inconvenient for XH given the probable insufficient contacts. Probably no IPJ.

**VA** — No GIPJ (not domiciled or essentially at home) for either, but, likely specific IPJ (LAS, FCs piggy back off state courts — 4k1a) for both. Both purposely availed themselves to the forum (*McGee*) — Faith advertises and sells here and purchases trucks from manufacturers at the Spike in VA. XH sells products exclusively in an "enormous" facility in VA. Fairness/justice? Both likely have minimal contacts here, failure to inspect (basis of suit) happened in VA, resultantly some witnesses/evidence are here, and VA has an interest in the business and redressing issues that originated within their borders.

### Where she SHOULD sue

**COL:**

- The issue of whether the § 5X5 violation can form the basis of the negligence claim is a substantive not procedural issue.
- Since failure to inspect = negligence only under NC/VA substantive law, Buffy should sue in a 2R state. Under a 1R state (PA, NC, SC), "blood was spilled" / *lex loci delicti* (LLD) is PA, which doesn't have this negligence law. Because XH has no MC in NC, VA is Buffy's best bet.
- 2R analysis — Start with presumption of LLD = Pennsylvania but consider other states' interests. The faulty car was manufactured in MD, it was sold and purchased in VA, inspection should've occurred in VA (i.e. where conduct-causing injury happened), see VA's other interests above. Ads targeted PA, aggrieved party domiciled in PA, harm occurred here, interest in addressing grievances that happened in state, see other PA interests above. But the conduct most closely tied to this particular issue, the failure to inspect, occurred in VA; the failure to inspect has nothing to do with Buffy, it was a failure between XH and Faith, whose entire relationship is centered in VA. Though PA's interests are likely stronger, Buffy's best shot at VA law is suing in VA.
