## (1)

Westley (W) *can* sue Montoya (M) in FL and MI; he *should* sue in FL.

### IPJ

State courts in FL and MI can likely exercise IPJ over M.

**FL:**

- M might be considered domiciled in FL; then FL can exercise general IPJ over him. M was registered to vote in FL and got a driver's license and opened a FL bank account with his RV address. On the other hand, he rented the RV plot for only one year and closed his post office box when he left.
- FL has specific IPJ over M if minimal contacts between M, FL, and the subject matter, and fair/reasonable factors met. M used a FL corporation, Bank of Florin, to cash the check that paid for the product at issue. This could be a minimal contact, but the bank account has little to do with the exploding computer. *International Shoe*. It seems unlikely that FL would be a fair/reasonable forum. M lived there before, but FL law will not apply, FL has little interest in the case, and FL is not convenient for W.

**MI:**

- M might be domiciled in MI. M was born and raised in MI. "Wanted to travel" — no indication he left MI with intention of having permanent residence in FL. Only rented RV plot in FL for one year. If shown M did not intend to stay in FL, M is still a domiciliary of MI. *Mas*. MI then has general IPJ over him.
- No *Daimler*-type IPJ because while MI has long-arm, no substantial and continuous contacts with MI.
- Specific IPJ likely. Minimal contacts met because M knew he was selling to a MI resident, shipped the product at issue to a MI FedEx, and was paid with a MI bank-issued check. Fair/reasonable factors: M is familiar with MI having been born and raised there, convenient for W, MI has interest in protecting citizens from defective products, the evidence (i.e., computer remains) is in MI.

**TX:** Little reason TX can exercise IPJ over M. Not domiciled: M was "intending to spend some time in Dallas" and the lease on the plot was only for four months — a definite leaving point. *Mas*. Also, no substantial, systematic contacts to justify *Daimler*-type IPJ. Finally, specific IPJ seems unlikely because few contacts linking M, subject matter (the "defective" Xerox Soprano allegedly causing injury), and TX. M cashed a check in TX (with a FL bank), did not manufacture or sell the product in TX; there is little purposeful availment otherwise. Also, not a convenient place for W (no sign he ever went or goes to TX), no evidence/witnesses there.

<!-- page: 2 -->

**VT:** No general or specific IPJ. While M might have spent some time in VT (during nationwide travels), M has not purposefully availed himself of the privilege of conducting any activities in VT. While M knew the computer would be used in VT because W told him, W just happened to be in VT for own reasons (favorite B&B) — not for any reason related to M or the subject matter. W's unilateral actions alone cannot force IPJ upon M. If there were minimal contacts, there is some evidence VT (the hotel wiring) and witnesses (hospitalization, fire inspector) in VT, and VT (place of the injury) law will likely be used, but this is likely not enough. *WWV*.

**OK:** No general IPJ; likely no specific IPJ. Not domiciled: M "happened to be" in OK. M received the check from W at a FedEx in OK and mailed the computer to W from OK, so there might be a minimal contact here with the subject matter (exploding computer). However, fair/reasonable factors reason against OK: not a convenient place for W, no evidence/witnesses there, no other contacts between M and OK, and OK law will not be used.

### Limitations Period

Limitations period is procedural, so use forum's own SOL.

W cannot sue M in MI or TX or OK because two-year SOL. W could sue under FL and VT's four-year SOL, but note: as stated, VT does not have IPJ over M. Best forum is FL.

## (2)

Westley can sue M in federal court based on diversity jurisdiction; Wesley can sue in federal court in FL.

### SMJ

*AU*: No AU SMJ because COA derives from state law. § 1331; *Mottley*. No *Smith-Grable* exception; no federal issue embedded in state COA.

*Diversity*: Since W is domiciled in MI, there is possibly diversity SMJ if M is domiciled in FL. § 1332. Mirrors analysis in Q1 about FL's IPJ over M; see above for why M can be considered domicile of FL. AIC > $75k also met.

### IPJ

All states have long-arm going to length of Constitution; federal IPJ piggybacks on state's exercise of IPJ under 4k1a. As stated, states that can exercise IPJ over M are FL and MI. Thus, federal courts in FL and MI can exercise IPJ over M.
