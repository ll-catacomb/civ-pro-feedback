## Substantive Assignment

Donkey is a businessman. He was born in New Hampshire and has lived there his entire life. He started a gum business when he was 17, quit school, and made the business his life's passion. Throughout the events relevant to this assignment, Donkey is registered to vote in New Hampshire and pays taxes there, and he runs his gum business out of a building built on his personal property in rural New Hampshire. He never incorporated the business. Donkey's gum business depends on purchases of raw materials from all over the United States. Donkey orders these materials online. Regarding sales, Donkey operates a sell-by-Internet business model. Customers visit www.DonkeyBestBlowingGum.com, create personal accounts in which they provide their addresses but not their financial information, and order their gum, using PayPal or a similar payment service. Donkey ships to the address provided by the customer, in the volume requested by the customer. By volume of sales, approximately 25% of Donkey's products are shipped to Massachusetts, 20% to New York, 20% to California, 10% each to Florida and Texas. The rest is sprinkled around various of the United States. Donkey buys no advertisements, and he maintains his website personally. Donkey maintains no salesforce, and he solicits no business. He depends on his website to sell gum.

Shrek was born in Rhode Island and lived there through high school, until he went to college at UMass Boston. When he graduated from college, he attended Harvard Business School, and decided he liked Massachusetts. At age 25, upon graduating from business school, Shrek used money he borrowed from his parents to start his investing business. At first, he lived at home in Rhode Island with his parents, with whom he has always stayed close. But over the next 18 months, as his business became more and more successful, he bought a luxury apartment in downtown Boston, where he set up his business office and conducts his meetings. Currently, he sleeps in the Boston apartment on weekdays, and returns home on the weekends to his parents' house. He has always been registered to vote in Rhode Island, but he started paying Massachusetts income taxes as soon as he began earning money, an arrangement he has continued for the past 15 years. Shrek buys and invests in businesses all over the world. Most of the businesses he invests in are incorporated in Delaware, but their principal places of business are located in more than 30 states and 15 foreign nations. He uses bank and investment accounts from Boston-based financial institutions so that he can use public transportation or app-rides to visit their locations (Shrek never learned to drive). Shrek's net worth is approximately $150,000,000.

MM Corporation, Inc. is a Massachusetts corporation with its principal place of business in Boston, Massachusetts. Its sole owner and employee is Mulan. MM's business model involves monetizing the cult of Mulan's personality. MM runs a website, a Facebook page, an Instagram account, a Twitter feed, and various other social media accounts and outlets. Mulan uses these platforms to post photographs she takes; short videos that she makes of her encounters with people she meets on the street, often while she is in disguise; and other short videos in which Mulan provides her opinion on current events and the quality of various products available on the market. All of the photographs and videos Mulan makes feature recognizable landmarks in Boston or elsewhere in Massachusetts, such as the Boston Common, the Public Garden in downtown Boston, beaches at Martha's Vineyard (a Massachusetts island), Boston's Bunker Hill, Boston's Museum of Fine Arts, and the Berkshire Mountains (in western Massachusetts).

MM never accepts an ad solicitation until Mulan has first tested/used the product and decided whether she likes it. One of the products MM endorsed in the fall of 2018 was Donkey's Best Blowing Gum. Donkey's gum was popular with kids aged 8–12. The video concluded with Mulan unmasking herself, to the delight of the children who had gathered for the free gum samples, and saying, "As you can see, Massachusetts loves you, Donkey's Best Blowing Gum. In December of 2019, Mulan received an email from Shrek. In the email, Shrek offered to buy all of the stock in MM, Inc., and asked Mulan to name a price to begin negotiations. Intrigued, Mulan sought bids from other investment firms to see what the best offer might be. The highest offer she received was for $5,000,000. Mulan emailed Shrek an offer to begin negotiations based on a minimum price of five million dollars. Shrek returned an email offering to begin negotiations with a maximum price of $4,300,000. Mulan replied with an email refusing the offer and terminating the negotiation. Shrek ended the exchange with an email dated January 2, 2020, saying, "You should have taken my offer, Mulan. Very shortly, your company will be worth less than my maximum offer. I'll make sure of that."

Around February 2 of 2020, Mulan received an email from a friend with a link to a YouTube video. The video was a short clip, about five minutes in length. It showed what purported to be a conversation in a conference room at the New Hampshire Donkey estate between Mulan and Donkey. In the conference room conversation, Mulan purportedly proposed that Donkey pay Mulan $50,000 in exchange for Mulan's endorsement of Donkey's Best Blowing Gum on "all of MM's social media accounts."

In fact, Mulan had never set foot on the Donkey estate. Mulan had never communicated in any way with Donkey. Mulan had never met Donkey in person, and had never been to the Donkey headquarters. This video was a "deepfake," a highly realistic fake video.

Mulan hired a private investigator. The investigator interviewed one Elsa Anna, who until just a few days earlier had worked for Donkey in the gum manufacturing business. She recalled that one day in early January of 2020, while she had been at the Donkey New Hampshire property, Donkey had called her in to record a five-minute video clip featuring a conversation between Donkey and Shrek (whom she recognized from photos in financial magazines). Anna recalled that the conversation centered on the endorsement of Donkey's gum products, and that at the end, Shrek promised to endorse Donkey's gum on "all of MM's social media accounts" if Donkey would pay Shrek $50,000. Massachusetts is a Second Restatement state for choice of law. New Hampshire is a First Restatement state. Both states recently amended their long-arm statutes so as to allow their courts to exercise jurisdiction whenever the U.S. Constitution would so permit.

The law in all fifty of the United States includes the tort of "Business Disparagement." Under this tort, a defendant is liable to a plaintiff if the defendant participates in the production of a false or misleading video that harms the reputation, public perception, or standing of a business in a way that causes a loss of value to the business. The last event necessary to complete the Business Disparagement tort is the infliction of the harm, in the form of the loss of value by the business. But: Massachusetts, unlike any other state in the United States, provides an affirmative defense to the Business Disparagement tort called the "Public Access" defense. Under Massachusetts law, the Public Access defense means that if a defendant proves that the affected business has constant and ready access to the public or to media outlets, then the plaintiff cannot recover.

## Assignment Question

You are an associate in the law firm representing MM. The partner you work for tells you that she's thinking of recommending that MM sue Donkey and Shrek for $500,000 each in either Massachusetts or New Hampshire federal district court. She asks you for a memo analyzing whether either or both of those two federal district courts will dismiss against either or both defendants for lack of subject matter or personal jurisdiction. She also asks you whether the Massachusetts Public Access defense will apply to the lawsuit, assuming that the Massachusetts federal court applies Massachusetts choice of law rules and the New Hampshire federal court applies New Hampshire choice of law rules.
