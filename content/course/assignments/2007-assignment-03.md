## Assignment 3 — Substance

You are an associate in the law firm representing Paula Corbin Jones in an appeal from the judge's summary judgment ruling (on page 158 of *The Power of Procedure*). The relevant partner asks you for a memorandum assessing the prospects for an appeal. As guidance only, the partner articulates the following questions (you need answer only those you think relevant): What is the applicable legal standard, given the procedural posture of the case? Did the trial court apply that standard properly in this case? If not, where did it get itself in trouble? Which theories of relief (quid pro quo, hostile workplace, IIED) are most likely prospects for an appellate court reversal, and why? As to the most likely prospects, where is the judge's reasoning strong, and where is it weak? Also, assume that we could somehow get additional evidence into the record at this point (actually this is unlikely, but assume); do you recommend any additional areas of investigation or forms of evidence?

Remember to support factual assertions with appropriate citations to the record (shorthand format, such as "PP pg. 80" would be fine for a citation to interrogatory Jones' interrogatory response on page 80 of *The Power of Procedure*).

**First note:** Don't spend time/space with LRW memo format or fact recitation. Get right to the analysis!

**Second note:** We haven't covered appellate jurisdiction and appellate standards of review yet. Assume that in this appeal, the appellate court would review "de novo," that is, the appellate court would give no deference to the trial judge's ruling and reconsider everything from scratch, conducting the same analysis as did the trial court. This is NOT something you need to discuss in your response.

**Time limit:** 150 minutes from download

**Word limit:** 750 words
