Begin by reviewing Fed. R. Civ. P. 8(a)(2).

Under North Dakota common law, in a lawsuit filed by a non-United-States plaintiff concerning events that occur in a location other than North Dakota, the court shall dismiss under the doctrine of forum non conveniens. A North Dakota statute provides that in any lawsuit alleging personal injury in which the plaintiff seeks to recover damages from medical expenses, the court shall dismiss the lawsuit unless the plaintiff attaches to the complaint complete copies of all corresponding medical bills. Finally, under a separate North Dakota statute, a plaintiff must file a personal injury lawsuit within two years of the action by the defendant alleged to have caused the injury. In 1990, Congress passed a statute providing that in any lawsuit filed in federal district court, any applicable limitations period begins to run when the plaintiff discovers the harm or injury giving rise to the claim.

A Canadian plaintiff living just across the North Dakota border in Manitoba, Canada filed a complaint against a company incorporated in North Dakota, with its principal place of business in Bismarck, ND, in the United States District Court for the District of North Dakota. Nothing was attached to the complaint. The plaintiff alleged that the company negligently shipped expired medicines from the company's storage facility in Pierre, South Dakota to them in their Manitoba home. The plaintiff alleged that they imbibed the medicines in their home four months before leaving for a scenic vacation in Montana. They became ill on vacation and spent several weeks in a hospital there before recovering. The plaintiff filed their lawsuit 22 months after they first became ill, alleging jurisdiction based on diversity of citizenship and seeking $300,000 in pain and suffering and hospital bills.

1. What arguments could the defendant use in support of a motion to dismiss the lawsuit? Analyze whether each argument is likely to succeed.
2. In what way do these arguments for the motion to dismiss differ vis-à-vis the application of the Erie framework?
3. Is there a concern for any of the Erie issues that using federal law for some aspects of it and state law for others may result in an incoherent system of law (like the "Frankenlaw" we discussed in our horizontal choice of law class)? If so, discuss how, and propose a solution.
4. Pick any one of the arguments for a motion to dismiss that you identified in your response to question 1. Discuss how your analysis and your recommended result further, are neutral with respect to, or undermine the justifications for the Erie doctrine that we discussed in class.
