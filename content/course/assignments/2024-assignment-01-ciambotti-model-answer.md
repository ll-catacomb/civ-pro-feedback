## QUESTION 1

There is no SMJ under § 1331 AUJ. This is a state law cause of action (*Mottley*) with no federal issue in sight, so no *Smith-Grable*. There may be § 1332 DJ if there is complete diversity, *Strawbridge*. Perrin satisfies the AIC by seeking over $75,000 from each defendant.

Perrin's domicile is TX. He was born and raised there and worked there as an adult. His monthly flights to CA show no intent to change domiciles.

Ishamael's domicile is NV. He was born and always lived there, and he operates his business from his home there.

Tuon's domicile is NY. She grew up, attended high school, and lives with her parents there. Renting property and operating a business in CA didn't change her domicile. Although she's "looking for a different opportunity," Tuon didn't change her voter registration, driver's license, etc., as would demonstrate an intent to change domiciles.

Damodred's domicile could still be TX. She hasn't changed her voter registration, driver's license, etc., to another jurisdiction. However, her domicile might have changed when she moved to NY, "decided she liked" and wanted to stay in Brooklyn, began working in NY, and only applied to schools in NY because she wanted to stay. Damodred's move to CA was only "temporary," suggesting an intent to remain in NY. If Damodred's domicile is TX, there is no SMJ over all three defendants. Perrin is also domiciled in TX, *Strawbridge*, and supplemental jurisdiction would fail (any anchoring claim would be based on § 1332, and § 1367(b) requires complete diversity).

## QUESTION 2

All states have LAS to constitutional limits. Under FRCP 4(k)(1)(A), we analyze federal district court IPJ by reference to the state in which it sits. Assume no defendant consents, and none was tagged. No defendant has anywhere near the kind of contacts required for "at home" IPJ, unless Damodred is domiciled in TX. But NY will not work even if so, see below.

TX potentially has no GPJ over anyone, and at least no contacts with Ishamael.
NY has GPJ over Tuon and Damodred but no contacts with Ishamael.
NV has GPJ over Ishamael but no contacts with Damodred.

Only CA might have sufficient contacts over the three defendants, but this is very tenuous for Ishamael. It is likely that no federal court can exercise PJ over all three.

Regarding SIPJ, Damodred operated the boats in CA, and Perrin was injured there. However, Tuon trained Damodred from NV, and Ishamael taught Tuon in NV. Although he marketed the boats in CA, consumers could take the boats anywhere (*Volkswagen*). This case concerns her negligent training (from NV), though Tuon also "instructed" and supervised Damodred in CA.

<!-- page: 2 -->

Damodred and Tuon's contacts in CA are significant. Damodred operated the boats in CA. Tuon rented space and supervised Damodred in CA. Ishamael has much weaker contacts, as he advertised the product online in CA. (*Volkswagen*). CA is convenient for Tuon (she rents property, drives, and resides there), and Damodred (she temporarily resides there). Ishamael is not far (a two-hour drive) from Sacramento. CA is a fairly convenient forum for Perrin since he flies there monthly.

CA has strong interests in deterring tortious conduct in CA that causes injury in CA, and in deterring the marketing of dangerous products for use in CA. The judicial system has a significant interest in CA as a forum. Evidence of Damodred's conduct and Perrin's injury are in CA (e.g., witnesses like Zarine, a CA resident; hospital records; etc.). Tuon's computer is in CA, with forensic evidence of her conversation with Damodred, including how short it was. Evidence of Ishamael's negligent training would be in NV, though. As a First Restatement jurisdiction, CA would apply its own law as the LLD.

Therefore, CA might be unable to exercise PJ over Ishamael, such that no federal court can exercise PJ over all three defendants.

## QUESTION 3

This issue is substantive, and a federal court sitting in diversity applies local (state) HCOL rules, *Klaxon*. The court will ultimately apply CA law, allowing contributory negligence.

CA, TX, and NV are First Restatement states. They apply the LLD (CA), which allows contributory negligence.

As a Second Restatement state, NY presumes the LLD (CA) applies, unless the § 6 factors weigh oppositely. They do not. The most relevant factors weigh in favor of CA:

(a) The only other interested state (NV) has weaker interests than CA's (injury was in CA from conduct in both CA and NV)

(d) As the victim, Perrin can expect LLD to apply. Damodred operated the boats in CA. However, Ishamael trained Tuon from NV, and Tuon trained Damodred from NV. They likely thought NV law would apply to their conduct.

(f) The LLD (CA) is more predictable/certain/uniform than locating vague elements of conduct.

(g) It's easiest for CA to apply its own law.
