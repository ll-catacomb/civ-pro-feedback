## Question 1

**There is D-J:** Fishlegs is ME domiciled; Hiccup is DE incorporated and has no physical PPoB, but there's no reason to anticipate a ME PPoB. AIC is alleged (albeit indirectly) to be over $1.25mil based on offers to purchase (> $75k) assigning value to stolen property.

If property is located in DE, might assert seizure on **QIRJ** basis. This presents *Shaffer* issue of seizing DE property and using it to drag defendant into court absent personal jurisdiction. If we do not take *Shaffer* seriously, then locus of property in DE would be enough. But where is the digital artwork located? Since artwork is incorporeal, this is a *Harris*-style problem. Here the code is spread across various states. Court should take functional approach and say digital artwork is located at the domicile of its owner (Hiccup), here DE. This brings certainty to parties and minimizes arbitrary location. Note that since Hiccup is incorporated in Delaware and has no physical PPoB, we do not have a concern of competing possible locations for the artwork based on owner domicile.

If we take *Shaffer* holding seriously, then QIRJ is dead and we need to establish IPJ with Fishlegs. Under 4K(1)(A), court looks to State of DE's jurisdiction. Fishlegs is ME-domiciliary, so absent consent, waiver or in-state tag (none of which are likely here), DE can't assert GPJ over Fishlegs. For SPJ, DE has long-arm. We likely have minimum contacts under *Int'l Shoe*, since arguably Fishlegs personally availed himself of DE law by incorporating a business there, serving as major owner and director of such business, and allegedly stealing a DE company's property. If property is located in DE then there is a strong connection between forum, Fishlegs and litigation since tort was completed in DE (where object was located at time of removal). Litigation in DE would also be F&R because DE has an interest in regulating (mis)conduct in its boundaries, DE law would likely apply (tort complete at location of stolen property at time of theft), and DE is not too inconvenient for Fishlegs. So likely have SPJ over Fishlegs.

**Electronic service:** fundamental requirement is notice reasonably calculated under the circumstances to apprise Fishlegs of the action. *Mullane*. Electronic service could be appropriate here considering can't find forwarding address for Fishlegs (after diligent effort), and she primarily conducts business online. No indication that the electronic messages are failing to reach Fishlegs, so no *Flowers* issue.

Does this FRCP seizure system meet **DPC guardrail** requirements of *Matthews*? Applying *Doehr* for private seizures: (i) Fishleg's private interest is low since seizure of the property should not prejudice Fishlegs, who allegedly has no direct ownership interest in the property to be seized. In fact, seizure may actually increase marketability of the asset to Fishleg's indirect benefit (and won't result in lost income); (ii) Risk of erroneous deprivation is low since (a) Fishlegs likely does not have direct ownership or possessory interest anyway given conclusive proffered assignment documents and (b) if Fishlegs did not steal the item (i.e. lawsuit ultimately fails), then the seizure won't deprive her of anything anyway. To reduce risk of erroneous determination, the court could require the movant (Hiccup) to post bond, allow Fishlegs to counter-bond to resist seizure, and/or hold a hearing *prior* to ordering the seizure; (iii) Plaintiff's (Hiccup's) interest is heightened by need to control and sell asset, but is attenuated by inconclusivity of whether it was actually stolen by Fishlegs, weighing against court interference. Gov't admin considerations are interesting since unclear how court will actually succeed in seizing this lost code. Seizure is judge (rather than clerk) administered, and hearing to be held in short order, but there is a higher risk of PDP issue if no bond is required. So seizure will likely be approved but not clear how it will be executed.

Fishlegs might counter that risk of erroneous seizure is high since the lawsuit does not plausibly allege that he stole the artwork, considering wide range of IP addresses who initiated the hack, a third party hacker or

## Question 3

Parties agree that Hiccup should rightfully have control over object. However there is a dispute over whether Fishlegs wrongfully removed such control, which is a necessary element for Theft tort. Quality documentary evidence does not relate to wrongful removal, so all we have is competing affidavits (so no *Scott* exception): Hiccup says Fishlegs wrongfully removed control, Fishlegs counters that he did not.

Hiccup is the movant and also bears burden of persuasion at trial. Based on its affidavits, Hiccup statements that the scrambling came from somewhere in New England, NH, MA, or ME, and "belief" that Fishlegs hacked the servers did not meet its burden to produce evidence that, if unrebutted and viewed in reasonably in favor of non-movant (Fishlegs), would result in no rational trier of fact concluding that Fishlegs did not wrongfully remove the object. Even if Hiccup had met this burden, Fishlegs rebutted it with his competing affidavit, resulting in a GIMF necessitating denial of the MSJ. *Celotex* (dissent).
