## Substantive Assignment

Assume the following (all parts of which are totally fictional).

Neither South Carolina nor North Carolina have the doctrine of respondeat superior. In other words, in neither South Carolina nor North Carolina may a plaintiff sue an employer for a tort committed by the employer's employee on the theory that the employer is responsible for the acts of an employee committed within the scope of her employment. Georgia, Virginia, and Delaware all have the doctrine of respondeat superior. Georgia, Virginia, and Delaware all follow the Second Restatement approach to choice of law. South Carolina and North Carolina follow the First Restatement approach to choice of law.

Arthur Weasley lives and works in Durham, NC, as a mid-level employee for the North Carolina Department of Inventions. Many years ago, Mr. Weasley inherited what is now (apart from his seven children) his pride and joy: a vintage, fully restored (with only original parts), mint condition Nimbus 1955 automobile, worth approximately $200,000. As just noted, Mr. Weasley lives and works in Durham, NC, but he has a side job as a salesman for his sons' joke shop business. This side job requires him to travel periodically to other parts of North Carolina, to South Carolina, and to Georgia for business.

There is only one shop in North Carolina with mechanics skillful enough, and with enough specialized knowledge, to work on Mr. Weasley's Nimbus. This shop, Crabbe's Repairs in Charlotte, is operated by the Crabbe Corporation. Crabbe Corp. is incorporated in Delaware; holds its board meetings, houses its chief executive officer and chief financial officer, and makes procurement and supply decisions in Danville, Virginia; and operates all of its 15 different car repair shops in various locations in North Carolina. As the name suggests, Crabbe's Repairs in Charlotte is located in Charlotte, NC.

One fine day, Mr. Weasley drove his Nimbus to Crabbe's Repairs in Charlotte for some repairs. Because he was an excellent customer, he was met by Crabbe's Repairs in Charlotte's chief mechanic and facility manager, Ludo Bagman. Bagman took his excellent customer for a cup of coffee, and the two discussed what needed to be done with the Nimbus. The following conversation occurred:

Bagman: "Now, Mr. Weasley, I know you have to travel regularly to Georgia on business. I can have the car ready for you tomorrow, but will you be taking it into Georgia tomorrow? I need to know because of that brake issue we've been discussing for some time now. If we don't get those brakes repaired, I'm worried that the car might not do well on a drive to Georgia; it's too long to go without taking care of the brakes."

Weasley: "Yep, you're right, Ludo. Let's go ahead and do the brake repairs today. I'll pick the car up tomorrow morning and do my trip to Atlanta, Georgia later in the morning."

Bagman: "Sounds great, Sir. And now, how are Molly and the kids?"

Weasley: "They're wonderful, Ludo, thanks for asking. You really must come see them. In fact, it will be a terrific chance for you to get away from your home and your work. You haven't left Charlotte for the

past three or four years, have you? It seems as though you hardly ever get out of your home in the downtown Charlotte area, do you? I mean, I know you were born here and have lived here all of your life, but it's time you got away from your home and your work for a bit. Come to Durham one day."

Bagman: "That's a wonderful invitation, Sir. It really would be lovely to see how all of your boys, as well as Ginevra and Molly, are doing. It would also be a nice thing for my wife, Nymphadora. I'm afraid we're about to lose our house to foreclosure because of a variable interest rate mortgage we signed, and we're not sure how we'll live next. Quite frankly, we're completely broke, and we could use a distraction. Thank you very much, Sir."

Weasley: "Call me 'Arthur.'"

The next day, Mr. Weasley came to pick up his Nimbus from Bagman at Crabbe's Repairs in Charlotte. Bagman greeted Mr. Weasley as usual. Before paying for the repairs, Mr. Weasley signed a "Contract and Statement of Work" detailing exactly what repairs had been made as well as the cost of each repair, broken down by parts and labor. The Contract and Statement of Work listed the brake repairs as having been complete.

Later that morning, Mr. Weasley drove the Nimbus to Atlanta, Georgia on business. On the way back home, while still inside of Atlanta city limits, Mr. Weasley's car came off the road and smashed into a nearby clothing establishment, Madame Maulkins' Robes for All Occasions. The Nimbus was totaled.

Mr. Weasley believes that the reason the Nimbus came off of the road was that Ludo negligently repaired the brakes. Mr. Weasley likes Ludo, but he LOVED his vintage Nimbus. He REALLY wants to get enough money to try to buy another one, and he doesn't care who he has to sue to get that money.

Mr. Weasley hires you as his lawyer. He asks you what his options are in terms of where he might sue, and the advantage and disadvantages of a lawsuit in each place. There is one catch: Mr. Weasley has heard that federal courts are pro-defendant. He VERY MUCH wants to keep his lawsuit out of federal court if at all possible.

**What advice do you give him?**
