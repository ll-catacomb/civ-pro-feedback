## Question 1

**AUJ:** Asserts 1 count of negligence, so state law cause of action and no AUJ (*Mottley*, § 1331) but there may be an embedded substantial and contested federal question so potential *Smith-Grable* issue. Likely both substantial and contested here. However, S-G exception unlikely as would sweep any cases involving any federal law violation into federal court, which would place undue burden on federal court system and blur state/federal courts division, the issue is primarily one of fact rather than one of law, and, not very important to the federal government (likely not an issue of such importance that the court wouldn't trust state court to handle).

**DJ:** There is still SMJ if S-G does not apply if complete diversity (§ 1332); AIC well above $75k. (*Strawbridge*) Buffy may be PA or NC domiciliary. On one hand, Buffy born and raised in NC and went to PA only for college (specific end time; schooling not usually = domicile change), suggesting NC domiciliary. However, Buffy decided she really likes PA after graduation and decided to spend her "gap year" there before law school, so possibly intends to reside there indefinitely. That said, these are still specific end times (one year "gap year"; three years law school), so close call whether Buffy has intent to remain indefinitely in PA (*Mas*). XH is a DE (incorporation) and MD (PPoB) citizen. Faith is an NC (incorporation) and SC (PPoB) citizen. If Buffy is an NC domiciliary, complete diversity with XH, no complete diversity with Faith. If Buffy is a PA domiciliary, complete diversity with XH and Faith.

## Question 2

**SC and NC:** XH: no general IPJ — XH not a citizen and does not do any business in those states so not "essentially at home" (*Daimler*); no specific IPJ — minimal contacts not met. Faith: general IPJ (NC incorporation; SC PPoB).

**MD and DE:** XH: General IPJ — DE (incorporation); MD (PPoB). Faith: no general IPJ — not a citizen of DE or MD; no specific IPJ — minimal contacts not met.

**PA:**

Faith: No clear basis for general IPJ (could be *Daimler* "at home" if PA = >50% of business but don't know % of business across states where operating) and no consent IPJ. For specific IPJ, PA has a long-arm (go to limits of Constitution) and FRCP 4(k)(1)(a) will piggy-back on state IPJ, so look for minimal contacts connecting defendants, subject matter of litigation, and PA, and fairness and reasonableness of PA exercising IPJ (*International Shoe*). Faith shows purposeful availment of PA customers, selling directly without an intermediary (unlike *J. McIntyre*), including storing goods in rented PA facility and targeting forum with significant advertisement, which is directly related to litigation (how Buffy became customer). Also fair and reasonable as a convenient place for Faith to litigate given noted connections and harm occurred there, PA has interest in protecting its citizens and regulating potentially negligent conduct of businesses targeting it.

XH: No general IPJ — not a citizen and not "essentially at home" (*Daimler*). XH potential PA IPJ. Minimal contacts issue as did not target PA, does not directly do business there, does not advertise anywhere. Reaching state through intermediary (*J. McIntyre*) or through "stream of commerce" foreseeability could end up there (*WWV*) not enough for IPJ. Possible that XH insisting companies it sells to sell directly to consumer is enough to establish greater connection through intermediary, but unlikely.

### Likelihood

Whether Buffy's negligence claim is likely to succeed will depend on which state's law will apply — she is likely to prevail only under NC/VA law. The issue of whether the § 5X5 violation can form the basis of her negligence claim is substantive (could affect pre-litigation conduct); HCOL analysis is necessary. The harm occurred in PA, which under RS1 (PA, NC, SC) would be the applicable law as *lex loci delicti* (where "blood" was spilled — i.e., Buffy got hit), so Buffy would prefer RS2 state (DE, VA) which will consider factors beyond this because claim unlikely to succeed under PA law. Since DE only has PJ over XH and not over Faith (as described above), VA filing preferable.

Under RS2, begin with presumption for *lex loci delicti* (PA) and then consider center of gravity: faulty truck manufactured in MD, sold/purchased in VA where alleged negligence (XH and Faith's failure to conduct front proximity detector test) occurred, Buffy hit in PA. PA and VA both have relevant state interests — both have interest in regulating business in the state (ads targeted PA/sale was to occur there; negligent sale occurred in VA and XH business concentrated there), central relationships in creating harm were formed both in VA (XH/Faith) and PA (Buffy/Faith), and the injured party (Buffy) likely PA domiciliary. Given mixed analysis, court may be more likely to remain with presumption and apply PA law, but a key fact in favor of VA law is that VA has the strongest relationship to the particular issue here, which is whether regulation § 5X5 can form the basis for the negligence claim, because that was where the failure to test occurred.
