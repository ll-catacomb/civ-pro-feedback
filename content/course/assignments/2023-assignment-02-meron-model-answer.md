# Assignment 2 Model Answer

## 1)

### SMJ

Duncan can try to dismiss for a lack of subject matter jurisdiction but is unlikely to be successful. The RRA provides a federal cause of action, so there is arising under jurisdiction. Supplemental jurisdiction to add Duncan is proper even though the count against him is a state cause of action (simple negligence) because the negligence suit arises from the same case or controversy (§ 1367(a)): Duncan's failure to inspect. Duncan can't move to dismiss under § 1367(b) because jurisdiction isn't based on diversity. He can't dismiss under § 1367(c) because there is no novel issue of state law, the state law claim doesn't predominate over the RRA claim, and there are no exceptional circumstances.

### IPJ

Duncan can try to argue that IPJ is unavailable in New York. There is no general jurisdiction for Duncan since Duncan, a PA domiciliary, is not at home in NY, but there is general jurisdiction for Leto since Leto is a NY corporation. There is likely specific IPJ for Duncan. NY has a long-arm statute that goes to constitutional limits, and because of 4k(1)(a), the federal court will piggyback on this long-arm. To dismiss, Duncan would have to argue that there are no minimal contacts between Duncan, NY, and the subject matter of the litigation. However, Duncan failed to inspect the train's coupling in NY, which is likely enough for minimal contacts (*International Shoe*). The fair and reasonable factors are likely present for NY. Any witnesses to Duncan's failure to inspect would be in NY, NY has an interest in safety measures that are undertaken in their state, and it can't be too hard for Duncan to go to NY since he often works in NY. Duncan can argue that he lives in PA and it is inconvenient for him to be sued in NY. He can also argue that witnesses to the injury may be in PA, and it is more convenient for Chani to be in PA because she lives there. Nevertheless, IPJ in NY is likely proper.

### Venue

Duncan can move to dismiss or to transfer for lack of venue. There is no § 1391(b)(1) venue in the Northern District of New York because not all defendants are residents of New York (Duncan is a PA domiciliary). There is no § 1391(b)(2) venue because no substantial event or omission occurred in the NDNY. Chani visiting her mother in Albany is not sufficiently related to the lawsuit.

### 12(b)(6)

Duncan can attempt to move to dismiss for failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted. Prong two of the RRA looks to the law of the state where the omission occurred. New York uses simple negligence so Chani can sue under the RRA. However, the choice of law clause is an issue. Under PA law, recklessness is the standard and Duncan wasn't reckless, so the suit would be dismissed. Under NY law, negligence applies. However, given the conflict between state and federal law on whether a choice of law clause is enforced, an Erie analysis is required. Under federal law, the suit must be dismissed. Under state law, the clause is not enforced, and negligence likely applies (see HCOL below).

Whether or not the choice of law clause is enforced is outcome determinative because the suit will be dismissed if federal, and thus PA, law is applied. Therefore, state law applies.

**Harlan**

The clause affects primary conduct because Chani (or passengers like her) may have decided not to sign the contract depending on which tort law applies. The clause also affects where a party decides to do business. State law applies.

**Scalia/Ginsburg**

The source of the federal law is federal common law, so both Scalia and Ginsburg will look to the twin aims of *Erie*. Following the Rules of Decision Act, state law applies when federal law would invite forum-shopping and yield disparate litigation outcomes. The choice of law provision encourages forum shopping because defendants benefiting from the clause will remove to federal court to enforce it. State law applies.

## 2)

The case must be dismissed or transferred, likely transferred. Possible districts are SDNY and EDPA. Duncan can only transfer to a happy court (*Hoffman*).

SDNY is a happy court. There is SMJ through arising under jurisdiction and supplemental (see above) and IPJ in New York (see above). Venue is proper in SDNY under § 1391(b)(2) because the omission occurred in Manhattan.

EDPA is a happy court. There is SMJ through AUJ and SSMJ. General IPJ exists in EDPA, and venue is proper under § 1391(b)(1), since all defendants reside in PA (Leto's PPoB), and Duncan resides in Philadelphia.

EDPA is better for Duncan. Duncan wants recklessness, not negligence. This is a substantive difference. PA follows the 1st Restatement. The injury occurred in PA, so PA law and recklessness applies. NY follows the 2nd Restatement. The injury occurred in PA, but Chani could argue the center of gravity is in NY because the omission was in NY, witnesses may be there, and NY has an interest in regulating safety in NY. If NY law applies, negligence is the standard, which is bad for Duncan.
