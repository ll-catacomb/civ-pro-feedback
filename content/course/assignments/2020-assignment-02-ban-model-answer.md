## 1. Motion to Transfer

SMJ is satisfied because defendant (Shrek; RI) and plaintiff (MM; NJ) are diverse; the AIC is $500,000 > $75,000. IPJ (RI long arm) and venue (§ 1391(b)(1) & (c)) are satisfied in D.R.I. because Shrek is domiciled in RI.

The facts of this case are analogous to *Stewart*.

Following *Stewart*, motions to transfer to comply with FSCs should be governed by federal statute and therefore any state law (MA or RI) that attempts to invalidate FSCs will not be applied, similarly to Alabama law. Therefore, *Stewart* points to the conclusion that the case should be governed by the federal transfer statutes, as interpreted in subsequent cases, particularly *Atlantic Marine*.

(*No Erie analysis here because* *Stewart* *is directly on point to the facts of our case.*)

According to *Atlantic Marine*, transfers should ordinarily be granted if plaintiff filed a suit in a forum other than the forum indicated in the FSC and defendant moved to transfer to the chosen forum. Private interests are not considered in light of the FSC and courts should only deny the transfer only when extraordinary public interest factors and circumstances unrelated to the convenience of the parties clearly disfavor a transfer.

Following *Atlantic Marine*, defendant's request to transfer to MA should be granted because there are no recognizable public interest factors present here that would clearly favor RI over MA.

Finally, *Hoffman* tells us that a case can only be transferred to a court where it could have been brought (SMJ, IPJ, VENUE). SMJ is satisfied for the same reasons in MA as in RI. For IPJ, MA has a long-arm statute going to the limits of the Constitution, and FRCP 4(k)(1)(A) makes it applicable in D.M.A. Minimal contacts can be established between defendant and MA. Defendant commuted daily to MA where his office was located. Defendant clearly enjoyed the benefits and protection of MA by conducting his business, including the business relevant to this lawsuit. It is both fair and reasonable to bring defendant into MA. Efficiency concerns point to MA (both parties, witness, and evidence available in MA) and MA has a strong interest in the behavior of two companies that principally do business in its own state. Venue is proper under § 1391(b)(2) since a substantial part of the events giving rise to the claim occurred in Boston: MM operates in MA, Donkey sells product in MA, deepfake video produced in MA, etc. SMJ, IPJ, and VENUE are all satisfied.

## 2. Choice of Law / Public Access Defense

Under *Atlantic Marine*, we ignore the choice of law rules of the transferor court. The choice of law rules of the indicated forum in the FSC applies.

MA is a 2nd Restatement choice of law state. Therefore, we consider the state interests and the center of gravity of the case by cutting cases into issues. The only issue here is the Public Access Defense. We start with the R1 presumption that NJ law governs because the injury, the loss of business value, occurred there. But the conduct causing the injury occurred in MA. Shrek is domiciled in RI and MM is incorporated in NJ but the two parties' principal place of business is MA. MM and Shrek signed the contract in MA. MA seems to be the key candidate state. Again,

efficiency concerns point to MA and MA has a strong interest in the behavior of two companies that principally do business in its own state. Therefore, MA law should be applied.

However, there is a COL clause in the contract which indicates that NJ substantive law should apply. An *Erie* analysis is required here because while MA law invalidates COL clauses, federal common law upholds COL clauses in contracts.

First, under *York*, we focus on the outcome. Applying state law will likely lead to different substantive law being applied, which will affect the outcome of the case. Apply **STATE LAW**.

Second, the Harlan test looks toward how the conflict will affect people's behavior. Knowing that COL clauses will be invalidated will most likely affect behavior of businesses — possibly some individuals. Apply **STATE LAW**.

Third, applying *Hanna* majority we first notice that the federal law at issue is Federal Common Law. Therefore, the question to ask is whether the twin aims of *Erie* are satisfied. COL is a significant issue that will lead to forum shopping and unfairness. Apply **STATE LAW**.

Thus, MA law invalidating COL clauses should be applied. MA has the "Public Access" (Affirmative Defense) and it is therefore available to defendant.
