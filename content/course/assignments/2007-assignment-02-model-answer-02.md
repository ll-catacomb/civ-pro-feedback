## 1. Preliminary Injunction Memo

Charming Lovely Art Posters, Inc. (CLAP) will probably not be able to obtain emergency relief against Mr. Picasso or against Strange Products And Tastes, Inc. (SPAT). There are four requirements to obtain emergency relief. The party seeking emergency relief must suffer irreparable harm if no injunction issues and be likely to succeed on the merits, the balance of equities must weigh in favor of the party, and the injunction must not be against public policy.

CLAP will probably not suffer irreparable harm if the court does not give CLAP emergency relief. CLAP's only losses are lost profits and costs from the sale of the "Eric Cartman" posters. CLAP will also argue that it will lose profits from the sale of other posters to customers interested in the "Eric Cartman" posters. Although this financial loss may be substantial, CLAP can still recover money damages for these losses. Thus, the harm is not irreparable. CLAP's money damages may be hard to calculate, which could warrant a finding of irreparable harm. As CLAP can probably present information and experts on this issue, however, the court will probably find that CLAP will not suffer irreparable harm if no injunction issues.

If CLAP can succeed on the procedural merits of the claim, then CLAP will likely succeed on the substantive elements of its claims. The primary procedural issues involve whether the court has subject matter jurisdiction and in personam jurisdiction. The federal district court would only have diversity jurisdiction if CLAP only sues SPAT because they are domiciled in different states. 28 U.S.C. 1332. If CLAP sues both SPAT and Picasso, then there would be no complete diversity because CLAP and Picasso are domiciled in Massachusetts. Diversity jurisdiction would also require that the amount in controversy requirement is met and it is not clear that the damages to SPAT would equal or exceed $75,000. The court could find that it has federal question jurisdiction if the declaratory judgment sought is for non-infringement of copyright because the copyright statute is federal law. 28 U.S.C. § 1331. The court could then exercise supplemental jurisdiction over the tortious interference claim because the second claim arises from the same nucleus of operative fact. 28 U.S.C. § 1367; *Gibbs*. This is a tenuous argument, however, because the real claim is a breach of contract claim.

CLAP will also have to demonstrate that the court has personal jurisdiction over Picasso and SPAT. Picasso is a citizen of Massachusetts, so the court has personal jurisdiction over him. SPAT, however, is not domiciled in nor does it have significant contacts with Massachusetts. The only argument for personal jurisdiction over SPAT is that SPAT's activities within other states could be affecting competition and people in Massachusetts. This is also not the strongest argument. If, however, CLAP can overcome these procedural obstacles, CLAP will most likely succeed on the substantive merits because the contract seems to clearly give CLAP the exclusive right to sell the posters.

CLAP probably meets the remaining two requirements, but cannot obtain emergency relief if it fails to meet either of the first two requirements. The balance of equities probably favors CLAP because CLAP stands to lose some possibly large and incalculable amount of profits. SPAT and Picasso's possible losses are probably small relative to CLAP's losses. There is also no indication that public policy would be against granting emergency relief in this case because the dispute seems limited to the parties without major implications for the public. CLAP will probably not be able to obtain emergency relief because it will probably fail to meet two of the four requirements.

## 2. Draft Complaint

DISTRICT COURT OF MASSACHUSETTS
CIVIL ACTION NO. ___________

CHARMING LOVELY ART POSTERS, INC.,
Plaintiff,

vs. COMPLAINT FOR DECLARATORY JUDGMENT AND AN EMERGENCY ORDER

STRANGE PRODUCTS AND TASTES, INC. and HARRY PICASSO,
Defendants.

### Introduction

This is an action for declaratory judgment that Charming Lovely Art Posters, Inc. (CLAP) will not infringe Harry Picasso's rights under 17 U.S.C. § 502. CLAP is also suing Strange Products and Tastes, Inc. (SPAT) for tortious interference with contractual relations.

### Jurisdiction and Venue

This Court has subject matter jurisdiction over the action for declaratory judgment under 28 U.S.C. § 1331 because the case arises under federal copyright law. This Court, however, has supplemental jurisdiction over the state law, tortious interference claim under 28 U.S.C. § 1367 because the two claims have a common nucleus of operative fact.

This Court has in personam jurisdiction over Picasso because Picasso is present and domiciled in Massachusetts. This Court has in personam jurisdiction over SPAT because its activities affect Massachusetts entities.

Venue is proper under 28 U.S.C. § 1391 because the Defendant, Picasso, resides within this Court's district.

### Parties

The Plaintiff, CLAP, is a startup company incorporated in Delaware with its principal place of business in Massachusetts.

The Defendant, Harry Picasso, is an adult citizen of the United States domiciled in Massachusetts. The Defendant, SPAT, is a company incorporated in Maine with its principal place of business in Connecticut.

### Facts

The Plaintiff and the Defendant entered into a written contract on July 15, 2007. The relevant portion of the contract stated: "I, Harry Picasso hereby grant a license of the copyright to my work, 'Eric Cartman in Guernica, Reclining,' to Charming Lovely Art Posters. In so doing, I intend to grant Charming Lovely Art Posters (and no others) the exclusive right to produce posters of the image in this painting."

Mr. Picasso has begun negotiating a contract with SPAT to allow SPAT to sell "Eric Cartman" posters in Connecticut. If Picasso signs such a contract, CLAP will be irreparably harmed because CLAP will lose an incalculable amount of revenue from the lost sales of "Eric Cartman" posters and of other posters to customers interested in the "Eric Cartman" posters.

### Prayer for Relief

The Plaintiff respectfully prays that this Court grant the Plaintiff declaratory judgment that its sale of "Eric Cartman" posters in Connecticut would not violate federal copyright law and an emergency order enjoining SPAT from selling "Eric Cartman" posters in Connecticut until the disposition of this action.

The Plaintiff respectfully prays that this Court award the Plaintiff 75,000 dollars in money damages.
