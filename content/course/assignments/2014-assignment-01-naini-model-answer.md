# Assignment 1: Model Answer

**Word Count: 872**

**SMJ citizenship:** Tonks (T) is domiciled in Oregon. WWW is a citizen of Delaware (state of incorporation) and California (principal nerve center, *Hertz*).

**T could sue in California State/Federal court, Oregon State/Federal Court, or DE State/Federal Court, but her best option is to bring her action in California State Court because WWW will not be able to remove to Federal Court.**

**Federal Court SMJ Analysis:** There is no FQJ because there is no Federal CoA or embedded federal issue. Complete diversity is satisfied because the parties do not share domiciles (*Strawbridge*), but the Amount in Controversy needs to be over $75,000 for diversity jurisdiction. AiC will depend on which law governs the limit on damages: if CA's $20k damages cap is in effect, T might be awarded less money *but* she won't have to litigate in federal court.

**California State Court:** If T files in CA state court, WWW is boxed in because removal is not allowed when an action is brought in a state where the defendant is domiciled. 28 U.S.C. § 1441(b)(2). IPJ over WWW exists through general jurisdiction: WWW is a CA citizen. The damages limit is substantive for HCOL purposes, so a court applies its choice of law rules to determine which state's laws govern. CA uses the 2nd restatement HCoL: the court dissects the event to determine which state has a controlling interest on a particular issue. CA will probably apply OR's rules for damages because that is where the harm occurred (i.e. where the need for damages exists), WWW is a major employer there so OR cares about protecting its residents from their activities, and substantial events (i.e. the use of the WAM) occurred there. However, an argument exists for CA law: by enacting a damages cap, CA may be attempting to protect its companies from crushing liability, and substantial events occurred in CA (the party, WAM's creation).

**California Federal Court:** Re SMJ, see above. Under 4(k)(1)(a), the federal court piggybacks on CA state court's long arm, so IPJ over WWW is satisfied, see above. Venue is proper because WWW is a CA citizen. 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b)(1). A federal court will use the HCoL rules of the state it sits in (CA) to determine the substantive law, see above. T would face the same problems as in CA state court along with the problems of litigating "Outrage" in federal court.

**Oregon State Court:** If T files in OR state court, WWW could remove the suit to federal court because of Diversity Jurisdiction, which we apparently don't want. T also needs to establish OR's IPJ over WWW. WWW's activity in OR is continuous and systematic (large manufacturing plant with thousands of employees), and the astronomical proportion of business conducted there should be enough for general jurisdiction. *Daimler*. If not, specific jurisdiction is established via minimum contacts. *Int'l Shoe*. WWW might argue that a significant portion of WWW's tortious activities occurred in CA (prototype built, WAM party). However, the photos were taken in OR and the data was received from OR. WWW also purposefully avails itself of OR law when conducting business, and the tort occurred in the course of business so there is connection and reasonable foreseeability. *McGee*; *WWV*. T unilaterally acted by driving to OR, but WWW knowingly invited people from around the country (even if email didn't specify geography), and WWW asked them to soberly transport the WAM in their cars so it knew this

prototype would be traveling somewhere by car (stream of commerce). *Kulko*; *Asahi*. Considering the close proximity and WWW's manufacturing presence there, it's not inconvenient for WWW to litigate in OR (so not a violation of DPC). Thus, IPJ is likely through either general or specific jurisdiction.

Under the 1st RST, the court will use "where is the blood?" to determine which state's laws govern. Here, the last event necessary for the tort to arise was when the plaintiff suffered emotional distress. T suffered emotional distress at her house in Oregon when she saw the photos and she immediately threw out the machine and sought counseling. Therefore, OR law governs and T is more likely to win greater damages.

**Oregon Federal Court:** Diversity jurisdiction exists because the lack of a damages cap means AiC is satisfied. IPJ is established through piggybacking on 4(k)(1)(a). Venue is proper because T can argue that a substantial portion of the tortious events occurred there (WWW will argue that the prototype was built in CA, but the photos were taken in OR and the use of the physical machine occurred there). 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b)(2). Federal Court would apply OR HCoL, so there is no cap on damages, but federal court is still hostile to Outrage claims.

**Delaware State/Federal Court:** IPJ exists over WWW through general jurisdiction (4(k)(1)(a) analysis same as above), SMJ in federal court same as above, and venue is proper because WWW is a DE citizen. If T files in state court, WWW can't remove for the same reason as CA state court. Choice of law analysis depends on if DE is a 1st (analysis like OR, above) or 2nd (analysis like CA, above) restatement state, but either way DE law wouldn't apply because no significant events leading up to or right after the tort occurred there.
