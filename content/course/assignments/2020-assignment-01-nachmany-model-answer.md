## SMJ

The location of the federal court does not matter for SMJ, so for both MA and NH federal courts: MM's suit asserts a state law cause of action, so no AUJ (*Mottley*). There may be DJ. AIC > $75K for each claim. MM is citizen of MA only (incorporated and PPoB in MA). Donkey is a NH domiciliary, so no DJ issue with him. Shrek may be either a RI or MA domiciliary. If he is a RI domiciliary, the federal court has DJ over both suits. If he is an MA domiciliary, MM/Shrek suit violates complete diversity (*Strawbridge*). Shrek is probably a MA domiciliary. The rule in *Mas* is: "you are where you are 'til you get where you're going." Shrek was a RI domiciliary through graduation from business school. Buying an apartment in MA, sleeping in MA during majority of the week, and paying taxes in MA, however, all point to MA domicile; voting in RI and living there on weekends point to RI domicile. The dispositive factor in favor of MA domiciliary is that a business, unlike schooling, is permanent occupation in MA. § 1367-SJ is no help if he is a MA domiciliary: this is a *claim* by a *plaintiff* (MM) against a *person* (Shrek) made a party under FRCP 20, whereby exercising SMJ would be inconsistent with § 1332 (incomplete diversity).

## PJ

PJ is different for NH and MA federal courts.

In NH, the court has general PJ over Donkey because it's his "real home court." The NH court may have specific jurisdiction, *International Shoe*, over Shrek. There is a long-arm statute going to the limits of the constitution, plus 4(k)(1)(a), so the only question is DPC. Assuming Elsa Anna is correct, Shrek has minimal contacts with NH by virtue of going there to transact business (the deepfake, the subject of the litigation) with Donkey. The exercise of PJ over Shrek is "fair and reasonable" — NH has an interest in stopping shady business in its state; at least some evidence is at Donkey's NH headquarters, plus Anna is likely there; it is convenient enough for Shrek, who traveled there at least once. One contrary factor is that the court will likely apply MA law.

In MA, the court has PJ over Shrek. Arguably Shrek is a MA domiciliary, but even if not, MA has a sufficiently broad long-arm statute, plus 4(k)(1)(a); Shrek has minimal contacts with the state through his business (which relates to this lawsuit), and the exercise of PJ is fair and reasonable because MA has an interest in preventing its businesses from being disparaged, MA is evidently a convenient location for Shrek, and MA law will likely apply. One contrary factor: most evidence is likely in NH. MA court probably has PJ over Donkey. He is a NH domiciliary, so the question is specific jurisdiction. The long-arm statute is sufficient, 4(k)(1)(a). Donkey arguably has minimal contacts — he sells the highest % of his products to MA (direct sales), and these products are related to the lawsuit. PJ fairness and reasonableness closer here — MA has an interest in its businesses and MA law will likely apply, but evidence is still in NH and MA is less convenient for Donkey.

## Public Access Defense

<!-- page: 2 -->

NH, an R1 state, applies *lex loci delicti* — "blood" was spilled in MA, because the *last* event (*Carroll*) is loss of business value. MM's business value is arguably "in the clouds," but the more likely interpretation is that as a MA corporation it lost value in MA. MA law applies.

MA, an R2 state, applies a presumption of *lex loci delicti*, but court considers 7 other factors. This is a substantive issue (regulates pre-lawsuit conduct, as lack of Public Access defense may make individuals less likely to disparage businesses). As above, "blood" was spilled in MA. There is no clear need for interstate system to apply non-MA law for a defense in MA about a MA business. Analyzing policies of forum vs. policies of other interested states, MA law should control with this affirmative defense, because MA's interest is greatest (how to protect a MA business). Justified expectations of MM were that being disparaged likely would not always result in recovery because of public access defense. The policies underlying the law: MA has chosen to promote free speech in this manner, which runs a risk MA was willing to tolerate of nasty conduct. Uniformity of result may cut either way: may not be uniform for resolution of business disparagement schemes originating in NH, but may be uniform for business disparagement against MA businesses (why should MM get to recover just because the scheme happened one state away, when other MA businesses might not get to recover if disparagement was planned and executed in-state?). Finally, MA court will have easier time applying MA law.
