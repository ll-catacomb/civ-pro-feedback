## Question 1

**MTD FRCP 12(b):**

**SMJ AU:** No arising under the *Mottley* rule (must meet § 1331 and Article III) since no federal COA created by TBCIAA. If embedded in state action (fraud in inducement = FI) is substantial issue of serious federal interest (TBCIAA is embedded since its requirements impact statements being "material" for FI), court considers whether exercising federal jurisdiction over the state COA sweeps lots of cases into federal court, if clear issue of law or fact-bound/specific and if trust state to adjudicate. TBCIAA important (Great Recession) but would sweep too many cases into federal court if each time defendant violates federal statute, FI could be heard in federal court (*Merrell Dow*), so it is unlikely there is AU.

**SMJ DJ (§ 1332):** GB = DE and NY (PPBO = nerve center, *Hertz*), AS = TX (likely changed from DE to TX since in residence in TX and has intention to stay in TX — though attending school in state doesn't normally trigger change, fact that shopping for condo and has job for after school in TX indicates intention to stay in TX) so complete diversity likely satisfied. AIC met ($150,000 lost is greater than $75,000.00).

**IPJ:** 4(k)(1)(a) piggyback on TX long-arm goes to limits of Constitution. Minimal contacts between GB, forum and subject (*International Shoe*): minimal contacts = GB purposefully reached into TX by operating TX branch (*McGee*), which is where key events occurred (the solicitation of the business that gave rise to this lawsuit). TX exercising IPJ would not offend fair play/substantial justice (witnesses with knowledge include Bellatrix, the contract was signed there, plaintiff in TX who has interest in a convenient place to litigate, TX has a strong state interest in regulating conduct in its borders).

**Venue:** substantial part of events giving rise to claim occurred in SDTX whereas court in NDTX. GB not deemed to reside in ND since not sufficient contacts to subject GB to PJ if ND were separate state (§ 1391(d)) so venue not proper = grounds for MTD or to transfer. § 1391(b)(3) is not available because there is a district where the action may be brought that is a proper venue (ex: SDTX).

Based on the analysis that the venue in the ND of TX is improper, a motion to transfer under the Transfer Statutes would be approved if the motion was to transfer to a federal district court where the lawsuit could have been brought (*Hoffman* — where venue and IPJ — federal courts as a whole have SMJ). As venue is proper in SD of TX (all contacts discussed in IPJ section occurred in SDTX) and TX has IPJ over GB, motion to transfer to this court would be granted.
