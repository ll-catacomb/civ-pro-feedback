## Assignment 1 — Civil Procedure 2, 2017

### Question 1: Remand

The USDC-DNJ should deny Malfoy's motion to remand. Removal was proper if the USDC-DNJ has SMJ over the case. The case was removed on diversity grounds. For diversity jurisdiction: (1) complete diversity between the parties, and (2) AIC exceeding $75,000.

**Complete diversity (*Strawbridge*):** Hermione and Farley are New Jersey citizens. Malfoy is a citizen of Delaware (state of incorporation) and Oregon (principal place of business, under *Hertz* nerve center test). No party is a citizen of the same state as an opposing party. Complete diversity is satisfied.

**AIC:** Hermione claims damages well in excess of $75,000. Malfoy's declaratory judgment action concerns the enforceability of Paragraph 9.75 — the forum selection clause. The value of the declaratory judgment is the cost of litigating in an unfavorable forum, which is likely to exceed $75,000 given the underlying warranty and products liability claims. Alternatively, the court may look to Hermione's counterclaim to measure AIC. Because Hermione claims well in excess of $75,000, AIC is met.

The USDC-DNJ has diversity jurisdiction. Remand should be denied.

### Question 2: Transfer

#### Whether to Transfer

The USDC-DNJ should transfer to the USDC-DOR under 28 U.S.C. § 1404(a). Transfer is proper if the case could have been brought in the transferee district and transfer serves the convenience of parties and witnesses and the interests of justice.

**Could the case have been brought in USDC-DOR?** Malfoy's principal place of business is Portland, Oregon. The USDC-DOR would have personal jurisdiction over Malfoy (it is at home there). The crash occurred on Interstate 5 near Eugene, Oregon — a substantial part of the events giving rise to the claims occurred in Oregon, so venue is proper under § 1391(b)(2).

<!-- page: 2 -->

**Convenience and interest of justice:** The accident occurred in Oregon. Physical evidence of the crash site is in Oregon. Hermione's attorney is in Portland. Malfoy's operations and most knowledgeable employees about the Comet 260 design are in Oregon. Witnesses to the accident are likely in Oregon. These factors strongly favor transfer.

Against transfer: Hermione and Farley are New Jersey citizens. However, their own attorney is in Oregon, suggesting they are already oriented toward Oregon litigation.

Transfer to USDC-DOR is appropriate.

#### Law Applied After Transfer

Under *Van Dusen v. Barrack*, when a defendant moves for § 1404(a) transfer, the transferee court applies the choice of law rules of the transferor court (USDC-DNJ, sitting in New Jersey). New Jersey uses the First Restatement.

**Enforceability of Paragraph 9.75 (forum selection clause):** Under the First Restatement, contract formation and validity issues are governed by the law of the place of contracting (*Albanese*). Hermione purchased the Comet 260 in New Jersey, so New Jersey law governs the contract's validity. New Jersey law holds forum selection clauses void. Therefore, Paragraph 9.75 is unenforceable.

**Products liability and warranty claims:** Under the First Restatement, tort claims are governed by the law of the place of the wrong. The accident occurred in Oregon, so Oregon law governs the products liability claim. The warranty is a contract claim; New Jersey law governs (place of contracting). The USDC-DOR, applying New Jersey First Restatement rules, applies Oregon law to the tort claim and New Jersey law to the warranty claim.

### Question 3: Supplemental Jurisdiction over Farley's Claims

The USDC-DNJ has supplemental jurisdiction over Farley's claims under 28 U.S.C. § 1367(a). The anchor claim — Hermione's warranty and products liability claims — is properly before the court on diversity grounds. Farley's claims arise from the same car crash, the same steering mechanism failure, on the same trip. They share a common nucleus of operative fact with Hermione's claims (*United Mine Workers v. Gibbs*). Supplemental jurisdiction is appropriate.

The § 1367(b) limitation: because the anchor is diversity, § 1367(b) bars claims by plaintiffs against persons made parties under Rules 14, 19, 20, or 24 where doing so would be inconsistent with § 1332's requirements. Farley is a co-plaintiff joining under Rule 20. § 1367(b) expressly limits "claims by plaintiffs" against persons joined under Rule 20. Because Farley is a New Jersey citizen joining against Malfoy (a DE/OR citizen), diversity as to Farley is independently satisfied anyway. But even if Farley's damages did not independently meet the AIC requirement, *Exxon Mobil Corp. v. Allapattah Services* held that § 1367 grants supplemental jurisdiction over the claims of co-plaintiffs who do not satisfy the AIC requirement, as long as at least one plaintiff meets AIC. Here Hermione's AIC is met. Farley's claim is properly before the court.
