### Background Facts and Law

*(Note: all of the following is fictional.)*

Alabama law prohibits the enforcement of forum selection clauses. Under Alabama law, a clause in a contract that purports to limit the amount of damages that a plaintiff can recover is per se invalid. Under New York law, Ontario law, and federal common law, forum selection clauses are valid, and there is no prohibition in either jurisdiction against contract clauses limiting damages.

**Note:** You may assume in answering this question that the validity of damages-limiting clauses in contracts is a substantive issue for vertical choice of law (*Erie*-analysis) purposes but is procedural for horizontal choice of law (state-state) purposes. Thus, the issue of the validity of a damages-limiting clause is just like the applicability of state statutes of limitation periods in federal court diversity cases.

Quality Quidditch Supplies, Inc. ("QQS") is an Alabama corporation with its principal place of business in Tuscaloosa, Alabama. (Note: the federal court whose geographic jurisdiction includes Tuscaloosa is the United States District Court for the Northern District of Alabama.) QQS manufactures high-quality racing broomsticks for Quidditch players around the world. QQS's motto is, "QQS: When jet speed won't cut it."

In September of 2016, QQS entered into two contracts, both negotiated and signed at QQS's Tuscaloosa offices.

The first contract was with Slytherin Straw Suppliers, Inc. ("SSS"), a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business in Manhattan (the federal district court having jurisdiction over Manhattan is the Southern District of New York). In this first contract, SSS agreed to supply QQS with top-notch, intensely magical, thick straw for QQS's racing brooms. Prices and supply dates were set in the contract, with the baseline price of $500,000.00 per ton of magical straw. The QQS-SSS contract included the following boilerplate clauses:

> Paragraph 9.75: The parties agree that any litigation relating to the subject matter of this contract shall take place in the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York.
>
> . . .
>
> Paragraph 713: The parties agree that under no circumstances will any recovery for breach of this agreement exceed the sum of $100,000.00 per shipment.

The second contract was with Hufflepuff Handles, Inc. ("HH"), an Ontario corporation with its principal place of business in Ontario, Canada. In this second contract, HH agreed to supply QQS with intensely magical broomstick-quality wood. The baseline price was $200,000.00 per ton. The QQS-HH contract included the following boilerplate clause:

> Paragraph 12: The parties agree that any litigation relating to the subject matter of this contract shall take place in a court located in Ontario, Canada.

<!-- page: 3 -->

In May of 2017, QQS took delivery of one ton of magical straw from SSS and one ton of magical wood from HH. Upon attempting to incorporate the straw and the wood into a new line of Firebolt 8s, QQS concluded that both the straw and the wood were defective. In fact, neither the straw nor the wood had any magical qualities, rendering both useless for QQS's purposes. Further investigation revealed that the evil Lord Voldemort had purchased both SSS and HH in December of 2016, and had attempted to sabotage QQS's operation by causing each company to produce inferior goods.

To maintain its production schedule for its Firebolt 8s, QQS turned to alternate suppliers. QQS obtained a replacement ton of magical straw and a replacement ton of magical wood from Lovely Longbottom, Inc. The straw cost $700,000.00, and the wood cost $450,000.00.

In August of 2017, QQS filed two lawsuits, both in Alabama state court located in Tuscaloosa. In the first lawsuit, the only defendant was SSS, and the only cause of action asserted was breach of contract. QQS sought $200,000.00 ($700,000.00 - $500,000.00) for the alleged breach. In the second lawsuit, the only defendant was HH, the only cause of action asserted was breach of contract, and the amount sought was $250,000.00 ($450,000.00 - $200,000.00).

Within two weeks, both defendants had removed their respective lawsuits to the United States District Court for the Northern District of Alabama.

In mid-September 2017, both defendants filed motions in their respective lawsuits. SSS moved to transfer the case against it to the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York, pointing to the forum selection clause in the QQS-SSS contract. HH moved to dismiss the case against it on the grounds of forum non conveniens, citing *Atlantic Marine* and pointing to the forum selection clause in the QQS-HH contract.

You are a law clerk for the Northern District of Alabama federal judge handling both lawsuits.

### Assignment Questions

1. With respect to the QQS-SSS lawsuit:
   a. Should the transfer motion be granted?
   b. If it is granted, is it likely that QQS's recovery will be limited to $100,000.00?

2. With respect to the QQS-HH lawsuit, should HH's forum non conveniens motion be granted?
