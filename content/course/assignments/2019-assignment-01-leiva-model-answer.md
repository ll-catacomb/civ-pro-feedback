# Assignment 1 Model Answer

**Parties:** Florean Fortescue (FF) domiciled in OR; Bathilda Bagshot (BB) a citizen of CA (PPoB) & DE (inc); Cho Chang (CC) a citizen of WA (PPoB) & DE (inc); Ted Tonks (TT) a citizen in ID (PPoB) & DE (inc).

---

## Question 1: Federal Lawsuit

### SMJ

No AUJ because state law contract claims; no federal claims. No *Smith*/*Grable* exception analysis because no federal issue embedded in state law claims. FF's diversity jurisdiction claims against BB lie (AIC = $112,500 > $75K), against TT lie (AIC = $112,500 > $75K), and against CC lie (AIC = $225,000 > $75K). There is also complete diversity with FF (plaintiff) domiciled in OR and the three defendants domiciled in DE, WA, ID, and CA (*Strawbridge*).

### IPJ & Venue

Can have general IPJ over all three defendants in DE since that is where they are truly at home. Similarly, have venue under §1391(b)(1).

General IPJ over BB in CA. CA has long-arm statute that goes to limits of the Constitution so can use 4(k)(1)(a) to establish specific IPJ over other defendants. Unlikely to have specific IPJ over CC in CA since CC only does business in OR and WA and no evidence of any Chang contacts in CA. CA does not have minimal contacts with CC (*Int'l Shoe*).

General IPJ over TT in ID but other defendants have no contacts with ID.

Only other state with Specific IPJ over defendants would be OR. OR has long arm statute that goes to limits of Constitution so federal court piggy-backs on that using 4(k)(1)(a). CC purposely availed itself of OR law by contracting with the OR Commissioners of Hogsmeade and exclusively doing business in OR (*Int'l Shoe*). BB also purposely availed itself of OR law by contracting with an OR farmer to get produce grown, harvested, and transported from OR (*McGee*). TT's minimum contacts with OR are a bit trickier because BB was an intermediary between FF and TT. Furthermore, it is unclear if TT knew where the corn was coming from since the contracting between BB and TT was done over the phone and internet, with TT never stepping foot in OR throughout this entire transaction. It is fair and reasonable to sue in OR because OR court has interest in protecting its residents, efficient resolution with all evidence of bridge closure in OR, convenient forum for at least three of the four parties who have been in OR recently to negotiate contracts for this transaction. But unclear if it is fair and reasonable to sue TT in OR since TT might not be on notice that jurisdiction would be available to OR court (*Denckla*). Yet, TT had inspectors ready to assess the corn bushels, signed a contract with BB about the corn, and had an interest in knowing where the corn was coming from. The state and plaintiff's interests, along with the likelihood of an efficient resolution in OR where most of the evidence is located, weighs in favor of IPJ over TT. Bridge closure evidence also allows proper venue to be laid under §1391(b)(2).

## Question 2: Strategic Considerations

**HCOL:** Federal court sitting in diversity will use the horizontal choice of law rules of the state in which it sits to determine what substantive law to apply (*Klaxon*). Want the federal court to apply negligent interference elements because those are easier to meet; FF more likely to succeed against CC with this lower bar.

Under 1st Restatement analysis, would use law of state where the harm occurred. In this case the harm occurred in CA because the last event necessary to complete the breach of contract was the second letter delivery in Sacramento. Under 2nd Restatement analysis, would use law of state that was the nexus of controversy and account for state interests. The spoilage of corn resulted from the Hogsmeade bridge closure, most of the parties contracted in OR, and an OR court has an interest in protecting its residents and regulating contracting behavior within its borders — so apply OR law. Prefer to apply OR negligence standard over CA intentional threshold. So use a 2nd Restatement court, either CA, DE, or ID depending on IPJ and Venue.

DE is the best court to file in because of 4(k)(1)(a) and long arm statute that goes to limits of Constitution, uses 2nd Restatement, and would likely apply OR negligence threshold. While CC's closure of the bridge did not account for "the needs of farmers in northwestern Oregon," it is unlikely a court will find the closure rose to the level of intentional interference with FF's contract. It was merely negligent because the bridge could have been repaired during the offseason in January. So we want a court that will apply OR law of negligent interference, leading us to file in DE rather than OR.
