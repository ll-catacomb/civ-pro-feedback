## 1. SMJ through AUJ or DIV

- **Under § 1331:** MM can't establish AUJ because the tort-claim is a state-COA with no federal issue (so no ***Smith-Grable***).
- **Under § 1332:** The AIC is > $75k. Donkey is domiciled in NH, and MM is a citizen of MA (PPoB and incorporation). If Shrek is domiciled in RI, then we have complete diversity; if in MA then we don't (**Strawbridge**). Shrek was previously domiciled in RI, and still votes there, but is likely now in MA. He has taken up residence in MA, works there, and lives mostly there. Simultaneously, he pays taxes, evidence of intent to stay (**Mas**). The court should likely dismiss for lack of SMJ.

## 2. HCOL

- In NH, a R1 state, the substantive law of MA will likely apply. The last event necessary to complete the tort was when MM's business, which is based in MA, lost value and thus was harmed (lex loci delicti). MM is an MA corporation, with content entirely generated in MA, reinforcing that the harm occurred in MA. Because MA-law applies, public access defense will be available.
- In MA, an R2 state, the deference to lex loci delicti means MA likely applies MA law. Additionally, it would not be unfair to apply MA law. True, the video was filmed in NH, so it is likely that the center-of-gravity is NH. NH has an interest in governing illegal conduct within its state. But MA has an interest in governing conduct between its residents/businesses (Shrek and MM/Mulan). In particular, these points apply to the issue of whether the public-access-defense, an affirmative-defense turning on the MA-based relationship of Shrek and MM, is available (**Babcock**). While it is unclear if NH has any interest in regulating an MA-based relationship, MA has an interest as the business harmed here was in MA. MA also has an interest in regulating liability for this tort as evidenced by the existence of the affirmative defense.

## 3. Personal Jurisdiction

Per FRCP 4(k)(1)(a), federal courts piggyback on the long-arm statute of the state in which they sit; hence both federal courts can exercise IPJ where their state courts could. With long-arm statutes going to the limits of the constitution, the focus shifts to whether there are minimal contacts.

### IPJ over Shrek: Likely in MA, but not NH

- If Shrek is domiciled in MA, then an MA federal court would have general IPJ over him. If Shrek is domiciled in RI, MA could still have general IPJ under **Daimler** because Shrek is essentially "at home" there — he practically lives there, works there, and his contacts are continuous and systematic enough. If no general IPJ, we need specific IPJ (*International Shoe*). MA has specific IPJ over Shrek because Shrek has minimal contacts in MA. He purposely reached into and availed himself to MA by living there, paying taxes there, doing business there, and he probably wrote the emails that began the whole sequence of events

from his MA offices (*International Shoe*). It would be fair and reasonable to exercise IPJ over Shrek. MA would be a convenient forum because key evidence (loss of MM value) is in MA, and MA has an interest in protecting both MM and Shrek and in regulating conduct (by Shrek) that occurred partially in its borders. Also, MA would probably apply MA law, see above.

- In NH, there is no general IPJ over Shrek. NH probably also has no specific IPJ over Shrek. Although Shrek traveled into NH to commit the tort, most of his conduct occurred in MA, and the harm was felt there, making this single contact too tenuous (*Worldwide Volkswagen*). It would also be unfair and unreasonable to sue in NH. MA would be convenient as two of the parties already live there; most of the evidence is online hence readily available (although a key witness lives in NH, she could testify through an affidavit/video-conference/short visit to MA). MA has a stronger interest in regulating its residents/businesses (here, both Shrek and MM), and MA law likely applies.

### IPJ over Donkey: NH (yes), MA (probably)

- Donkey is domiciled in NH so NH has general IPJ over him.
- MA probably has no general IPJ over Donkey. His shipments are not systematic and continuous enough when compared to his business in other states to establish "at home" general IPJ (*Daimler*). But MA can likely exercise specific IPJ over Donkey. Donkey has reached into MA and has purposely availed himself to the privilege of doing business in MA. Under the effects test (*Luban*), Donkey targeted an MA resident, whose reputation is tied to MA, and MM felt the brunt of the tort in MA. It would be fair and reasonable to exercise IPJ. Though Donkey is a NH homebody, MA has stronger interests in litigating this issue (see above), and MA law likely applies.
