## Substantive Assignment

**Part 1)** You are a legal staffer for Congresswoman Hermione Granger. Congresswoman Granger has been doing what she often does, which is read social science studies that might suggest reforms of the legal system. As an initial matter, Congresswoman Granger wants to understand more completely the law governing the US jury system. She asks for a memorandum that names two federal civil procedure doctrines that allocate power between judge and jury, and briefly (in two or three sentences for each doctrine) discusses the rationales, advantages, and disadvantages for each.

**Part 2)** While you are in the midst of drafting your memorandum responding to her first request, Congresswoman Granger comes to you again. She has in her hand a study by a group of social scientists purporting to show that persons adjudicating disputed issues of fact do a better job ferreting out truthful versus untruthful testimony if they do not see live testimony. Partly on the basis of this study, Congresswoman Granger has become convinced that the jury trial in civil cases as presently constituted is not a good idea. She asks you for a memorandum that discusses the implication of the following bill that Representative Granger proposes to put before the U.S. House of Representatives:

"Resolved:

- That a constitutional amendment repealing the Seventh Amendment our Constitution shall be drafted and passed by this House of Representatives.
- That federal judges wherever possible shall avoid deciding cases on the basis of live testimony, and shall resolve disputed issues of fact on the basis of transcripts of witness testimony, preferably testimony taken by deposition during the discovery phase of litigation."

Specifically, Representative Granger would like to know whether you think this proposal is a good idea, *i.e.*, should the proposed bill be enacted? Among other things, Representative Granger asks that you discuss how her proposed changes would affect debates over the need for/appropriateness of so-called "gatekeeping" stages of pre-trial civil litigation, such as motions to dismiss for failure to state a claim and motions for summary judgment. She asks you to include in this discussion an analysis of how, if at all, the proposed changes might lead to arguments for altering standards used to adjudicate (a) motions to dismiss for failure to state a claim, and (b) motions for summary judgment.
