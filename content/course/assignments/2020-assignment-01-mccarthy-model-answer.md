All-in-all, the MA court could dismiss for lack SMJ if Shrek is deemed a MA domiciliary, or for lack of IPJ against Donkey. The NH court might dismiss for IPJ against Shrek.

## SMJ

Business Disparagement is state law COA, with no federal issue, so there is no FQJ (*Mottley* + § 1331).

MM is incorporated in MA with MA PPOB. Donkey is domiciled in NH — he has lived there his whole life.

Shrek is domiciled in either MA or RI. Shrek was domiciled in RI, and he continues to visit his parents on the weekend and votes there. But Shrek has slept in MA during the week for many years, has business there, pays taxes there, and intends to continue doing all this, so his domicile may have changed to MA (*Mas*).

If the court determines Shrek is an MA domiciliary, it will dismiss for lack of complete diversity (*Strawbridge*). If Shrek is a RI domiciliary: complete diversity. The AIC > $75k.

## IPJ: If Case Brought in NH

Donkey is a NH domiciliary, so the NH federal court has general jurisdiction.

For Shrek more complicated. NH's long-arm statute goes to the limits of the Constitution; the federal court will piggyback, 4(k)(1)(a).

Because the video featuring Shrek and forming the basis for the claim was taken in NH, and perhaps uploaded there, there is likely minimal connection between the forum, theory of relief, and Shrek. Shrek had to enter New Hampshire to film the video with a New Hampshire resident. As discussed below, the harm likely occurred in MA, where the MA stock lost its value, so that provides only a weak connection between Shrek and forum. Shrek might have businesses in NH; however, MM is suing Shrek himself, not his businesses.

Jurisdiction in NH would be arguably fair and reasonable. NH has interest in governing misconduct that allegedly occurred in that state. It is convenient and efficient for all parties — evidence of the deepfake video (including Elsa Anna's testimony) is likely in NH. NH also has an interest in protecting its citizen (Donkey), and if the court determines that the injury occurred in MA (NH 1st-restatement), Donkey will be potentially protected in NH court because MA substantive law allows Public Access defense.

## If the Case Brought in MA

<!-- page: 2 -->

Shrek will either be considered actually at home (if a MA domiciliary) or likely essentially "at home" (lives five nights a week and pays taxes there, conducts his business there, plus the MA long-arm to the limits of the Constitution together with 4(k)(1)(A) completes the "at home").

The MA Court's exercise of IPJ over Donkey is more difficult. Donkey isn't domiciled/essentially "at home" in MA, so only specific IPJ is possible. We have the MA's long-arm and 4(k)(1)(a).

Regarding minimal contacts, Donkey ships 25% of his gum directly into MA (not through an intermediary, as in *Nicastro*), and the gum was the subject matter of the tortious conduct. The tort arguably concerned the MA activities of Mulan/MM, whose reputation is entirely located in MA (she only makes videos about MA locations). The prompt says the harm occurs where the business lost its value, in MA, as Donkey knew and (allegedly) intended. The arguments against IPJ over Donkey are that he maintains no offices there, has no agent there, and solicits no business there (or anywhere else); his business is "passive" in that he fulfills orders that he receives from the Internet, and as far as one can tell, has never visited the state.

## Q2

If MM sues in NH, a 1st restatement state, we must determine where "blood spilled." The prompt specifies that the last event necessary for the tort to blossom into existence is the loss of value to the business, which almost certainly occurred in MA, where MM is incorporated and where Mulan, its sole shareholder, resides and has her reputation. Under the 1st restatement, all major substantive issues are determined by MA, so the PA defense is available.

If MM sues in MA, 2nd restatement (blood-spilled + center of gravity/interests). The video was recorded in NH, but MM was harmed in MA, where the loss of value occurred and where its reputation is grounded (though it has viewers from everywhere, MM engages significantly with MA landmarks and public). Shrek's exchange with MM also occurred (to the extent it occurred anywhere) in MA, and MA has interest in protecting citizens and in governing the relationship between Mulan and Shrek; the availability of the PA defense relates more to this MA relationship than anything else. If MA court chooses MA substantive law, then the PA defense applies — more likely outcome. True, NH law means no PA defense, which if applied, ends up harming its own NH citizen (Donkey), and vice versa for MA law and MM. Overall, the defense is likely to apply because arguably most harm was felt in MA.
