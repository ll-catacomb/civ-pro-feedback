## (1) Yes, and he should sue in FL.

### PJ

#### General

*Likely FL:* There will be general IPJ wherever Montoya is domiciled, but Montoya's domicile is unclear. He perhaps left MI with intent to stay in FL (*Mas*). No other state clearly evinces intent to stay besides MI/FL; he has been transient since leaving FL. He registered to vote in FL, obtained FL driver's license, established place of residence/mailing, and opened bank account there. The one-year plot rental perhaps suggests no sufficient intent to stay, but then again, most residential leases are one year. Also, he closed PO Box without telling anyone, though he does still list it as his address. There is either general IPJ in MI (if no change of domicile) or FL (if domicile changed).

Note that FL's only other contact to this litigation is that Montoya opened his Florin account there, and that Florin is an FL bank, which is likely not enough for minimal contacts for specific IPJ.

#### Specific

For specific jurisdiction, we need (1) a long-arm statute going to the limits of the Constitution (LAS); (2a) minimal contacts linking Montoya, subject matter of litigation, and forum (*International Shoe*); and (2b) that the exercise of IPJ comport with traditional notions of fair play and justice.

*Likely MI:* If MI does not have general IPJ, MI has necessary LAS going to the limits of the Constitution (same with all states; assume applied below). Regarding minimal contacts, Montoya did "purposefully avail" himself of state in connection to the subject of litigation (*International Shoe*) by arranging for the computer to be sent there and forming a contract with a person who identified as being there during the negotiation, which was integral to the business transaction. One sale giving rise to this particular lawsuit is likely sufficient (*McGee*). Litigation in MI is likely fair and reasonable. MI has strong interest in regulating products shipped into the state that could harm its citizens; key evidence (computer remains) and witness (Westley) are there; it's convenient forum for Westley; it's not entirely inconvenient for Montoya as he grew up there and frequently travels cross-country; though MI law will likely not apply.

*Unlikely OK:* Minimal contacts is close, maybe probable. Montoya did "purposefully avail" himself of state/its resources in connection to the subject of litigation by negotiating the sale, receiving the check, and mailing the computer all from OK. But litigation in OK is perhaps not fair and reasonable. Though Montoya's contacts were not fleeting (i.e. staying there for a bit, visiting/posting about typical tourist spots, and doing transaction there), no witnesses/evidence of accident there; Westley's contacts are insignificant; location is not convenient for either; and OK law will likely not apply.

*Possibly TX:* Minimal contacts are unclear, but likely. Montoya "purposefully availed" himself of TX by doing bank business there. But this forum has little other connection with the litigation (e.g., RV plot not connected to litigation). On the other hand, TX bank access is how Montoya benefited from the sale of the computer, which might be just enough. But litigation in the forum would probably not be fair and reasonable. Montoya likely had only fleeting connection with state, and we don't know where he is now, so convenience of forum is unclear. TX is inconvenient for Westley. TX may have an interest in ensuring businessmen who avail themselves of state act legally, but unclear if some banking related to business activities is significant enough. Furthermore, TX law will likely not apply.

*No VT:* There are no minimal contacts. Montoya did not "purposefully avail" himself of state/its resources; did not target VT in particular, nor ship a substantial number of products to VT (*J. McIntyre*); and computer was only located in VT due to unilateral actions of Westley (*Hanson*). If minimum contacts *did* exist, VT is probably still not fair and reasonable. True, VT (place of the injury) law will likely apply, and evidence of wiring/fire chief witness is in VT. But Westley's contacts are relatively insignificant; Montoya has no contacts; and the location of forum is probably relatively inconvenient for both.

### HCOL

Statute of limitations is procedural, so law of the forum governs. SoL clock starts ticking when harm was suffered, aka 4/17/2017. MI, OK, and TX courts will dismiss under their two-year SOLs. FL and VT have four-year SOLs, and so will not dismiss, but there is likely no IPJ in VT.

Florida is only state where a court will likely have PJ and won't dismiss on SoL grounds.

## (2) Yes, and he should sue in FL.

### SMJ

*No AUJ:* Tort is state law cause of action, so no federally created cause of action (*Mottley*). No federal issue embedded in state claim, so no *Smith-Grable* exception.

*Most likely diversity jurisdiction:* AIC is sufficient; even smaller claim of 110k is > 75k. There is no complete diversity if Montoya is a MI domiciliary, since Westley's domicile is MI (*Strawbridge*). Assuming Montoya is FL domiciliary (see Q1), a federal court can hear the case.

### PJ

The same IPJ analysis from Q1 applies here, 4(k)(1)(A), so a FL federal court will likely have IPJ.
