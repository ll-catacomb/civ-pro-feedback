**We should file a petition of remand.** Defendant's removal petition was filed >30 days after the removable event (service). If we file a timely remand petition, EDPA will remand.

Because we'll be challenging removal, **I'd caution against a five-week vacation.** We have to file the remand petition within 30 days of removal — by June 6. So you have to be back at work roughly three weeks from now. Sorry!

**We should also file a PRCP 8(f)(2) motion.** Defendant didn't file the required affidavit. The EDPA will apply PA HCoL rules, *Klaxon*, and anything arguably procedural is procedural for HCoL, so 8(f)(2) is procedural for HCoL, Thus, if PRCP 8 applies as an *Erie* matter, the court will prohibit Defendant from disputing the damages we demand for pain and suffering. It definitely applies in PA state court, so all the more reason we want to end up there. Whether it applies in EDPA is a closer call. We apply the *Erie* tests. Under *York*, it's substantive because applying PRCP 8 could significantly alter the case's outcome by basically ensuring we get large pain-and-suffering damages if we win. Under Scalia/*Hanna* Majority, it's procedural, because FRCP 8 is both valid (it "really regulates procedure" under the broad *Sibbach* test) and applicable because it directly speaks to how defendants must respond to complaints. Under Ginsburg/*Hanna* Majority, PRCP 8 is substantive; FRCP 8 is valid (see above) but not necessarily applicable because you could narrowly interpret it as setting a minimum requirement that PRCP 8 merely adds to. Under Harlan, it's substantive because PA crafted PRCP 8 to shape primary conduct: to attract more qualified doctors to the state.

**Defendant's MTD will likely fail.** Applying Iqbal: The only conclusory statements to cross out are in ¶14. The underlying facts left over certainly nudge the allegations for each element from possible to plausible. They allege specific facts that could support findings of breach (¶¶10–11), causation (¶11), and injury (¶12). The complaint survives 12(b)(6).

**Defendant's motion to transfer will also likely fail.** Must the court transfer, i.e., is EDPA "unhappy"? No. EDPA has PJ over Daario. First, note 4(k)(1)(A) and the PA long-arm lay the groundwork for IPJ. There are minimal contacts (*International Shoe*): The key conversation kicking off the allegedly negligent sale took place while both Podrick and Daario's employee were in PA, and the allegedly negligent act — the switching of the plants — occurred in PA. And it would be fair and reasonable (*McGee*) for PA to exercise jurisdiction over Daario. It wouldn't be too inconvenient for the defendant, since Daario

has an office in PA. Plus, Stark, a key witness, works in PA — going toward both extent of the defendant's contacts and convenience for witnesses. Venue is automatically proper in EDPA because of the removal.

May the court transfer, i.e., is DSC "happy?" Maybe. It can lay venue under 1391(b)(2) because SC is where the blood was spilled (the allergic reaction). But PJ is a close call. Given 4(k)(1)(A) and the long-arm (and no sign of general PJ), we again look for minimal contacts. While Daario didn't specifically target SC, it did deliver there without intermediary, unlike in *Nicastro*; in fact, it advertised that it could deliver anywhere on the Eastern Seaboard (also unlike *Nicastro*). That's probably enough purposeful availment for minimal contacts. The *McGee* factors are iffier. On the one hand, it would be convenient to view the flower and the medical records in SC. And maybe SC would be convenient for the plaintiff (but the plaintiff selected another forum, so plaintiff convenience cuts both ways!). On the other hand, Daario's contacts in SC are hardly extensive. All it did in SC was send a delivery to a customer there. And while it is possible for Daario to send delivery trucks to SC, it might not be so convenient for it to litigate a lengthy lawsuit there.

Even if EDPA somehow found DSC "happy," should it transfer? Presume Podrick has the privilege of choosing the forum: Because he chose PA state court, maybe EDPA is closer to being Podrick's chosen forum than DSC. The *Gulf Oil* factors don't override that presumption. The private factors are mixed; true, it would be easier to access the flower and the medical records in SC, but it would be easier to get Stark's testimony and to view the relevant Daario office in PA. The public interest factors don't seem applicable. And it wouldn't be "in the interest of justice" (*Hoffman*) to transfer from EDPA, given that the initial removal from PA state court was improper and ought to be reversed. In sum, a transfer is unlikely.
