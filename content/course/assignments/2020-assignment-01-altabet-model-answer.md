## SMJ

**AUJ:** There's no FQJ because the well-pleaded complaint states a state, not federal, cause of action (*Mottley*). There is no *Smith-Grable* FQJ because there's no federal issue.

**DJ:** The complaint demands greater than $75k so AIC met for both.

MM is incorporated/PPOB in MA.

Donkey is a citizen of NH. Complete diversity between MM and Donkey.

Shrek's citizenship is unclear: RI or MA?

Shrek started as a RI citizen. He went to MA for schooling (definite end time, no change). Then he moved back in RI (still RI). He bought an apartment in MA, conducted business, paid taxes, and stayed on weekdays. He is in RI on weekends and registered to vote there. It's a close call if Shrek has an intent to remain indefinitely in MA (*Mas*). Given the 15-year pattern, I'd say he changed to MA. If true, there is no complete diversity and the court should dismiss the claim against Shrek. If Shrek has RI citizenship then there is complete diversity.

## PJ

**MA Court:**

There is no general jurisdiction over Donkey. If we analogize *Daimler*'s "at home" given Donkey's business activities, there is still none because 25% is not enough for continuous systemic contacts. If Shrek is domiciled in MA then there would be general jurisdiction over him, but that would mean no SMJ. If Shrek is domiciled in RI, then using MA banks is likely not enough for general jurisdiction.

There is unlikely to be specific jurisdiction over Donkey, but there should be over Shrek. Both states' long-arms go to limit of constitution and 4(k)(1)(a) allows the federal courts to piggyback. Regarding minimal contacts, Donkey did reach into MA to harm a MA-based business. He did not enter MA. The video posting is directing activity at the internet, not MA, even if he knows MM operates in that state. It's possible that MA might be center of Mulan's reputation (always filming at MA landmarks, *Calder*, Hollywood reputation). Fairness and reasonableness suggest that Donkey might be able to be brought in. He sells a significant portion of his goods to MA customers.

There's a much stronger case for bringing Shrek in since, in addition to targeting a MA-based business, he sent emails and might have been in MA when doing so. He also has constant business contacts with MA. Fairness and reasonableness should be met. MA also has a significant interest in protecting the reputation of resident corporations. It might be unfair for Donkey given lack of contact with the state, but Shrek has frequent contact. Convenience and fairness, however, does seem to posit NH as best since the relevant evidence should be there, and

a video can be viewed anywhere, although we don't know if Shrek doctored/uploaded the video in his MA apartment.

**NH Court:**

The NH court has general jurisdiction over Donkey (domiciled there) but not over Shrek (only one contact there, the making of the video).

The NH court might exercise specific jurisdiction over Shrek. See above regarding the long-arms, so we need minimum contacts. Minimal contacts relate defendant, forum and subject matter. Here the deepfake video appears to have been filmed in NH, linking the subject matter of the case, NH, and Shrek. Fairness and reasonableness might counsel for NH since relevant evidence should be there (such as Anna's testimony), and NH has an interest in regulating possibly illegal conduct occurring in its borders. But, other than going to NH for the filming, there appears to be no connection between Shrek and NH. This is possibly enough to allow for specific jurisdiction.

## HCOL

The public access defense is substantive (could affect pre-litigation conduct). HCOL analysis is necessary.

**NH Court:**

NH is First Restatement and will apply the law of where the last act needed to complete the harm occurred (LLD). The tort is complete when the loss of value occurs. MM's business is centered in MA and MA should be the location of damage. The affirmative defense will be available.

**MA Court:**

MA is 2R. LLD is the presumption, but check § 6 for whether other factors counsel override. The center of gravity is mixed. The recording was in NH, one participant's domicile was in NH, and the video was recorded in NH. However, the video may have been doctored in MA, the damage was there, and the scheme began with email sent to (and likely from) MA. NH has an interest to prevent deepfakes from being recorded there. But MA has an interest in defending resident corporations against falsehoods (especially one associated with MA landmarks) and the attack was made by a MA resident. At minimum, analysis is mixed and might even favor MA. MA law should apply under 2R and affirmative defense will be available.
