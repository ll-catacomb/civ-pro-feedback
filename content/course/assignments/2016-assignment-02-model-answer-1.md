## Assignment 2 — Civil Procedure 2, 2016

### Question 1: Motion to Dismiss

#### Count 1 — Negligent Operation Against Oliver Wood

The EDVA should deny the motion to dismiss Count 1. Because EDVA sits in Virginia and this is a diversity case, EDVA applies Virginia's First Restatement for horizontal choice of law. Under the First Restatement, the court asks "where is the blood?" — i.e., where did the tort occur? The drone crashed in Roanoke, Virginia, so Virginia law governs. Virginia requires recklessness for drone crash claims, not negligence. Count 1 pleads only negligence against Oliver Wood. Since Virginia law applies and negligence is insufficient under Virginia law, Count 1 fails to state a claim.

However, dismissal under Rule 12(b)(6) requires the court to accept all well-pleaded facts as true (*Twiqbal*). The facts as alleged — that the Tornado 360 had a known steering malfunction reported by three prior operators including Wood himself — could plausibly support an inference of recklessness even if the count is labeled "negligent operation." The court should consider whether the pleaded facts are sufficient to sustain any viable claim. If the court reads the complaint as pleading facts that support recklessness, the motion to dismiss Count 1 should be denied. If the court reads the complaint strictly as pleading only negligence, it should grant the motion to dismiss with leave to amend.

#### Counts 2 and 3 — Negligent and Reckless Maintenance Against Chudley Cannons

Virginia law applies to both maintenance counts (tort occurred in Virginia). Count 2 alleges negligent maintenance, which is insufficient under Virginia's recklessness standard for drone crashes. Count 3 alleges reckless maintenance. The facts support Count 3: the Chudley Cannons had received reports of the steering malfunction from three prior operators and did nothing to fix it. Knowingly failing to repair a defect that had already caused crashes is intentional inaction in the face of a known high probability of injury — this meets Virginia's recklessness standard. Count 3 survives the motion to dismiss.

Count 2 should be dismissed because Virginia requires recklessness; negligence alone is insufficient.

### Question 2: Motion to Transfer

#### 2(a) — Should EDVA Transfer?

The court should transfer to the Southern District of West Virginia (SDWV). Under 28 U.S.C. § 1404(a), a court may transfer to any district where the case could have been brought if transfer serves the convenience of parties and witnesses and the interests of justice.

<!-- page: 2 -->

The case could have been brought in SDWV: the Chudley Cannons have their principal place of business in Wheeling, WV, which is in the Northern District; however, the SDWV may also be proper if there is a connection to that district. More precisely, transfer is proper to any district with proper venue. The Cannons' principal place of business in WV and Oliver Wood's West Virginia citizenship mean that the SDWV likely has proper venue.

Factors favoring transfer: (1) the Chudley Cannons' witnesses and records are predominantly in West Virginia; (2) the Cannons' maintenance was performed in the Wheeling, WV workshop; (3) two of the three drone workshops are located in WV. Factors against transfer: the tort occurred in Roanoke, Virginia; Kingsley Shacklebolt is a Virginia citizen; Virginia witnesses such as the other spectators and medical providers are in Virginia. On balance, the convenience factors may favor transfer given that the Cannons' maintenance records and employees are in WV, but this is a close question.

#### 2(b) — What Law Applies After Transfer?

If the transferring court (EDVA) had proper venue, the receiving court (SDWV) applies the transferor court's choice of law rules under *Van Dusen v. Barrack* (defendant-initiated transfer). EDVA is in Virginia, which uses the First Restatement. Under the First Restatement, the blood is in Virginia (the crash occurred in Roanoke, VA), so Virginia law applies. Therefore, SDWV would apply Virginia law — including Virginia's recklessness standard — to Kingsley's claims, even though SDWV itself sits in a Second Restatement state.
