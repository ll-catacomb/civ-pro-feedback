All states relevant to this assignment have a standard products liability cause of action. Products liability requires a plaintiff to prove that the defendant sold the plaintiff a product, that the product was defectively designed or manufactured, and that the defect caused the plaintiff's injuries. Products liability is a tort. The Florida limitations period for a products liability cause of action is four years. Vermont's is also four years. Michigan, Oklahoma, and Texas have two-year limitations periods for products liability lawsuits. Michigan and Florida use the First Restatement for horizontal choice of law. Vermont and Texas use the Second Restatement for horizontal choice of law. All states relevant to this assignment have long-arm statutes that go to the limits of the U.S. Constitution.

Inigo Montoya was born in Detroit, MI, and grew up without family, living largely in foster homes. He loved vintage computers and computer games, particularly games with revenge themes. After graduating from high school in Detroit, and wanting eventually to pursue a career in the revenge business, he began to support himself by obtaining computers from the 1970s and early 1980s, restoring them so that they could play games from that era, and selling them to customers with whom he connected on the Internet. His business was a modest success, and having always wanted to travel, he bought himself a full-service recreational vehicle ("RV"), which he christened "Roberts," to use as his home. Hating the cold, in 2015 at the age of 23, he drove Roberts to Pensacola, FL, and rented an RV plot for one year, registering to vote there, obtaining a FL driver's license, and receiving mail at a local post office box. Financially, he opened a business checking account with the Bank of Florin at a Pensacola Branch, giving the bank his RV plot address and his post office box. The Bank of Florin was Florida corporation with its principal place of business in Pensacola, FL, but it had branches around the country. Although Montoya did almost all of his banking online (he received all of his statements electronically), occasionally he visited Bank of Florin branches wherever he happened to be, sometimes to deposit old-style personal checks that his customers sent him as payment. Perhaps illegally, Montoya never paid taxes.

In 2016, when the lease on his Pensacola RV plot expired, Montoya closed his Pensacola post office box (telling no one) and began to drive around the country in Roberts, parking for as long as he felt like staying, then moving on. He never stayed in one location for more than four months. He received any necessary mail at a FedEx facility near wherever he happened to be (he always told people with mail he wanted to receive which FedEx facility to use and did not worry about any other mail). As he began to travel, he bought an old Polaroid instant camera and a scanner, and he began to document his travels on his personal website, youkilledmyfather.net. He also ran his business from this website. He listed his (now-closed) Pensacola P.O. Box on the website, but he never used it. His business model was as follows. Montoya perused various traditional and non-traditional outlets, mostly online, across the world for no-longer-functioning vintage computer hardware as well as vintage software packages, particularly pieces that no longer worked. He bought these items cheaply and had them shipped to a FedEx wherever he happened to be at the time. He refurbished and repaired the vintage items, and marketed them on youkilledmyfather.net, which could be viewed anywhere in the world. He sold the products to customers from whom he received orders online, shipping them to addresses and receiving payments via Paypal.

In early 2017, Montoya finished work on a multi-month project, the restoration of a rare first edition of a Xerox Soprano personal computer, capable of running Guilder, one of the first multi-game personal computer software packages. Montoya listed the device on youkilledmyfather.net, with accompanying software, at $110,000.

Westley, a 30-year-old fencing instructor, contacted Montoya about the device via youkilledmyfather.net's chat function. Westley grew up and lived all his life Ann Arbor, Michigan. Westley conducted all of the negotiations via the youkilledmyfather.net chat function from his Ann Arbor apartment. He began all of his posts to youkilledmyfather.net's chat function, "Hello from not-so-sunny Michigan! Almost as bleak as a fire swamp, but twice as cold!". During the time he was negotiating with Westley through the website's chat function, Montoya happened to be in Oklahoma, parked outside Oklahoma City. Montoya enjoyed posting photos of the Oklahoma City Zoo, Museum of Science, and Museum of Art.

The negotiations between Montoya and Westley resulted in the following arrangement: Westley agreed to pay Montoya $110,000 via a personal check drawn on a Michigan bank, mailing the check to a Fed Ex facility in Oklahoma City. When he received the check, Montoya would ship the computer and accompanying floppy disks to a FedEx facility in Ann Arbor, Michigan, with instructions to allow Westley to pick it up. Westley told Montoya that he was looking forward to using the new machine for the first time on a vacation he was planning just outside Burlington, Vermont, where he intended to stay in his favorite bed and breakfast (in a historic building, constructed in the 1800s) and visit nearby lakes.

The transaction went off without a hitch. Westley mailed the check to the FedEx location that Montoya had specified in Oklahoma City. Montoya received the check, but did not immediately cash it. After mailing the computer from Oklahoma City to the specified FedEx location in Ann Arbor, MI, he hopped into Roberts and started driving south, intending to spend some time in Dallas, Texas. When he arrived, he rented an RV plot for Roberts in Dallas. The "lease" on the plot was for four months. When he arrived in the Dallas area, he visited a local Bank of Florin branch in person, deposited several customer checks including that of Westley, and did some other banking there related to his ongoing business.

On April 14, 2017, Westley retrieved the vintage computer from the FedEx in Ann Arbor, Michigan. He then drove his car through the night to Burlington, Vermont, arriving on April 17, 2017. Too excited to go to bed, he checked into his bed and breakfast, plugged in the computer, and started to play the vintage games. The computer caught on fire and exploded. Westley suffered moderate burns and had to be hospitalized in a Burlington hospital. His medical bills totaled about $150,000. When he recovered, Westley took the remains of the computer back to his apartment in Ann Arbor, Michigan.

Back in Burlington, the fire had been contained in the Westley's bed and breakfast room before spreading very far. The fire chief on the scene determined that the fire started at or near the outlet into which Westley had plugged the new computer, but did not attempt to determine further whether the wiring on the outlet was old or faulty, or whether there was something wrong with the computer's power cable.

Near the end of May, 2017, Westley received his bank statement and saw that Montoya had cashed his check at the Bank of Florin branch in Dallas, TX. He wrote to Montoya using the chat function of youkilledmyfather.net. He told Montoya about the fire and his injuries, and informed Montoya that he thought that faulty wiring on the computer's power cable caused the fire. He demanded that Montoya
