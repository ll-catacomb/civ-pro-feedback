## Applicable Law

In all jurisdictions relevant to this assignment, a tort exists called "fraud in the inducement to contract" (usually shortened to "fraud in the inducement"). The elements of a claim for fraud in the inducement are as follows:

1. a contracting party intentionally makes a statement (oral or written), or fails to make a statement, during contract negotiation with an opposite party;
2. such statement, or omission, is false or misleading;
3. such statement or omission is material;
4. the opposing party reasonably relies on the false or misleading statement or omissions by entering into a contract; and
5. the opposing party suffers damages as a result of entering into the contract.

Under Texas Law, the last event needed to make the tort of fraud in the inducement complete is that the plaintiff/opposite party suffers damages (which may be earlier than when she discovers the harm). Texas does not allow punitive damages for fraud in the inducement.

Under Louisiana Law, the last event needed to make the tort of fraud in the inducement complete is that the plaintiff/opposite party discovers that she suffered the loss or harm. Punitive damages are available for the tort of fraud in the inducement.

TX uses the Second Restatement for HCOL purposes. LA uses the First Restatement. Both jurisdictions' long-arm statutes go to the limits of the Constitution.

Case law in both Texas and Louisiana has established that, if a state or federal statute specifies that a contracting party must make a particular disclosure to the opposite party during contract negotiations/formation, then an omission of that disclosure, or a false statement made during that disclosure, is conclusively deemed to be "material" for the purposes of a fraud in the inducement tort.

Texas has four United States District Courts (Northern, Southern, Eastern, and Western). The Northern District of Texas is located in the northern portion of the state, and the primary courthouse is in Dallas. The Southern District of Texas is located in the southern portion of the state; the primary courthouse is in Houston.

Delaware has only one District, the United States District Court for the District of Delaware.

Louisiana has three United States District Courts (Western, Middle, and Eastern). The Eastern District of Louisiana is located in the eastern portion of the state, and the primary courthouse is in New Orleans. The Western District of Louisiana is located in the western portion of the state, and the primary courthouse is in Shreveport.

New York has four United States District Courts (Western, Southern, Northern, and Eastern). The Northern District is located in the Northern portion of the state, with its primary courthouse in Albany.

### The Truth in Bank Consumer Investment Accounts Act of 2012

In 2012, reacting to the Great Recession, Congress passed and the President signed the Truth in Bank Consumer Investment Accounts Act ("TBCIAA") of 2012. Section 9.75 of the Act provides as follows:

> **Section 9.75**
>
> Any investment account provided by a bank to a consumer shall be memorialized by a written contract signed by both the consumer and an appropriately empowered officer of the bank. If such investment account empowers the bank to invest the consumer's funds in bonds issued by business or corporations, such written contract shall specify the ratings level of bonds that constitute permissible investments, according to a large ratings agency.

No other portion of the Truth in Bank Consumer Investment Accounts Act of 2012 is relevant to this assignment.

### Facts

Gringotts Bank is incorporated in Delaware with its principal place of business in Albany, New York. It has for some time offered investment accounts in which consumers could deposit money with Gringotts, which would then pool it with other such accounts for investment purposes. Gringotts' typical investments were corporate bonds. Gringotts has a single branch in Texas, located in Houston.

You are a lawyer in the office of general counsel of Gringotts Bank; your office is in Albany, NY. On August 17, 2018, you receive a complaint that was filed and served in the United States District Court for the Northern District of Texas, in the Dallas Courthouse. Service of process was completed on that same day. In the complaint, Alicia Spinnet alleged that the court had jurisdiction "pursuant to sections 1331 and/or 1332 of Title 28 of the United States Code," and that venue "was proper under section 1391." She alleged that she was a citizen of Texas.

The complaint further alleged as follows: On or about May 2, 2018, Ms. Spinnet visited the Houston branch of the defendant, Gringotts Bank, seeking an appropriate place to invest her savings. During her visit, Ms. Spinnet spoke to Gringotts Customer Service Agent ("CSA") Bellatrix Lestrange. CSA Lestrange suggested that Ms. Spinnet consider a Gringotts Grade AAA Investment Account. In the conversation, CSA Lestrange explained that the name "Grade AAA" referred to ratings applicable to the kind of bonds that an investment account of this type would buy. More specifically, CSA Lestrange explained, Gringotts Grade AAA Investment Account funds would be used to buy only bonds rated AAA by the Standard & Poor's ratings agency; AAA is Standard & Poor's highest rating, i.e., in the company's opinion, AAA bonds are the least risky available on the market. Ms. Lestrange further explained that Gringotts would take a share of the return on the investment as its fee, "So we will keep your money safe for you because that's what is best for us." Ms. Spinnet told Ms. Lestrange (using the words that follow, or words to that effect) that she was interested only in "extremely safe investments," and that "AAA bonds sound OK, but if y'all were going to use anything more risky, I'd rather stick with the savings account that I have. It may be boring, but at least it's safe." Ms. Lestrange responded, "The Grade AAA Investment Account is perfect for you, then. We're experts. You can rely on us."

After hearing these statements, Ms. Spinnet explained that she wanted to open a Grade AAA Investment Account with Gringotts, but that she did not have a transfer slip with her that would allow her to deposit the funds (which were currently on deposit from another bank). Ms. Spinnet stated that she was to travel the next day to New Orleans to visit a friend, and asked if she could deposit $150,000.00 at the Gringotts branch in New Orleans. Ms. Lestrange said that would be fine, but she urged Ms. Spinnet to execute a contract opening the Grade AAA Investment Account immediately.
