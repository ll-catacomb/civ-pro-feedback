## Question 1

### Seizure

Working through the FRCP requirements, the court has SMJ through DJ (Fishlegs domiciled in Maine and Hiccup incorporated in Delaware/no facts suggesting Hiccup's PPOB is Maine to break complete diversity (see *Hertz*), and AIC well above 75k, assuming sale offers are valid proxy). Hiccup's lawsuit was already filed, so first requirement is met. The copies of the minutes and contract should be sufficient to establish Hiccup's ownership interest, asserted in Astrid's affidavit, for the purpose of requirement three.

The more complicated requirements for the court are two and four. First, it is unclear if the artwork materials are located within the geographic boundaries of the District of Delaware. The court must have power over the artwork to seize it, but here, power can't follow from GIPJ over Fishlegs because GIPJ doesn't exist over her in Delaware (and unclear if artwork is properly considered her property). All *Shaffer* concerns are raised, leading to a QIR analysis, even though the pre-judgment seizure wouldn't be used to establish jurisdiction over Fishlegs. If *Shaffer* preserved old QIR rule, court will likely consider artwork to be within Delaware even though location is ambiguous (given that the servers for the code/commands are on servers in three different states). Like in *Balk*, where the court located debt with the debtor, perhaps the court will locate the artwork in the only place where all requisite components are authorized to be exploited (only in Delaware). If Fishlegs did steal the artwork, each requisite component could be used by her in Maine, but if the court considers Maine as a location, they may choose not to effectively reward theft by refusing to find QIR in Delaware. This also aligns with the tort itself, which could find the artwork was in Delaware when "removed" from Hiccup's control.

If *Shaffer* extended a *Shoe* analysis, QIR may still be found. The FRCP authorizes seizure almost like a long-arm, Fishlegs purposely availed herself of Delaware more than the stock owner in *Shaffer* by choosing to participate in an incorporation in DE, benefiting from its corp-friendly rules. The artwork is the subject of the litigation and therefore bears a strong relationship, and there is some link between Fishlegs' ownership interest, given that she assumedly will profit from Hiccup's work/pending sale, and the cause of action for theft, which (if true) let her profit far beyond her valid ownership of 1/3 the voting stock.

As such, the court could seize, but the FRCP demands a hearing within 60 days, where Fishlegs could raise PDP concerns about the pre-hearing seizure as compared to post-lawsuit seizure (*Eldridge*). She may be unsuccessful. First, Hiccup's private interests outweigh Fishlegs': all rights/interests in the artwork are vested only in Hiccup, by a contract to which Fishlegs was a party. If Fishlegs did not steal the artwork, her private

interests as CEO/voter weigh in favor of seizure to prevent further destruction while legal issues are sorted. If she did steal it, court will find seizure *more* compelling as any extra private interest there is illicit, and livelihood is not at stake for Fishlegs more than Astrid and Ruffnut. Second, the public interest weighs in favor of less litigation/resources and restoring rightful property interests; Fishlegs could mess with artwork further, so seizure is urgent. Third, though the risk of erroneous seizure is mitigated by the post-seizure hearing, the underlying tort suit requires tons of evidence. Seizure should turn on whether Fishlegs' stole the artwork, which will not be determined until the case is fully decided. Because of this, the court should **alter** the seizure motion to require a bond to protect Fishlegs and allow a counterbond so Fishlegs can repossess her interest (if that comports with how corporate ownerships function).

### Electronic Notice

Actual notice is not required to satisfy PDP so long as a reasonably calculated attempt was made (*Mullane*). Here, communication has only occurred electronically. Astrid and Ruffnut do not have a physical address for Fishlegs and don't have a duty to search for an unknown address (*Mullane*) or undergo another special heroic effort to ensure receipt (*Dusenberry*). If any notice-related communications had failed to send/bounced back, Fishlegs might have some defense (*Flowers*), but on these facts, electronic notice is sufficient.

## Question 2

Hiccup can control seating a jury by staying on the equity side of the law/equity divide. To do so, Hiccup must avoid seeking damages (remedies at law) and instead demand an injunction (such as forcing Fishlegs to return artwork to pre-theft state/continue duties or desist from theft) and/or perhaps restitution (disgorging any ill-gotten gains that may somehow have accumulated when Hiccup's possession was disrupted).

## Question 3

The court should not decide this case on SJ. Hiccup is the moving party and bears the burden of persuasion at trial as plaintiff. Hiccup might have produced evidence compelling a rational jury to find that Fishlegs stole the artwork (forensic reports, etc.), so the burden of production might shift to Fishlegs. Fishlegs' affidavit is part of the SJ record and rebuts key facts from Hiccup's filing, including whether a theft took place at all. The forensic reports are far from factually conclusive enough to preclude Fishlegs' version of events. Thus, drawing all reasonable inferences for Fishlegs (as the nonmoving party), there is a genuine issue of material fact that cannot be fairly settled based on the SJ record alone. As such, the SJ motion will be denied.
