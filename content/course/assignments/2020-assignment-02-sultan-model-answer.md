## Question 1

Motion to transfer should probably be granted. Under *Hoffman*, RI district court can transfer to MA if MA is a happy court — proper venue and PJ. MA has venue under § 1391(b)(2) because a substantial part of the events giving rise to the claim occurred in MA (contract with MM established in MA, MM recorded in MA, Shrek conspired in MA, deepfake recorded in MA). MA has PJ — forum selection clause ("FSC") indicates Shrek's consent. MA likely has PJ even without FSC; MA district court can piggyback off MA state long-arm statute (4(k)(1)(a)), there are minimum contacts in MA (contract established in MA, Shrek conspired in MA) and suit is fair and reasonable (evidence of tort in MA, MA has interest in hearing claim, minimal burden to Shrek). Under *Atlantic Marine*, usually transfer to FSC forum — no presumption in favor of MM's choice, private interest factors weigh for forum because already privately agreed (also better able to view Donkey's offices which may have evidence and better able to compel Donkey to testify in MA), and public interest factors weigh for forum because MA has an interest in hearing this case of tort committed in MA that implicates MA citizens and MA sellers (MM clients). Note — all 3 states declare FSC invalid, but state law does not apply because *Stewart* held transfer motions, including those made to comply with FSCs, are governed by the transfer statutes (as interpreted by *Atlantic Marine*) which allow FSCs to be considered in transfers. SMJ also present via diversity jurisdiction — complete diversity (Shrek = RI domicile, MM = NJ PPOB) and $500k AIC (> $75k).

## Question 2

First, *Erie* problem because federal common law upholds choice of law ("COL") clauses and state law (of MA or RI, the only two who could possibly matter here) invalidates COL clauses.

*York* outcome determinative test favors state law. Under federal common law, COL clause is upheld (NJ law applies, no defense) whereas under state law COL clause is invalid and HCOL analysis would determine which state law to apply (apply the MA defense, see below). Outcome of suit will be different because Shrek can use MA defense to prevent MM's recovery because MM has access to public outlets. *Hanna* majority test favors state law. Use twin aims of *Erie* to determine whether federal common law is valid and applicable. Federal common law encourages forum shopping; parties will move states to bring diversity actions because they want the ability to specify and enforce COL in contracts, in evasion of state laws that invalidate these clauses. Federal common law also promotes inequitable administration of law because plaintiffs have different access to federal law depending on diversity. Ginsburg and Scalia don't differ in approaches to interpreting federal common law. Harlan's primary conduct test in *Hanna* also favors state law. If federal law is applied, individuals will likely change pre-litigation conduct. Individuals, aware that COL provisions will be upheld, will spend more time and money negotiating and drafting these provisions in every contract they enter into. Therefore, *Erie* analysis suggests state law invalidating COL clauses should apply over federal common law upholding COL clauses. If COL clause upheld, NJ law applies and Shrek can't use the MA defense to prevent MM's recovery.

Next, if state law applies and COL provision is invalid, a COL analysis must be done to determine which state's substantive law applies. Usually, the happy court rule articulated in *Ferens* applies for transfers. Under the happy court rule, RI law still applies after MA transfer if RI is a happy court (which it is — venue is proper and there is PJ as Shrek has RI domicile). However, under *Atlantic Marine*, the happy court rule is not used to determine COL when there is a FSC in order to

discourage gamesmanship and maintain expectations. With a FSC, we apply law of the transferee court (*Klaxon* — apply HCOL rules of state in which transferee federal court sits), which is MA. MA is a R2 state and will therefore conduct a factor-analysis to determine which substantive law to apply. This analysis will likely favor applying MA substantive law, including the defense, because the conduct causing the injury occurred in MA, the relationship between MM and Shrek was created in MA (contract established in MA), and MA likely has an interest in regulating torts that involve its citizens and its businesses (MM's clients). If the factor-analysis favors applying NJ law, the MA public access defense will not be available. However, the only factors favoring NJ law are that MM's loss of value was felt in NJ (lex loci delicti presumption) and MM is a NJ resident (states have an interest in protecting residents — only difference between *Babcock* and *Neumeier* was residency of victim). Therefore, it seems more likely that factor-analysis will weigh in favor of applying MA law.
