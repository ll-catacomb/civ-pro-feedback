**Weasley should sue Bagman and Crabbe in North Carolina state court.**

## Choice of Law Issues

If Weasley sues only Bagman he is unlikely to win enough money to pay for a new Nimbus because Bagman is "quite broke." Weasley needs to sue in a state that will allow Weasley to hold Crabbe Corp. responsible for the tort (presumably Crabbe Corp. is rich and can pay). GA, VA, and DE utilize the respondeat superior doctrine, which will allow Weasley to hold Crabbe Corp. responsible. Therefore, Weasley should sue in a state that will use GA, VA, or DE substantive law.

As Second Restatement states, GA, VA, and DE will use the law of the state that has the most significant relationship to the issue. (*See Babcock*). In this case, that state is probably NC because the brake repair happened in NC, Bagman and Weasley are both domiciled in NC, and the courts will need to grapple with the Bagman-Weasley contract which was signed in NC. However, as First Restatement states, NC and SC courts will use GA state law because the last event necessary to create the cause of action (the car crash and brake failure) happened in Georgia. (*Lex loci delictus*). Therefore, choice of law considerations suggests Weasley should bring the suit in either NC or SC courts.

## State IPJ Issues

Although choice of law suggests that Weasley should bring suit in either NC or SC, jurisdictional requirements only allow him to do so in NC. Weasley cannot bring suit in SC because the state does not have IPJ over Bagman. Bagman is not domiciled there and does not have minimum contacts in the state. (*International Shoe*).

NC courts have general personal jurisdiction over Bagman because he is domiciled there. Bagman was born and raised in NC and has lived there all his life. (*Mas*). NC has no general jurisdiction over Crabbe which domiciled in its place of incorporation (DE) and nerve center (VA) (*Hertz*). However, NC has specific personal jurisdiction through minimum contacts (*International Shoe*; *Kulko*). Given the 15 businesses in NC Crabbe should have reasonably foreseen being hauled into NC state court (*See Volkswagen*). Moreover, Crabbe has "purposefully availed itself" of the NC state laws to run its businesses in NC. Finally, unlike in *Ashai*, the interests of the plaintiff, defendant, forum state, and justice system do not raise concerns about whether it is just to bring suit against Crabbe in NC.

## Federal vs. State Courts

Weasley will be able to avoid federal court. Federal courts will not have arising under jurisdiction because the issues are state tort and contracts law, typical state law issues. There is no *Smith*-*Grable* exception because there is no substantial or disputed federal issue (and this seems like a case that might disrupt the traditional balance of power between state and federal courts if it were moved to the federal arena).

Weasley can also ensure that federal courts will lack diversity jurisdiction, and thereby assure that the defendant(s) will not be able to remove, by suing both Crabbe and Bagman. The claim in this case meets the amount in controversy requirement of more than $75,000, but although Weasley and Crabbe are diverse, Weasley and Bagman are not. The parties will not be *completely* diverse as required by *Strawbridge*.
