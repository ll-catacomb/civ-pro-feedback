## Assignment 1 Logistics and Rules

1. Prepare for the assignment however you'd like. I suggest reviewing and outlining the course materials to date, nothing more.
2. Download the substantive assignment from the course website.
3. Compose a response to the assignment of between 500 and 600 words, including footnotes (if any), case citations (if any), etc. If your response is longer than 600 words, the first 600 will be reviewed and the remainder ignored.
4. You must complete your response within 150 minutes of the time you have finished downloading the assignment. Students are on their honor to comply with this time limit.
5 . Your response must be typed. If you are worried about your ability to type quickly enough, you may use your 150-minute time block to write the assignment out long-hand, then use any length of time to type precisely (including all spelling, punctuation, and other errors) what you wrote. If you choose this option, hand in both the long hand and the typed version.
6. You will not need to do any additional research or read any source materials other than those assigned for Classes 1-6. You will not receive additional credit for citing or relying on materials other than those assigned for Classes 1-6.
7. You may refer to or use any non-human source to aid in your answer. You may not, however, communicate with any person other than Professor Greiner regarding this assignment until its due date/time below. This prohibition includes librarians.
8. Although I will read questions regarding the assignment submitted via email, it is unlikely that I will chose to answer them. If I do answer them, I will forward the question and answer to the entire class. The assignment is straightforward; don't make it more complicated than it is.
9. Your response is due by 8:40 am Wednesday, September 19. Absolutely no excuse will be accepted for a late paper. This unavailability of excuses includes computer problems, printer problems, travel (including the T) issues, alarm clocks, troubles with the HLS uploading system, etc. I suggest doing and handing in the assignment several days before its due date to avoid any timeliness issue.
10. You may return your response via two methods:
   - In paper by 8:40 am of class on the due date; or
   - Via the Drop Box system under the "Assignments" section in the course web page.
If some reason you have difficulty with the upload system on the course web page, print a hard copy of your response and turn it in by 8:40 am on Wednesday, September 19. Note that the Drop Box will close at 8:40 am on the due date.
11. If you believe yourself unable to comply with any of the directions associated with this assignment, you must notify me during or before class on September 12.
12. Your response on this assignment will receive one of three grades: 0%, 2%, or 7.5%. You will receive a 0% if you fail to turn anything in, or if your response is so incoherent as to constitute a failure to complete the assignment. You will receive a 2% if there is a noticeable lack of serious thought in the response. Otherwise, you will receive 7.5%. I anticipate awarding every student in the class 7.5% on this assignment.
13. This assignment will count as 7.5% of your semester grade.
14. PLEASE DO NOT PUT YOUR NAME ON YOUR RESPONSE. Please put ONLY your 8-digit Harvard ID number on your paper.

## Assignment 1 — Substance

You are a member of the staff of the Judiciary Committee in the House of Representative. The chairwoman of the committee is considering a proposal that would repeal 28 U.S.C. § 1367(b) and make stylistic conforming changes (such as removing subsection 1367(a)'s reference to subsection (b) and re-lettering subsections (c)-(e)). No other change to section 1367 is contemplated. The chairwoman requests that you draft a short memorandum addressing the following two questions:

1. What would be the legal and practical effect of the change?

2. Given the historical development of federal court jurisdiction and the current state of the world, is this change a good or bad idea?
