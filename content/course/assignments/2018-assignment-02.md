## Applicable Law

Please review Fed. R. Civ. P. 11(a).

Texas state rules of civil procedure include the following rule. TXRCP 11:

> **(a) Signature.** Every complaint or answer must be signed on each and every page by at least one attorney of record — or by a party personally if the party is unrepresented. Each such page must state the signer's address, e-mail address, and telephone number below the signature. The court shall immediately grant a motion to dismiss responding to a complaint that fails to comply with this rule, and must immediately grant a default judgment against a defendant whose answer fails to comply with this rule. The court may issue an order of dismissal or default sua sponte without notice to the party affected.

In the 2010 case of *Weasley v. Malfoy*, the Texas Supreme Court (the highest court in Texas) held that the purpose of TXRCP 11(a) was to force the attorney of record to review each page of the complaint for accuracy, something it doubted attorneys would do without the signature requirement.

Supposed that in January of 2013, Congress passed, and President Trump signed, the Diversity Limitations Period Act of 2013. It provided as follows: "In all civil actions in which a United States federal district court sits in diversity, the applicable limitations period shall be four years."

Texas has a 2-year limitations period for all negligence tort actions.

### Facts

On May 16, 2018, Padma Patil, a domiciliary of Oklahoma, sued Vernon Dursley, a domiciliary of Texas of Dallas, TX, in the United States District Court for the Northern District of Texas (which includes the city of Dallas). The complaint alleged that on May 12 of 2015, Dursley had played a drinking game at a bar for three hours before trying to drive his car home. On the way home, in the Deep Ellum neighborhood of Dallas, he had driven into oncoming traffic and hit Patil, who was driving her own car, causing her $200,000 in damages to her car and to her person. The complaint alleged that Dursley's drunk driving caused the crash and thus her injuries. Patil's lawyer, Aberforth Dumbledore, signed only the last page of the five-page complaint.

On May 30, 2018, Dursley's attorney moved to dismiss the complaint for failure to comply with Texas' 2-year limitations period and with TXRCP 11.
