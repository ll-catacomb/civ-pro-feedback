## Question

Assume that Alabama has by statute prohibited its courts from dismissing any lawsuit on the ground of forum non conveniens, and that the state follows the First Restatement on Conflicts of Laws.

Peter Pettigrew was born and raised in Mexico. Dedalus Diggle and his friend, Ginny Weasley, were born and raised in Alabama. All three are currently students at San Antonio College in San Antonio, Texas. On May 4, 2007, the three students took Diggle's car across the boarder to Mexico to blow off steam. On May 6, as the students were returning with Diggle driving, the car struck a telephone poll about 10 miles south of the US/Mexico border. The car, damaged but still operational, was driven to a doctor in Mexico, who diagnosed Pettigrew as having a case of whiplash. The doctor put Pettigrew in a neck brace, whereupon both he and the car were transported back to San Antonio. Subsequently, Pettigrew's stay in a San Antonio hospital resulted in major complications, and he incurred about $110,000.00 in medical expenses.

On April 28, 2008, Pettigrew sued Diggle in Alabama state court, claiming that Diggle's negligent driving of the automobile caused Pettigrew's injuries. On May 12, 2008, Diggle removed the case to Alabama federal court. Diggle then answered, asserting as a defense that the road where the accident occurred was in a poor state of repair and had confusing signs, and thus that the accident was not his fault. On the same day as he filed his answer, Diggle moved to dismiss the case on the grounds of forum non conveniens, arguing that the case should be litigated in Mexico.

You are an associate in the law office that represents Pettigrew, and the date is June 9, 2008. The partner handling the case asks you to prepare a memorandum, 625 words or less, analyzing what steps might be taken, and arguments might be made (as well as possible responses), to keep the litigation in a court located in the State of Alabama (where the partner thinks that the jury pool will be more favorable). The partner also asks whether there is any need for haste on the subject.
