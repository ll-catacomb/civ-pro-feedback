# Assignment 1 Model Answer

## Question 1

For one suit, Florean needs SMJ and IPJ and Venue over all three in one location. Florean can sue in DE and maybe OR.

### SMJ

Since all of Florean's claims are state tort/contract claims, there is no AUJ. *Smith-Grable* does not apply because these claims do not involve federal questions. However, there is SMJ under 1332 because there is complete diversity of citizenship (which is based on domicile) as required by *Strawbridge* and AIC > 75k for all claims. AIC for claim against Chang is $225,000, and $112,500 for claim against each Bathilda/Tonks. Florean = OR (*Mas v. Perry*), Chang Corp. = DE (place of inc.) and WA (PPOB) (*Hertz*). Similarly, Bagshot = DE and CA (PPOB), and Tonks = DE and ID. It is okay if defendants share citizenship (DE) because there is complete diversity between parties on opposite sides of the lawsuit.

### IPJ

**Chang** — There is general IPJ in DE and WA. There is specific IPJ in OR. OR has a long arm statute that goes to limit of constitution, and 4(k)(1)(a) allows federal courts to piggy back. By serving as the contractor on a bridge repair in OR, there are more than sufficient minimal contacts among Chang, OR, and the dispute. OR's exercise of jurisdiction would not offend "traditional notions of fair play and substantial justice" (*International Shoe*); OR has an interest in Chang's conduct, which occurred in its borders, and key evidence (regarding the bridge closure) is in OR. However, because Chang does no business in CA and has no contacts there, there is no IPJ there.

**Bagshot** — There is general IPJ in DE and CA. There is likely specific IPJ in OR because of Long arm + 4(k)(1)(a), and Bagshot likely has minimum contacts with OR. While the contract specified grain delivery in CA, the contract was partly negotiated in OR and Bagshot knew Florean would produce the grain in OR. The exercise of jurisdiction would be fair and reasonable because of the business relationship with an OR farmer under *McGee*.

**Tonks** — There is general IPJ in DE and ID. There is likely specific IPJ in CA. CA has a long arm + 4(k)(1)(a). Tonks agreed to receive product in CA by contracting with Bagshot in CA and agreeing to receive corn in CA, and thus purposefully availed itself to the benefits and protections of CA law (*WW Volkswagen*). IPJ in OR is uncertain. It is unclear that by stepping into Bagshot's shoes in a contract Tonks has availed himself to OR law. Tonks might not know that corn is even coming from OR. (This could be more like *Hanson* than *McGee*). A contrary argument might be that Tonks likely does know that the corn came from OR (perhaps from conversations with Dobby, or with Bagshot), and that he purposefully availed itself of the economic benefit of commerce with an OR farmer. But absent any indication that the corn was uniquely "Oregonian," this seems weak.

### Venue

Under 1391(b)(1) Venue could be laid in DE because all defendant corporations are DE residents and all three are subject to PJ in DE. Under 1391(2) venue could be laid against Chang in WA or OR. Florean could argue the events arose in OR (at the bridge) or in WA at the corporate headquarters where Chang made the decision when to repair the bridge. Under 1391(2) Venue could be laid in EDCA against Bagshot/Tonks because corn inspection/rejection occurred there. Florean could try to claim that event that *led* to corn rejection occurred in OR, so it should count for venue, but this could be challenging.

## Question 2

Florean would want WA or OR substantive law for his claims against Chang because there is negligent interference, which is easier to satisfy than the intentional interference required by CA, DE, and ID. The substantive law doesn't matter for Florean's breach of contract claims against Bathilda/Tonks. Florean could sue the parties in DE or maybe OR (if OR has IPJ for Florean's claim against Tonks and Venue for Florian's claims against Bagshot/Tonks) but Florean would be more likely to succeed in DE. A Federal Court sitting in diversity will use HCOL rule of state in which it sits (*Klaxon*).

**OR Analysis**

Florean should NOT sue defendants in OR. OR is a 1st Restatement state for HCOL, which means that courts in OR will apply the substantive law of the state where the last act required to complete the tort occurred to the Chang claim. The final element of the cause of action is "that the plaintiff did in fact suffer such damages," which occurred when Bathilda/Tonks delivered the letter in CA! If Florean sues defendants in OR, Florean will get CA substantive law in the Chang claim, which does not have *negligent* interference with contractual relations but only *intentional* interference.

**DE Analysis**

Florean should sue defendants in DE. DE is a 2nd restatement state and thus would take into account the interests of the state and the parties and use the substantive law of the area most affected. The DE court likely will use OR substantive law, since most of the events happened there (or WA, but WA has negligent interference as well), to determine the claim against Chang. The Chang-Florean relationship is centered on OR; OR has an interest in protecting its resident (Florean) and in regulating conduct that occurred in its borders. Thus, there would be a negligent interference with contract cause of action available, which is better for Florean.
