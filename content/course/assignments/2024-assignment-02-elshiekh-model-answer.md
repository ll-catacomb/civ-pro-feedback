## SMJ

- No SMJ objection since there's DJ: Snow (DE/TX); Peeta (CA); AIC: $400k > $75k.

## PJ

- **GPJ:**
  - There likely isn't GPJ over Snow since Snow is actually at home in DE/TX, not CA.
  - Snow is not "essentially at home" (*Daimler*) since less than 50% of Snow's business is done in CA.
- **SPJ:**
  - CA's long-arm statute with 4(k)(1)(a), shows it welcomes such suits, but there likely isn't minimum contacts (*International Shoe*).
    - Snow didn't specifically target CA/purposefully avail itself (e.g., did not advertise in CA). Snow used Capitol as an intermediary (*McIntyre*), which makes SPJ even harder.
      - Under Breyer's concurrence in *McIntyre*, it's clear that one sale is not enough. Here, there is more than one sale — there's 10, but it's unclear whether 10 sales are enough.
  - Fair/reasonable (*McGee*)
    - Interest of judicial system: This likely points to CA where any evidence from the accident is and likely where any witnesses are. However, there may also be evidence at the manufacturing site in TX.
    - Forum's interests: CA's long-arm statute specifically includes such suits, which indicates CA's strong interest, but TX has an interest in protecting its businesses from meritless product liability suits.
    - CA is more convenient for Peeta, but TX is likely more convenient for Snow since its PPOB and factory are there. Snow doesn't have an office in CA. However, Snow's products are sold in CA, but through an intermediary.
    - HCOL likely points to CA since CA is a first statement state and CA is where the blood is.

## Venue

- Venue is proper in district where substantial events giving rise to claim occurred (§ 1391(b)(2)). Since the accident happened in SD, venue is proper.

## Happy Court (venue and PJ)

- If no PJ (see above), then SDCA not happy court and must transfer.
  - Snow can transfer to NDTX because Snow's PPOB is in TX, so there's GPJ and venue is proper under § 1391(b)(1).
  - Snow can transfer to WDTX. Venue is proper under § 1391(b)(2) since potentially substantial events occurred there since manufacturing happened in San Antonio. There's SPJ because of CA's long-arm statute (4(k)(1)(a)). There's likely minimum contacts (*International Shoe*) since Snow purposefully availed self of San Antonio by having manufacturing site located there.

  - The court could transfer to DE since there's GPJ (Snow is really at-home in DE). Venue is proper under § 1391(b)(1) since Snow is incorporated in DE. However, the *Gulf Oil* factors don't point to DE, so a court wouldn't transfer to DE.
- If there's PJ, then SDCA is a happy court, but Snow **may** still transfer if in the interest of justice.
  - There's a presumption in favor of plaintiff's choice of forum (*Piper*).
  - *Gulf Oil* Factors
    - Private:
      - Sources of proof is likely in both CA, the site of the accident, and TX, the manufacturing site where the archery set likely was misaligned.
      - Any witnesses to the accident are likely in CA.
    - Public:
      - CA expressed local interest in such cases by adding products liability provision.
      - Relation of events to geographic location of court: The site of the accident is in CA, but manufacturing error happened in TX.
  - *Gulf Oil* factors seem to point slightly more to CA, so likely not enough to overcome presumption, making transfer unlikely.

## Transfer/TRCP 8(a)(4) defense

- If Snow can transfer to NDTX or WDTX, then can *maybe* use TRCP 8(a)(4) defense. Snow can get the defense if SDCA is an unhappy court and TRCP is procedural for HCOL.
- If transfer to NDTX/WDTX:
  - TRCP likely is procedural for HCOL because most things arguably procedural are procedural for HCOL. Therefore, apply the law of forum, so TX courts would apply defense.
  - If SDCA was a happy court, then SDCA HCOL rules follow to NDTX/WDTX, so no defense.
  - If SDCA was an unhappy court, then WDTX/NDTX HCOL rules govern.
- TRCP conflicts with federal law, leading to an *Erie* problem.
  - **York:** Peeta doesn't comply with TRCP. If TX law applies, case is dismissed. If federal law applies, case survives. Use **state** law.
  - **Hanna (Scalia):**
    - Valid? FRCP is valid because it really regulates procedure (*Sibbach*).
    - Applicable? Based on plain textual meaning, FRCP addresses pleading standards.
    - Use **federal** law.
  - **Hanna (Ginsburg):**
    - Valid? Same as Scalia.
    - Applicable?

      - Federal Rules are just minimum requirements and TX adds additional requirements.
      - Here, FRCP are silent on additional requirements for products liability, so seems like not applicable → use **state** law.
  - **Harlan:**
    - TX thinks companies are under-investing in production and thinks TRCP 8(a)(4) will change companies' behavior, so since affects primary conduct of parties, use **state** law.
