## (1)

### In Personam Jurisdiction (IPJ)

Florida (FL), Vermont (VT), Michigan (MI), Texas (TX) have long-arm statutes to Constitutional limits.

**FL:** If Montoya is a FL citizen (see below, probably not), FL would have general IPJ: really at home. Otherwise, there are likely insufficient minimal contacts (*International Shoe*): The Pensacola P.O. Box was listed on Montoya's website but never used in Westley/Montoya's transactions; Montoya used a FL bank, but the bank is not relevantly involved in the facts leading to the lawsuit. Regarding fair/reasonable: Montoya has only small ties to FL (voting, license); Montoya can drive/live anywhere for litigation; inconvenient for Westley to come from MI; FL has no interest in noncitizens injured elsewhere; no evidence in FL; HCOL is likely VT. There is likely neither general nor specific IPJ in FL.

**VT:** There are likely not sufficient minimal contacts (*International Shoe*): Although the explosion burned Westley in VT, that's like the accidents in *World-Wide Volkswagen* and *Nicastro*; the accident alone is not enough for minimal contacts, even when Montoya knew Westley was going to VT to use the computer (*WWV*). Regarding fair/reasonable: Montoya has no contacts with VT; inconvenient for both parties; VT has interest in dangerous products brought into its borders and in fire management/prevention; the fire chief to-be-interviewed is in VT, hotel-room's wire-socket to-be-examined in VT; hospital records of Westley's injuries in VT; VT law likely applies for substantive HCOL (place of accident). There is likely no IPJ.

**MI:** If Montoya is a MI citizen (see below, likely), then MI will have general IPJ: really at home. Otherwise, there are minimal contacts (*International Shoe*): damaged computer/Westley in MI, computer shipped to/picked-up in MI, Montoya sold to a MI citizen (*McGee*). Regarding fair/reasonable: Montoya's client (Westley) in MI; long drive for Montoya but can litigate anywhere; MI interested in protecting safety of its citizen, regulating business with citizens; computer/Westley's burns in MI (although see VT above); MI law will likely not apply. There is likely general/specific IPJ.

**TX:** There are likely insufficient minimal contacts (*International Shoe*): Montoya cashed Westley's check in TX, but that's the only contact. Regarding fair/reasonable, Montoya likely has no more contact with TX (unless new clients); inconvenient for both parties; TX has little interest in culmination of business transaction (one cashed check); only physical check in TX (see evidence in VT/MI); and TX substantive law likely does not apply. There is likely no IPJ.

### Horizontal Choice of Law (HCOL)

Statute of limitations (SOL) procedural by state-court precedent: use forum law.

Clock starts 4/14/17 when Westley suffered injuries, it's now 3 years later (it's 5/2020). Westley cannot sue in MI or TX (2-year SOL), but can sue in FL and VT (4-year SOL).

Westley's best choice is FL and hope that it has general IPJ (still within SOL).

## (2)

### Subject-Matter Jurisdiction (SMJ)

**Arising Under Jurisdiction (AUJ)**

Products-liability = State-law cause of action (COA). There is no federal COA (*Mottley*) or federal issue embedded (*Smith-Grable*).

**Diversity Jurisdiction (DJ)**

The amount-in-controversy (AIC) is sufficient under § 1332 ($200k > $75k).

Montoya's citizenship: domicile pegged at MI (birth), but he moved to FL, showed some intent to reside there, which may be enough to change domicile (*Mas*). Montoya leased FL RV plot for only 1-year, but longer than his subsequent leases, and many leases are only one year. He registered to vote in FL, obtained FL driver's license, opened bank account in FL although he did so in a nation-wide bank accessible by him anywhere. Although he closed his FL P.O. box, he left address on his website. But his home/RV moves! Montoya's domicile likely MI but maybe FL. Subsequent 4-month moves had insufficient intent to change domicile afterwards.

If Montoya is a MI domiciliary, there is no DJ (Westley domiciled in MI too) because we lack complete diversity (*Strawbridge*). If Montoya is a FL domiciliary, § 1332 is satisfied.

There is likely no SMJ, meaning he likely cannot sue in federal court.

### IPJ

Under F.R.C.P. 4(k)(1)(A), federal court in FL, VT, MI, TX may exercise IPJ if FL, VT, MI, TX can. See above.

### HCOL

If DJ, federal court uses HCOL rules of state in which court sits (*Klaxon*). See above.

Ultimately, if Montoya FL domicile, have DJ: can sue in federal court in FL (general IPJ, within SOL). Otherwise, no SMJ for federal court.
