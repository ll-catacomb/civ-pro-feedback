## Question

Assume the following: California and Arkansas follow the First Restatement on choice of law (and you know the choice of law approach in New York). Both the New York and the Arkansas Rules of Civil Procedure include rules worded identically to those of Fed. Rs. Civ. P. 8 and 12. In the 2008 case of *Granger v. Skeeter*, a defamation action, the Arkansas Supreme Court held that the Arkansas version of these rules established a system of notice pleading akin to the Seventh Circuit's decision in *American Nurses* and the United States Supreme Court's decision in *Swierkiewicz*. The New York Court of Appeals held similarly regarding its version of the rules in the 2009 case of *Weasley v. Malfoy*. Both courts considered and explicitly declined to follow the Supreme Court's interpretation of Fed. Rs. Civ. P. 8 and 12 in *Bell Atlantic* and *Iqbal*.

Arkansas has an antidiscrimination statute similar to Title VII (or, if you prefer, to § 1983, as applied to these facts). As applied to the Paula Jones fact situation, the essential elements of this Arkansas statute are as follows:

1. that the plaintiff refused to engage in a sexual relationship with the defendant;
2. that the plaintiff suffered adverse employment consequences;
3. that these adverse consequences were orchestrated by the defendant because of the plaintiff's refusal to engage in a sexual relationship;
4. and that the defendant's actions were taken "under color of state law."

Assume that there is no res judicata (preclusion) or limitations problem with Paula Jones' ability to file a second lawsuit against Bill Clinton. (In other words, I don't want you discussing or considering preclusion or limitations in this assignment!) Assume that Bill Clinton is currently domiciled and residing in Manhattan, which as far as federal courts go is in the jurisdiction of the Southern District of New York.

Jones has decided to sue Bill Clinton (again) using exactly the same factual allegations as were in paragraphs 6–57 of her first, Eastern District of Arkansas complaint in the case (those paragraphs are attached to this assignment), except that instead of alleging that Clinton is president of the United States and living in D.C., she will allege that he is a New York resident and domiciliary (assume that he is in fact a Manhattan resident and domiciliary). She refuses completely to consider changing any of the factual allegations in the complaint. The relevant language of the complaint is attached to this assignment. Assuming she can get a court to apply it, her sole and only cause of action would be the Arkansas anti-discrimination statute. She plans on requesting $200,000 in damages.

You are Jones' lawyer. In what courts might Jones sue Clinton? What would be the legal advantage or disadvantage of the various choices? What legal issues would the various choices raise? (Do not consider the political aspects of any lawsuit, or jury pools, or judge selection, or convenience of the parties, including forum non conveniens.) What would you recommend?
