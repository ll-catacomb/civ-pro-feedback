Assume the following, which is totally fictional (including the law part).

The federal rules of civil procedure authorize the pre-judgment seizure of an item under the following circumstances:

- The court has subject matter jurisdiction over the plaintiff's lawsuit, and the plaintiff must file a lawsuit in a reasonable time after the seizure of the item;
- The object is within the geographic boundaries of the seizing district court; and
- The plaintiff asserts, and supports with competent evidence, an ownership interest in the item to be seized.
- In the event that the court orders a seizure, it shall hold a hearing on the topic of whether to continue to exercise dominion over the item within 60 days of the seizure.

All states relevant to this assignment have long-arm statutes going to constitutional limits. All states relevant to this assignment have the following cause of action:

- **Theft:** The elements of this tort are the wrongful removal of control over an object from those who should rightfully have it. The tort is complete where the object was when it was removed from the control of those who should have it (under either the laws of property or a duly executed contract assigning such rights).
