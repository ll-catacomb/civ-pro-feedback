**Assignment 1 Draft Answer**

## Question 1

There likely would be SMJ.

There is no AUJ because there is no federal cause of action (*Mottley*) or embedded federal issue (*Smith*).

There may be DJ. Perrin is domiciled in Texas (once-a-month trips to California do not change one's domicile). Damodred's domicile may be Texas, where she was raised, instead of New York, since she only rented her apartment in NY and was hoping to attend a fixed-length school program. However, she did inquire into buying an apartment in NY and potentially getting a full-time job there. This may showcase an intent to stay in NY (*Mas*). Tuon is domiciled in NY, and Ishamael is domiciled in Nevada.

If Damodred is domiciled in Texas, then there is no DJ (*Strawbridge*), and the case would get dismissed or split. Perrin could try keeping Damodred in the suit by invoking SJ — since this is part of the same case or controversy, but this would not work per § 1367(b), because it would break complete diversity. If Damodred is domiciled in NY, then there is complete diversity.

Since Perrin seeks $100,000 from each defendant, which exceeds the amount-in-controversy requirement of $75,000, per § 1332, there is DJ.

## Question 2

All states below have a to-the-limits long-arm statute to constitutional limits, such that federal courts can piggyback off it (4(k)(1)(A)).

### NY

If Damodred is a NY domiciliary then there is GIPJ. If not, there is still sufficient minimum contacts. Damodred is likely "at-home" in NY since she spends greater than 50% of her time there (*Daimler*). Also, Damodred underwent training/her interview for the job in NY, and the issue at hand centers around her inadequate training (even though it was virtual, so the location is likely gratuitous).

The court likely lacks IPJ over Ishamael. Ishamael doesn't target New York through advertising nor have any offices there (*McIntyre*). A mere likelihood that a product will enter the forum state is insufficient (*Volkswagen*). He entered a contract with a NY domiciliary, *McGee*, but the F&R factors weigh against exercising IPJ, as NY is inconvenient for Ishmael and there likely are not

many witnesses or evidence in NY to corroborate Ishmael's negligence (though there may be witnesses in the NY bar that can attest to Damodred's lack of training). NY law will not apply.

### Nevada

A Nevada court would have GIPJ over Ishamael and potentially IPJ over Tuon, but there are likely insufficient contacts for Damodred. Tuon was in Nevada when she provided Damodred with training on operating the apparatus, but this meeting occurred virtually, making Tuon's location gratuitous. Damodred has no other connection to Nevada. The F&R factors weigh against exercising SIPJ because it is inconvenient for Damodred, who has never been, and Perrin, although NV does have an interest in regulating possible dangerous activity in its borders.

### Texas

A Texas court may have GIPJ over Damodred, but likely not sufficient minimal contacts for the others. No other defendant has any contacts at all to TX. Texas has no connection to the events of the case (harm/negligent act occurred elsewhere), other than its interest in protecting its domiciliaries (Perrin).

### California

A California court likely has IPJ over Damodred and Tuon, but not Ishamael.

Damodred worked there, purposefully availing herself of the privilege of conducting activities in California (*Hanson*). Also, California is F&R since a significant amount of evidence is there, the harm occurred there, and Tuon/Damodred/Perrin got there once so it isn't significantly inconvenient to return. CA law will apply per HCOL (see below). Additionally, California has an interest in managing negligent businesses within its forum.

However, IPJ is harder for Ishamael. He hasn't been to and doesn't advertise in California, and allegedly negligently trained Tuon in Nevada. The apparatus ending up in California after Tuon took possession (*Volkswagen*) resembles the unilateral action of a third party, which is insufficient to establish SIPJ (*Hanson*).

## Question 3

Regardless of what state hears the suit, California law will apply, allowing for contributory negligence.

The question of available defenses is substantive, not procedural, so we go through a HCOL analysis. The harm was felt in California (where the accident occurred). Any First Restatement state (California/Texas/Nevada) would apply California law and allow for contributory negligence.

<!-- page: 3 -->

NY, as a Second Restatement state, would likely also apply California law. We start with the presumption for California. Section 6 factors do not overcome this presumption. Significant evidence and witnesses are present in California, it is a convenient forum for the plaintiff and most defendants because they got there once, and CA has an interest in regulating potentially dangerous conduct occurring within its borders. Therefore, NY will apply CA law, allowing contributory negligence.
