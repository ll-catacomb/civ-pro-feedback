There is SMJ. Plaintiff is citizen of SC, defendant is corporation incorporated in DE with PPOB in MD, and AIC of 200K > 75K, meaning there is diversity jurisdiction. (*Strawbridge*). Court will not sua sponte remand to PA state court.

**(i)**

The partner needs to take a vacation <30 days to timely file a motion to remand arguing procedural defects — defendant's removal on May 7 was untimely because it was >30 days after service on April 1. This is the only way to return to PA state court.

**(ii)**

Defendant's motion to dismiss unlikely to succeed. Under *Iqbal/Twombly*, we eliminate paragraph 14 as legally conclusory. Existence of duty is always present. Paragraphs 10-11 describe breach through failure to exercise ordinary care despite knowledge of Podrick's severe allergy. Paragraph 11-12 describe causation. Paragraph 12 describes damages. The complaint is not just possible but plausible and contains sufficient specificity.

Motion to transfer is unlikely to succeed.

EDPA is a happy court, so the court does not have to transfer. EDPA does not have GPJ over Daario given it is a DE corporation with PPOB in MD, and there is unlikely an argument for "at-home" (*Daimler*) since Daario appears to ship and sell its plants across US East Coast (unlikely >50% of plants sold in US is sold in PA). Under FRCP 4(k)(1)(A), court will use PA long-arm to constitutional limits. Minimum contacts (*Shoe*) for SIPJ are likely available because Daario purposefully availed itself to doing business in PA by having an office there (*McIntyre*) and having consumers contact its PA office phone numbers to purchase. Both the purchase of the plant and the switching of the plant occurred in PA. It would be fair and reasonable (*McGee*) because Daario has an office in PA, PA has interest in negligent sales within its borders, and a key witness Stark works there. Venue is proper because case was removed.

DSC is likely happy court, so court may transfer. Similar arguments suggest no GPJ over Daario in SC. SIPJ similarly utilizes FRCP 4(k)(1)(A) and SC long-arm. There may be minimum contacts because Daario purposefully availed itself to doing business in SC by shipping products there directly without an intermediary (*McGee*, contra *McIntyre* and *Hanson*), but less contacts than between Daario and PA. It could be fair and reasonable given SC has interest in injuries from dangerous products shipped within its border, SC law likely applies in HCOL analysis (where the injury happened), and evidence of type of plant is in SC. Venue is proper under § 1391(b)(2) since allergic reaction occurred there.

Given EDPA is happy, apply *Gulf Oil* FNC test. Since Podrick is domestic, presume their choice of forum in PA is appropriate, then consider private and public interest factors. Evidence is spread across SC and PA. Witness Stark is in PA. The injury happened in SC, but the negligent act occurred in PA. Given *Gulf Oil* factors could go either way, they likely will not overweigh presumption of plaintiff's choice in PA so court will likely not transfer from EDPA to DSC.

Firm should file PRCP 8(f)(2) motion because Daario failed to attach to its motion to dismiss an affidavit of a licensed physician. The PA federal court will apply PA HCoL rules, *Klaxon*, and anything arguably procedural is procedural for HCoL, so 8(f)(2) is procedural for HCoL, and would apply. PRCP applies in PA state court but may not in EDPA or DSC, so if motion to remand is not timely filed, we apply *Erie* tests to determine if federal court follows FRCP 8 or PRCP 8.

Under *York*, it is likely outcome-determinative because it determines damages amount, though possibly an argument that it doesn't determine who wins, which is the real outcome, but this is a weak argument. Apply state law.

Under *Hanna* majority, FRCP is valid because Congress delegated authority to promulgate FRCP in the Rules Enabling Act. FRCP is a housekeeping rule which is procedural since it really regulates procedure (*Sibbach*) and does not abridge/modify substantive rights.

Scalia: The federal rule is applicable because its plain meaning suggests FRCP directly conflicts with PRCP. The FRCP does not require any physician affidavit. Given supremacy clause we follow FRCP and not state law.

Ginsburg: The federal rule is likely inapplicable because we consider the policy behind state rule and see that federal rule can be narrowly construed to avoid conflict. Here, the FRCP can be considered to set a minimum baseline for personal injury torts so states are free to set stricter standards, since they would still comply with FRCP. Follow state law for federalism reasons so state law applies.

Under *Harlan*, federal law may only cause alteration in conduct of lawyers in what motions they choose to file. However, given PA state enacted PRCP 8 to attract physicians into the state, that is likely substantive primary conduct that could be changed if we apply federal law, so follow state law.
