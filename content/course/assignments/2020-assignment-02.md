## Substantive Assignment

Massachusetts is a Second Restatement state for choice of law. New Jersey and Rhode Island are First Restatement states. All three states have long-arm statutes that allow their courts to exercise jurisdiction whenever the U.S. Constitution would so permit.

Federal common law upholds so-called "choice of law clauses" in contracts, which are provisions in contracts specifying that disputes related to that contract will be governed by the law of a specified state.

The law in all fifty of the United States includes the tort of "Business Disparagement." Under this tort, a defendant is liable to a plaintiff if the defendant participates in the production of a false or misleading video that harms the reputation, public perception, or standing of a business in a way that causes a loss of value to the business. The last event necessary to complete the Business Disparagement tort is the infliction of the harm, in the form of the loss of value by the business or of the stock of that business. But: Massachusetts, unlike any other state in the United States, provides an affirmative defense to the Business Disparagement tort called the "Public Access" defense. Under Massachusetts law, the Public Access defense means that if a defendant proves that the affected business has constant and ready access to the public or to media outlets, then the plaintiff cannot recover.

In early 2019, the legislatures of Massachusetts and Rhode Island (but not New Jersey) passed the Uniform Forum Selection and Choice of Law Prohibition Act. Under this Act, which since early 2019 has been the law in both Massachusetts and Rhode Island, both choice of forum clauses and so-called "choice of law" clauses (clauses purporting to specify either the choice of law rules that will apply to a dispute or the substantive law that will apply to a dispute) are invalid, and may not be enforced in court.

Shrek was a business investor domiciled in Rhode Island with a net worth of approximately $150,000,000. Shrek commuted daily to Boston, MA to work in his offices there, but he returned home every night to his family in Rhode Island.

MM Corporation, Inc. was a New Jersey corporation with its principal place of business in Jersey City, New Jersey. Its stock was publicly traded on various stock markets.
