## Q1

**SMJ:** The requirements for diversity jurisdiction are clearly met, as we have complete diversity with MM, a New Jersey domiciliary, on one side and Shrek, a Rhode Island domiciliary (he clearly lives here, not in Massachusetts) on the other, and $500,000 clearly exceeds the required $75,000 amount in controversy.

## Transfer Motion

**VCOL:** Per *Klaxon*, the Court would treat HCOL as substantive in diversity cases and so will apply Rhode Island procedural law banning the forum selection clause, unless trumped by the transfer statutes. This is the same fact pattern as *Stewart*: the federal transfer statutes say to honor forum selection clauses, conflicting with state law banning such clauses, so we apply the valid and applicable test:

*Validity*: Article III gives Congress the power to create the lower federal courts, so combined with the Necessary and Proper Clause, Congress clearly has the power to pass the power to enact the transfer statutes.

*Applicable*: The transfer statutes are clearly applicable.

Therefore, the federal transfer statutes apply. Now to apply them:

There is no *Hoffman* problem as Massachusetts can clearly exercise personal jurisdiction over Shrek because it has a long arm statute that the federal court can piggyback under rule 4(k)(1)(a), he commutes to work there every day, giving minimum contacts, and the exercise of jurisdiction is fair and reasonable given the location of the transaction. Under *Atlantic Marine*, the transfer statute analysis presumes that the case should be transferred to the location selected by the FSC, and the court should only consider the public interest factors, not the private interest factors, and see whether they overwhelm this presumption. They clearly do not here, as Rhode Island has no real interest in this dispute, while Massachusetts has substantial interests. Therefore, the court should transfer.

## Q2

Because there is a conflict between federal and state law, we have another VCOL problem.

**If FCL:** FCL upholds choice of law clause, so look to NJ substantive law, which has no public access defense.

**If State law:** *Atlantic Marine* specifies that if there is a forum selection clause, transferee forum law applies, regardless of whether transferor court was "happy."

<!-- page: 2 -->

Therefore, Massachusetts is the relevant state. Massachusetts does not enforce choice of law clauses, so we apply Massachusetts HCOL rules. It is a 2d Restatement state, so we start with *lex loci deliciti* but apply the § 6 principles. Everything about these events except for the harm took place in Massachusetts; all of the conduct took place there; Donkey and Mulan are exclusively based there, while Shrek conducts his business there; there were no justified expectations that other law would apply, and the relationship was clearly centered there. Massachusetts has an interest in protecting its citizens and governing business relationships centered there; etc. Therefore, we apply Massachusetts law, and the public access defense is available.

### *Erie* Tests

***York*:** If the public access defense is available, then the plaintiff cannot recover because MM's business is communicating with the public on social media, so it clearly has access to the public. Therefore, the choice between the two is likely to be outcome determinative, state law applies, and the public access defense is available.

**Harlan Concurrence:** Availability of public access defense likely affects primary conduct — if the defense is available, defendants will be more likely to utter business-disparagement-like statements when it would apply, so we apply state law, and the defense is available.

**Hanna Majority** (for FCL, Ginsburg and Scalia are identical): Federal common law is the source of the applicable federal law, so we look to twin aims of *Erie*: avoiding forum shopping and promoting equal protection before the law. If we follow federal law, we likely have a forum shopping problem because interstate disputes can obtain a different outcome by using federal law and parties will therefore forum shop. We also don't promote equal protection because like parties are treated differently. Therefore, we apply state law, and the defense is available.

**Summary:** All *Erie* tests point to the defense being available.
