## Assignment 3, Question

The Committee in charge of recommending alterations to the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure to the US Supreme Court is considering a proposal to replace portions of the Fed. Rs. Civ. P. with the following rule.

> **Rule X: Compulsory Joinder:**
>
> (a) Notwithstanding any existing rule or doctrine of common law to the contrary: Any person pleading a claim for relief (whether in the nature of a claim, counterclaim, cross claim, or third-party complaint) shall join any additional person against whom she knows she has a claim for relief and shall assert such claim, provided that the claim against the to-be-joined party arises out of the same transaction or occurrence (or series of related transaction or occurrences) as the first claim for relief.
>
> *Interpretive note: For the purposes of this rule, "person" means any entity or individual who can sue or be sued in court.*
>
> (b) Nothing in this rule should be interpreted to create subject matter jurisdiction in the federal courts where it does not otherwise exist.

You are a staff attorney for the Committee. The Committee asks you for a memorandum addressing the following questions.

## Questions

**1)** What would be the effect of Rule X in the following situation under the best interpretation of the Rule?

Antioch Peverell, a famous opera singer domiciled in South Carolina who earns $200,000 per performance, received an outpatient tonsillectomy. The throat surgeon was Dr. Gabrielle Delacour, a Georgia domiciliary. Dr. Delacour performed the surgery in the Atlanta offices of Dursley Medical Care, Inc. ("DMC"). DMC is incorporated in Georgia with its principal place of business in South Carolina. The anesthesiologist in the surgical procedure was one Dilys Derwent, a Georgia domiciliary in town for the week. DMC employs Dr. Delacour and Dr. Derwent. And all jurisdictions relevant to this question have the doctrine of respondeat superior.

Three months after the surgery, Peverell could not hit his high notes and had severe dizzy spells, causing him to miss three of his performances. He consulted a different doctor, who told Peverell he had received an inappropriate mix of anesthesia and that the surgery on his tonsils had been botched.

Peverell sued Dr. Delacour in Georgia federal court for medical malpractice. Peverell and Delacour settled, and pursuant to the settlement, Peverell dismissed his lawsuit with prejudice.

Peverell then brought suit in Georgia state court against DMC and Derwent for medical malpractice. DMC and Derwent moved to dismiss, or in the alternative, for summary judgment, citing Rule X. What result?

**2)** In your opinion, should this proposed rule be adopted? What would be the advantages of the rule? The disadvantages?
