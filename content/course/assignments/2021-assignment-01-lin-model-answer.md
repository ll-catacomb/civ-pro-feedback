## #1

**AUJ:** Negligence is a state claim so there is no federal COA (*Mottley*), but there may be a substantial federal issue embedded within the state law claim. *Smith-Grable*. The alleged negligence stems from the failure to comply with 49 C.F.R. § 5X5. Congress expressed substantial federal interest in protecting pedestrians from the hazards of self-driving trucks. However, the defendants are unlikely to contest the law. The issues in this case are questions of fact. Additionally, exercising federal jurisdiction any time a plaintiff uses failure to comply with federal law to prove a state COA would sweep up too many cases into federal court and violate the division of labor between state and federal court systems. There is likely no *Smith/Grable*.

**DJ:** There is SMJ through DJ because there is complete diversity. *Strawbridge*. It is a close call whether Buffy is a PA or NC domiciliary. Buffy has not heard about admission to UPenn so it is unclear whether she will continue to reside in PA (*Mas*). However, because Buffy really likes PA, it is possible she intends to reside in PA whether or not she gets into UPenn. XH is a citizen of DE (incorporation) and MD (PPOB, *Hertz*). Faith is a citizen of NC (incorporation) and SC (PPOB, *Hertz*). The AIC is well above $75K.

## #2

SC and NC have no general IPJ over XH because XH is not a citizen and it does not do any business in those states, so it cannot be essentially at home (*Daimler*) nor have the minimum contacts required for specific IPJ.

MD and DE have no general or specific IPJ over Faith because Faith is not a citizen and does no business in either state.

Faith is not a citizen of PA, but PA has specific IPJ. PA has a long-arm going to the limits of the constitution and FRCP 4(k)(1)(A) piggybacks on state IPJ. There are likely minimum contacts. *International Shoe*. Faith keeps the trucks for sale, including the one delivered to Buffy, in a rented parking facility in Philly until delivery. Although the sale was over the internet, the negligence occurred in Philly, where Chase delivered the truck without conducting a test of the front proximity detector. Faith advertises in PA through newspaper ads and television ads, purposefully availing itself of doing business in the state and rendering it foreseeable that it could be called to litigate in PA. It is fair and reasonable because the witnesses and documents relating to the accident are likely to be in PA, it is convenient for the plaintiff, the harm occurred in PA, PA has an interest in protecting its' citizens and regulating business conduct occurring in its' borders.

XH is not a citizen of PA so there is no general IPJ. It is a closer call whether there is specific IPJ over XH, but it is unlikely. Like *Nicastro*, XH is an out of state manufacturer that sends their products through an intermediate purchaser-reseller. There are no contacts with PA since they

sell their products in VA. XH did not conduct any sales activity in PA, it does not advertise in PA and it does not deliver to PA. While XH does require that its' purchases must resell directly to a consumer, it's likely not enough that XH put its' goods into the stream of commerce, making it possible for their products to end up in PA. They must have targeted the forum. *Nicastro*.

XH and Faith are not citizens in nor essentially at home in VA, but both have minimum contacts with VA. XH operates an enormous purchase and sale clearinghouse market facility in VA. Faith advertises, sells and purchases self-driving vehicles in VA. They both purposefully avail themselves of doing business in VA by entering business transactions in VA. These contacts are related to this lawsuit: the failure to inspect forming the basis of the claim occurred in VA.

Since "failure to inspect" will only likely constitute negligence under NC and VA substantive law, Buffy has a better chance of prevailing under a second restatement state because the HCOL analysis will consider factors other than where the harm occurred. In a first restatement state, we apply PA law because it's the place where the injury occurred (Buffy got hit in PA). VA and DE are both 2nd restatement states, but DE likely does not have PJ over Faith so Buffy is more likely to prevail by filing in VA.

Second Restatement analysis presumes we apply the law of the place where the injury occurred unless the center of gravity test demonstrates otherwise. The accident occurred in PA, but the conduct causing injury occurred in Alexandria because both XH and Faith were supposed to conduct a test of the front proximity detector. VA has an interest in regulating business activity in the state and all XH's sales were conducted in VA. PA also has an interest in regulating businesses who sell to PA residents. The relationship between Faith and XH was formed in VA while the relationship between Faith and Buffy formed in PA. Because key events occurred in both VA and PA, the court is unlikely to depart from *lex loci delicti*, but it's more possible using VA HCOL analysis.
