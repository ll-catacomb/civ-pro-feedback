## Q3

### Brennan *Celotex* framework (FRCP 56)

Court will grant if Buffy shows no genuine dispute as to any material fact and that Buffy is entitled to judgment as a matter of law. Buffy has the burden to produce evidence that, if unrebutted, would compel a rational trier of fact to rule in her favor (drawing all reasonable inferences for the nonmoving party).

Buffy meets burden of production via Maclay/Angel affidavits because they show that XH/Faith didn't inspect (breach) when presented with scratches and dents in the front suggesting malfunctions in bumping into things, and in fact, the trucks did bump into things at the Spike, followed by the lack of inspection, caused cars to hit Buffy (causation).

<!-- page: 2 -->

The burden of production shifts to XH/Faith, whose Chase affidavit (alleging testing demonstrating that trucks stopped when approaching solid objects) seems to show a genuine issue for trial.

However, because Buffy has Angel's photos of the fronts of vehicles showing dents and scratches (XH/Faith do not attempt to contradict), this is analogous to *Scott v. Harris*: no rational trier of fact can disregard this evidence, which shows that the fleet of trucks purchased do *not* stop when approaching solid objects. So judge cannot draw the reasonable inference in favor of XH/Faith w/r/t whether or not XH/Faith inspected the vehicles (they clearly didn't, or if they did, they did not react appropriately).

All in all, court would likely grant Buffy's motion for SJ under FRCP 56, but this depends on VCOL analysis (below).

### VCOL

Buffy sought damages for pain and suffering in a negligence action, but did not provide affidavit from licensed psychiatrist detailing nature/extent of plaintiff's mental distress, implicating VRCP 56 § 6.7(b)(i). If this section applies, they will deny Buffy's motion. Erie analysis needed.

**York:** outcome determinative because state/federal dictates whether or not motion for SJ will be granted. Use state law.

**Harlan:** wouldn't change Buffy's pre-litigation conduct. This is about attaching an affidavit to a complaint — which would be *during* litigation. However, perhaps (as the General Assembly found in the Giles Act § 6.7(a)) companies like XH/Faith who are typically defendants would become more aggressive in their conduct, perceiving less danger of litigation where plaintiffs have a hard time complying with VRCP 56 and procuring an affidavit from a licensed psychiatrist detailing nature/extent of mental distress (perhaps due to lack of merit in the case). Overall, probably use state law, but it's close.

**Hanna/Scalia:** Apply FRCP framework. Valid because under *Sibbach*/2072, really regulates procedure. Applicable because federal/state law both address what a SJ motion, as interpreted by *Celotex*/Brennan, must contain. Use federal law.

**Hanna/Ginsburg:** Same validity test as Scalia. Applicability = similar approach to *Cohen* — FRCP silence on needing psychiatrist affidavit means no particular preference on the issue, allowing states like VA to impose additional requirements. No conflict. VA expressed its interest in § 6.7: VA does not want litigation to deter investors from starting or investing in industries in VA. Use state law.

All in all, state law likely applies, so motion for SJ likely dismissed.
