## Substantive Assignment

You are a legal aid attorney in St. Louis, MO. One morning, Hermione Granger walks into your office to ask for your help. Hermione had just received seizure orders from two different state courts, one in Illinois, the other in North Carolina. She asks for your help with both of them.

### The Illinois Bank Account Seizure Order

Hermione was born in St. Louis, MO (note: "MO" is the postal code for the state of Missouri) and has lived in St. Louis for all of her life. She lived most of her life in poverty. A deflected curse early in her career as an Auror for the Ministry of Magic left her disabled, and she survived largely on disability benefits, supplemented by payouts from a program run by the state of Missouri. Sometimes, especially long and cold winters drove up her heating bill, she struggled to keep up financially. She had to pay her heating bill, put food on the table for herself and her children, take care of rent, and maintain her life and the lives of her children. Sometimes, she had to charge basic life necessities to an American Express card, which she struggled to pay off. To keep her financial life simple, she had only ever used the single AmEx card, and had only opened a single checking account. All charges on the AmEx card were with local vendors in St. Louis. The checking account was with Hogwarts Bank, Inc., an Illinois corporation with its principal place of business in Fairmont City, IL. Hogwarts was a small community bank with three outlets, all located in or around Fairmont City, IL. Hermione's parents had used the bank, so Hermione continued to patronize it even though she had to cross the state line between St. Louis, MO, where she lived, and Fairmont City, IL, where the bank was located, to visit the bank. She used a bus to make the trip.

During 2018, Hermione got behind on the payments to her AmEx card, and the heating company threatened to cut off her heat. She ended up charging a lot to her AmEx card, but then struggled to make the monthly payments. Hermione eventually stopped making payments to AmEx in October of 2018. She now thinks that she had about $400 left to pay on the card. Around that time, Hermione closed her mailbox at the local St. Louis post office. She had been getting all her mail delivered to the post office box for almost two decades, but she decided that she wanted to save $5 per month it cost her to keep it to help to pay for heat, and she hated getting mail, anyway. She told Hogwarts, her utility companies, and her friends to start using her apartment address for mail (she was able to receive mail there), but no one else.

In April of 2019, things went from bad to worse for Hermione. She woke to find a letter from Hogwarts waiting for her. The letter informed her that, pursuant to a writ of seizure issued by an Illinois state court, her Hogwarts checking account had been seized/frozen. She could no longer write checks on it, and any checks she had written that hadn't cleared yet would not be honored. The letter included a copy of the writ, reciting that the petitioner on the writ was WeBuyDebts.com, Inc. The letter, which Hermione showed you, recited that as of the time of the seizure, the bank account had $975 in it.

With Hermione sitting in your office, you do a little research on the Internet about WeBuyDebts.com. You find out as follows. WeBuyDebts.com buys delinquent debts from credit card companies and sues the alleged debtors to try to collect. WeBuyDebts.com is known for its aggressive investigative staff. The company's website includes a description of its "beyond outstanding" investigative team, reciting that the team regularly peruses social media, government records, the pages of local newspapers, and any other source of information to find assets belonging to alleged debtors that it can seize.

<!-- page: 3 -->

You are able to look up the Illinois state court lawsuit online, and you quickly review the filings in the case. From the complaint, you find out that it is a quasi-in-rem suit. WeBuyDebts.com asked the court to seize the bank account as part of the quasi-in-rem suit. The underlying basis of the lawsuit was the alleged non-payment of $400 owed on the AmEx card. The complaint alleged that WeBuyDebts.com had purchased the rights to collect on the debt from AmEx. The complaint alleged that "attempts to contact Ms. Granger via mailing to a P.O. Box in St. Louis to resolve this issue were not successful." The complaint asked for recovery in the amount of $400, plus fees and costs and interests totaling $300, for a total of $700. Also included in the case file was the motion for seizure of the Hogwarts, IL bank account. The motion for seizure restated the statements in the complaint, and sought seizure of the bank account as assurance of payment, as per a historically standard quasi-in-rem action, for $700 allegedly owed. The motion was signed by an attorney. A judge signed the order the same day the motion was filed, and the writ of seizure was served on the bank the next day. There were no other papers in the file.

### The North Carolina Racing Broom Seizure Order

Going back to January of 2019, Hermione thought her luck had changed. She'd been able to save about $900 in her checking account. More importantly, her friend Fenrir Greyback reached her through one of Hermione's cousins who also lived in St. Louis (Hermione herself did not own or rent a phone). Fenrir said that he had a great investment deal for her. All she had to do was to mail him a money order for $500. Fenrir would make her half owner of a rare, extremely valuable racing broom. The broom was on sale from a store outlet of a company called "WeBuyDebts.com" (the same company as in the Illinois case, above). According to Fenrir, WeBuyDebts.com bought debts, and sometimes accepted valuable items in return for forgiving debts. It frequently sold those valuable items in its outlet stores. One such store was in Columbia, SC, where Fenrir lived, and that store had the racing broom in question. The broom was priced at only $25,000.00. Fenrir assured Hermione that the broom was really worth at least $50,000.00. Fenrir proposed that Fenrir himself would buy the broom for both of them on an installment contract. He would make a first payment of $1,000, using Hermione's $500 plus $500 of his own money. They would both have to sign an agreement to pay $1,000.00/month until the $25,000.00 was paid off, or they could repay the entire purchase price at any time. Fenrir assured Hermione that he could resell the broom very quickly, so quickly that they would never have to make the payment due one month after the purchase of the broom.

Hermione agreed. Fenrir mailed Hermione a copy of the WeBuyDebts.com purchase agreement, which Fenrir himself had already signed. Hermione signed it in January, made a copy for herself at her local library, and mailed it back to Fenrir, along with a money order for $500.00. Her signature on the WeBuyDebts.com purchase agreement included her address, as follows, "Hermione Granger, #4 Privet Drive, St. Louis, MO." Fenrir wrote to Hermione in mid-February to say that he had purchased the broom the day before, giving WeBuyDebts.com the $1,000 ($500 from Fenrir and $500 from Hermione), and was looking for a buyer.

<!-- page: 4 -->

In late February, Fenrir wrote Hermione with the following news: From his home in South Carolina, Fenrir bought advertisements in print newspapers (Fenrir was old-fashioned) in South Carolina (where he had lived for all his life) and Virginia (where he had some friends). He took no other action to publicize the sale. The ads all said the following words, or words to this effect: "Hermione Granger of St. Louis, MO and Fenrir Greyback of Columbia, SC seek to sell rare racing broom they own. Call Fenrir Greyback, XXX-XXX-XXXX, to hear a description of valuable broom and to offer bid." Fenrir's ads drew the attention of a reputable racing broom dealer in Roanoke, VA, and Fenrir negotiated a purchase price of $65,000.00. Fenrir wrote to Hermione to tell her this news, and Hermione had high hopes, as half of the proceeds of the broom sale was more money than she had ever had in her life.

In early April of 2019, Fenrir arranged to drive the broom himself to Roanoke on a particular day. On his way from South Carolina to Virginia, however, just after he drove into North Carolina, a sheriff from a North Carolina state court pulled him over. The sheriff showed Fenrir a writ of seizure issued the previous week by a North Carolina state court authorizing seizure of the broom. The writ showed the petitioner to be WeBuyDebts.com, Inc., a Delaware Corporation with its principal place of business in New York. Fenrir had to give the broom to the Sheriff. He drove back home and called Hermione's friend to get in touch with her to give Hermione the bad news. He emailed a copy of the writ to the mutual friend, who gave Hermione a printout. In the email, Fenrir admitted that he never made the first month's payment on the broom, which was due in the middle of March, 2019.

You recognize WeBuyDebts.com as the same company that had requested the seizure order of the Illinois bank account. You are able, on the Internet, to download the papers from the North Carolina case.

- The complaint alleges that Hermione and Fenrir failed to make the payment on the broom due in the middle of March, 2019. The complaint further shows that the case is an in rem proceeding; WeBuyDebts.com asked the court for an order declaring that it owned the broom and returning the broom to its possession. The complaint alleges that WeBuyDebts.com retained an ownership/property interest in the broom until all of the installments were paid off.
- Attached to the complaint is the purchase/sale agreement for the broom. You see in that agreement that, indeed, it provides that WeBuyDebts.com retains an ownership/property interest in the broom until all payments were made. The agreement also provides that in the event of any missed payment, the entire amount of the loan becomes immediately due, and that the seller (WeBuyDebts.com) may petition any court of competent jurisdiction for an order to seize the sold item (here, the broom).
- Also attached to the complaint is an affidavit from an employee at WeBuyDebts.com swearing under penalty of perjury that the copy of the purchase/sale agreement is true and correct, and that the March payment was never paid.
- Also attached to the complaint is a copy of the receipt given to Fenrir for the initial $1,000 payment made at the time when he first bought the broom in Columbia, SC, in early February.
- The last documents in the file are a motion for seizure, incorporating all of the documents just described by reference, and the North Carolina Court's seizure order signed by a judge, which was issued the same day the motion was filed.

### Questions

You do a little investigating. You find out that both the North Carolina and Illinois legislatures had some years ago adopted the Uniform Pretrial Seizure Code, a model statute governing predisposition civil seizures of property.

- Based on what you know thus far, what (i) ***procedural due process***, and (ii) ***quasi-in-rem/in rem jurisdiction over the property arguments*** could you make against the seizures in either North Carolina or Illinois or both?
- The seizures were implemented in North Carolina and Illinois using the same statutory scheme, the Uniform Pretrial Seizure Code. You decide to review this statute to try to form (i) ***procedural due process***, and (ii) ***quasi-in-rem/in rem jurisdiction over the property*** arguments you could use to attack its constitutionality. What aspects of this statute will you look for to see if they might support arguments against seizures like those your client suffered? What corresponding arguments might you make?
