## SMJ

It's a declaratory judgment action so we consider the imaginary lawsuit if Connor sued Skynet/Reese ("Connor's lawsuit") to assess SMJ in Skynet/Reese's declaratory judgment action against Connor ("declaratory judgment action").

AUJ is likely not met. There is no federal statute that Connor can sue Skynet/Reese under (*Mottley*). Violation of the Intoxicated Pilots Act (federal statute) however could serve as a basis of Connor's theory of negligence per se (that Reese's drunkenness violated T-800), so we look to *Smith Grable* factors. The federal issue at stake is relatively substantial (risk to public safety is important) but is a fact bound inquiry (whether pilot here was drunk in violation of the statute, compare with constitutionality of federal bonds or proper administration of IRS policies). Allowing this case in federal court is likely to increase number of negligence cases being brought in federal courts under state causes of action via trying to use violation of a federal statute for negligence per se (burdensome for federal courts). Plus Congress affirmatively indicated that private individuals could not bring suit under Intoxicated Pilots Act, suggesting that Congress wants to limit the cases involving this statute that are brought into federal court. *Smith Grable* exception likely does not apply.

DJ is also unlikely met in Connor's lawsuit. DJ requires complete diversity and AIC being at least $75k. Reese is domiciled in AZ because he resides there and intends to keep living there for the rest of his life (*Mas*). Connor is likely domiciled in AZ because parents still live there and she is in college elsewhere but with no intent to remain there. She could arguably be domiciled in CO because currently lives there and may intend to keep living there, but based on the available information, including that she maintains her AZ driver's license, she is likely domiciled in AZ. Skynet is domiciled in Mexico because incorporated and PPOB there (*Herz*). There is not complete diversity between Reese and Connor but there is complete diversity between Skynet and Reese.

Connor could also argue that the AIC is insufficient (based on the damages she intended to sue for) as to the claim between her and Skynet, so DJ altogether fails. Even if Skynet argued that the AIC is higher, Connor could assert that her medical bills were well below $75k and even the amount she intended to sue for pain and suffering was additionally below $75k.

## IPJ

Connor could contest that the federal district court in CO lacks PJ over her. Likely no GPJ because she is most likely an AZ domiciliary. If *Daimler* applies to people then it's possible there is GPJ because she's a student in Boulder and spends much of the year there, but

otherwise no GPJ. For SPJ, CO has a long arm statute and 4k1a applies. There are unlikely sufficient minimum contacts between Connor, CO, and the subject matter of the litigation. Connor called Skynet from CO to make the reservation, but all of the flight tour occurred over AZ. Plus, fair and reasonableness factors lean away from exercising SPJ. Reese and Skynet are principally located out of state, so limited convenience for them (plus Skynet is foreign company, no offices or resources in U.S., even less convenient than if were just geographically distant). Connor resides in CO, so it's convenient for her to litigate in CO. Evidence and witnesses likely in Mexico or AZ (plane, doctors from Connor's treatment), though also some in CO (Ventura). Court in CO might want to assert PJ and take the case because Connor is a student there and also might have some claim to air traffic safety for Skynet flights in general (although not for this flight). It's close but Connor could win by contesting PJ.

## HCOL

For purposes of HCOL, statute of limitations is procedural, so we look to law of the forum (CO). In CO, the statute of limitations for a negligence suit is 1 year. At the time of filing the declaratory judgment action, about 15 months has passed since Connor's Grand Canyon tour. Unlikely that Connor can challenge SOL if declaratory judgment action makes it this far.

## Colorado River

Colorado River is irrelevant since there is no simultaneous state court lawsuit (nothing for federal court to abstain from).
