### Background Facts and Law

*(Note: all of the following is fictional.)*

New Jersey uses the First Restatement for horizontal choice of law. Oregon uses the Second Restatement.

New Jersey law provides that forum selection clauses are void as against public policy and courts must refuse to enforce them. Oregon law provides that forum selection clauses are presumptively valid and courts must enforce them absent compelling countervailing circumstances. For purposes of this assignment, whether a forum selection clause is enforceable is a substantive issue.

Malfoy Industries, Inc. ("Malfoy") is incorporated in Delaware, with its principal place of business in Portland, Oregon. Malfoy manufactures automobiles. Its most popular product is the Comet 260 sedan.

Hermione Granger is a citizen of New Jersey. She purchased a new Comet 260 in 2015 in New Jersey. The purchase contract contained a forum selection clause (Paragraph 9.75) specifying that all disputes arising from the purchase must be filed in courts located in Portland, Oregon. The contract also contained a warranty provision (Paragraph 4.5) under which Malfoy warranted that the Comet 260 would be free from defects in materials or workmanship for the life of the car.

Farley Granger is Hermione's husband. He is also a citizen of New Jersey.

In June of 2017, Hermione was driving the Comet 260 on Interstate 5 near Eugene, Oregon. The steering mechanism failed, causing the car to veer off the road. The car crashed, injuring Hermione seriously. Farley was a passenger in the car and was also injured.

<!-- page: 3 -->

Hermione hired an attorney in Portland, Oregon. Her attorney advised her that Malfoy had breached Paragraph 4.5's warranty, and that this breach was actionable under a New Jersey consumer protection statute and Oregon product liability law. Her attorney further advised that Paragraph 9.75 (the forum selection clause) was valid. Malfoy, by contrast, believed that Paragraph 9.75 was unenforceable.

Seeking to litigate in a court it found favorable, Malfoy filed a declaratory judgment action in New Jersey state court in September of 2017. The complaint sought a declaration that Paragraph 9.75 was unenforceable under New Jersey law and that the venue and forum for any disputes should be in New Jersey.

Hermione's attorney, who also represents Farley, removed the case to the United States District Court for the District of New Jersey (USDC-DNJ), invoking diversity jurisdiction.

Hermione then filed a counterclaim in the USDC-DNJ asserting her warranty and products liability claims. Farley filed a claim in the same action asserting his own personal injury claims arising out of the same accident. Hermione and Farley each claim damages well in excess of $75,000.

### Assignment Questions

1. Malfoy moves to remand the case to New Jersey state court, arguing that there is no SMJ. Should the USDC-DNJ remand the case?

2. Assume Malfoy's remand motion fails. Malfoy then moves to transfer the case from the USDC-DNJ to the United States District Court for the District of Oregon (USDC-DOR). Should the USDC-DNJ grant the transfer? If so, what law will the USDC-DOR apply to Hermione's claims?

3. Assume instead that the case remains in the USDC-DNJ. Does the USDC-DNJ have SMJ over Farley's claims?
