# Assignment 1: Model Answer

---

Gringotts should file a declaratory judgment lawsuit in South Carolina federal court. To hear the case, the court must have subject matter jurisdiction through either "arising under" or diversity jurisdiction. Because the declaratory judgment act does not create a federal cause of action, we must show that Helga Hufflepuff (HH) could have filed her lawsuit in federal court. Imaginary Lawsuit Rule.

Establishing "arising under" jurisdiction under both Article III and § 1331 may prove difficult because breach of contract is a state law claim. The federal law at issue, the EHMA, would not be the basis of HH's cause of action, but only an anticipated defense. Thus, according to the Well Pleaded Complaint Rule, the EHMA would not establish "arising under" jurisdiction. *Mottley*.

Still, Gringotts could argue that a federal court can exercise "arising under" jurisdiction because the case involves an important federal issue: the constitutionality of the EHMA. Using the *Grable* balancing factors, Gringotts can argue that this federal issue (1) is very important, as Section 1 of the Act characterizes EHMA provisions as "the most important to have passed Congress in history"; (2) is legal, not factual; (3) is live; and (4) will not upset the state/federal balance. Given that Gringotts Bank has modified several contracts to comply with the EHMA, it will likely show that the issue is live. However, if too many contracts were modified, a federal court may decide that the federal issue will arise too frequently and upset the state/federal balance.

Alternatively, Gringotts can try to establish diversity jurisdiction. § 1332 allows a federal court to exercise jurisdiction over a state law claim if (1) the parties are completely diverse and (2) the claim meets the amount in controversy requirement. Being that HH lost her $80,000 down payment, she will likely satisfy the $75,000 amount in controversy requirement.

To be diverse, HH and Gringotts may not be domiciled in the same state. Gringotts is a domiciliary of both Delaware and North Carolina. Delaware is its state of incorporation and North Carolina, as Gringotts' operational headquarters, is its principal place of business. Nerve Center Test. Gringotts must show that HH is a domiciliary of South Carolina because diversity jurisdiction fails if HH is deemed a domiciliary of North Carolina.

Gringotts can argue that HH is a domiciliary of South Carolina because she moved and officially established her residency there. It can also argue that she lacks a definite plan to leave because she bought a house there. However, HH can argue that her domicile never changed from North Carolina (her place of birth) because she does not plan to reside indefinitely in South Carolina. She plans to move to New York to pursue a career in international business.

Even if Gringotts establishes federal subject matter jurisdiction, it still must show that a South Carolina federal court can exercise personal jurisdiction over HH. Gringotts must show that (1) HH has minimum contacts with South Carolina and that (2) jurisdiction comports with fair play and substantial justice. *International Shoe*. Being that HH lives in South Carolina, bought a house there, and applied to the South Carolina mortgage czar for her mortgage modification, Gringotts will have little difficulty satisfying either prong of the test.
