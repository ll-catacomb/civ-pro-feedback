# Assignment 1: Model Answer

**Word count: 897**

Tonks can file suit in six systems: CA state court, OR state court, federal court in CA, federal court in OR, DE state court, or federal court in DE. She should file in CA state court because it's likely CA state court will apply OR law on outrage, so Tonks can avoid CA's cap for damages, and because WWW would be unable to remove to federal court.

## CA State Court

CA has general IPJ over WWW because WWW is a citizen of CA; its principal place of business/nerve center (*Hertz*) is in Sacramento.

Problem for Tonks is that CA's outrage law caps damages, so she wants OR outrage to apply. HCOL analysis suggests that CA state court will probably use OR substantive law: Is outrage law procedural or substantive? If procedural, CA law would apply. But outrage is substantive, so CA's COL rules apply. CA is Second Restatement state, so case is split up into issues. Focusing on damages alone, both center of gravity theory and Section 6 factors suggest that OR law should be applied since the harm was done in OR and because OR has legitimate interest in protecting its citizens. There is some uncertainty here though since Second Restatement is subjective, and CA could argue that it has a strong interest in protecting its corporations from high damages.

Another advantage of CA state court filing is that WWW cannot remove because WWW is a citizen of CA (§ 1441(b)(2)).

## OR State Court

Tonks needs to establish that OR state court has IPJ over WWW, which may be difficult. OR likely doesn't have general jurisdiction over WWW because its only connection to the state is its manufacturing plant there, and *Daimler* makes general IPJ hard to establish. OR might be able to establish specific jurisdiction. Assuming OR has long arm statute, constitutional basis for IPJ is through minimum contacts test (*International Shoe*). First, the connection between WWW, OR, and the subject matter is tenuous: WWW's only contact with OR are its production facility, but the prototype WAMs in this case weren't made at that plant. WAM was brought to OR by the unilateral action of third party, Tonks (*Hanson*). Moreover, WWW didn't know Tonks lived in OR; invitations were emailed. On other hand, Tonks can argue that WWW intended WAM users to take WAMs back to their homes, and WWW eventually knew WAM was operating in OR. Second part of minimum contacts analysis is to consider other factors, which cut both ways in this case. E.g., WWW didn't purposely avail itself of OR with in WAMs. But, OR has an interest in litigating and protecting its citizens. So disadvantage to bringing suit in OR state court is it's unclear if court has IPJ over WWW.

Advantage to bringing suit in OR is that OR law on outrage and damages would apply, and there'd be no damages cap. Outrage tort is substantive, and OR is First Restatement. Where was blood spilled? All of the tortious activity happened in OR: Tonks' emotional distress all happened in OR. So, apply OR substantive law. Tonks thus can get high damages in OR state court.

## Federal Court in CA

Case cannot get into federal court through FQJ because there's no federal tort of outrage and no federal issue at stake.

Case can get into CA federal court through DJ: Tonks domiciled in OR; WWW is domiciled in CA (and DE) (*Hertz*). Complete diversity satisfied. However, AIC depends on whether OR or CA law governs on damages. Problem is that if CA's damages cap applies, AIC is not met.

So what law is applied? Federal court will do HCOL outlined above for a CA state court. Likely that OR substantive law would be applied, so AIC would likely be met. DJ thus satisfied.

Federal court in CA has IPJ over WWW via 4(k)(1)(a): since CA state court has IPJ, so too does a federal court in CA.

Venue is okay since WWW is citizen of CA (§ 1391(b)(1)).

Disadvantage to bring suit in federal court is that federal courts have not been receptive to outrage claims.

## Federal Court in OR

SMJ analysis is similar to federal court in CA: no FQJ; must have DJ. Complete diversity met, and because federal court in OR will apply OR law (same HCOL analysis as discussed for OR state court above), AIC is met.

IPJ carries up from OR state court's IPJ via 4(k)(1)(a).

Venue is fine under § 1391(b)(2), since a substantial portion of events occurred in OR: this is where the data was taken from Tonks and transmitted back to WWW.

Same disadvantage here: federal courts have not been receptive to outrage claims.

## DE State Court or Federal Court in DE

A DE court has IPJ (general jurisdiction) over WWW (and thus DE federal court has IPJ under 4(k)(1)(a)) because WWW is incorporated in DE and is thus a citizen of that state. Federal court SMJ is the same as above, venue lies because WWW is citizen of DE.

An advantage of filing in DE is that WWW cannot remove because WWW is a citizen of DE (§ 1441(b)(2)).

If DE is a 1st Restatement HCOL state, analysis would be like OR, above. If DE is a 2d Restatement HCOL state, analysis would be like CA, above. DE law would not apply.
