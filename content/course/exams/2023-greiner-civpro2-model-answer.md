## Question 1

There is no federal CoA or issue and thus no AUJ. There is likely DJ. Perrin is a TX domiciliary (short CA trips do not establish residency), and the AIC is $400K. Damodred was born in TX, but regarding change of domicile, she (i) has been living in NY and (ii) intends to/hopes to settle down in NY, as evidenced by her only-NYC college plans plus that she alas likes Brooklyn plus that she is making inquiries to purchase real estate (Mas). <!-- uncertain: "Mas" appears to be a case citation abbreviation; original may read "Mas." as a shortened cite for Massachusetts or a domicile case --> If she has insufficient intention to stay, then she remains a TX domiciliary/citizen, and the court will dismiss (*Strawbridge*).

If Damodred is a NY domiciliary, there is general IPJ. If not, then under 4(k)(1)(A), SDNY will use constitutional-limits NY longarm. Damodred's three-year, stable residence in NY may trigger GIPJ under *Daimler*. Otherwise, SDNY will consider whether Damodred's use of computers, cell phones and physical spaces in NY to place her job-seeking ads, to conduct her job interview, and above all (given its link to the CoA) to receive the allegedly deficient video training is sufficient to constitute minimal contacts. If so, SDNY will find strong other contacts linking Damodred to NY (her residence for some years), making litigation there convenient for her, but will find NY an inconvenient place for Perrin, the situs of no relevant evidence (unless there were witnesses to Damodred's video and telephone conversations), and unlikely to be the state whose law is used (see below). Specific IPJ might fail on F&R.

(b)(1) venue is proper in the EDNY, where Damodred lives, not in the SDNY. (b)(2) venue depends on the tenuous minimal contacts to SDNY listed above. Venue is proper in the EDCal under (b)(2) (situs of the accident), so (b)(3) is inapplicable. Venue is likely improper.

Under *Iqbal*, we eliminate paragraph 11 as conclusory. Paragraph 4 describes an interaction imposing a duty of due care, paragraph 8 alleges sale of a ticket knowing that training was insufficient, paragraph 9 describes causation, paragraph 10 damages. The complaint is not just possible but plausible.

If IPJ is lacking or venue improper, the court must transfer, so long as some happy court exists; EDCal is obviously happy (4(k)(1)(A), long-arm to constitutional limits, loads of Damodred contacts including sale of ticket and allegedly negligent conduct there, F&R because CA law (see below) plus witnesses and evidence (the apparatus) plus not too inconvenient for Damodred because she worked there). The EDNY is also happy if Damodred is a NY domiciliary (GIPJ due to residence, (b)(1) venue). If SDNY is happy, SDNY may still transfer. SDNY will begin by presuming that it will honor the plaintiff's SDNY choice. Re private interest factors, there are no witnesses or evidence other than Damodred in NY, and SDNY is convenient for Damodred but not Perrin. Re public interest, there is little nexus to NY, juries will not be familiar with the situs, and NY law is unlikely to apply (see below). SDNY would analyze the same factors (minus the presumption) if it must transfer to decide where, and likely pick EDCal due to CA law as well as the presence of more witnesses (e.g., Zarine) and evidence (e.g., the construct).

## Question 4

Rule 14 (or 20, common issue is who is at fault for the same accident t/o) authorize Perrin to sue Tuon, and a federal CoA triggers 1331 SMJ. Venue is proper under (b)(2) (accident site), and IPJ over Tuon is permissible, see above.

Although this seizure is not strictly QiR (because it is not a precursor to establishing jurisdiction), it raises all *Shaffer* concerns because the court is nevertheless seizing a piece of property and must have power to do so. In addition, the EDCal court lacks GIPJ over Tuon, and thus power what she owes cannot follow from general power over her. The power analysis is thus the same as QiR. If *Shaffer* preserved traditional quasi-in-rem (meaning *Shaffer* turned on DE's overreaching sequestration statute), then the presence of both the BustYourYouKnowWhatBumpers apparatus and the Mustang in EDCal is sufficient to provide power for seizure of both. If *Shaffer* imposed *Shoe* doctrine for QiR, then the Act authorizes the seizure (analogous to a long-arm), but the Mustang bears no relationship to the CoA, and no link exists between Tuon's ownership interest in it and the CoA. The BustYourYouKnowWhatBumpers apparatus is highly related to the CoA, but there is still little link to Tuon's ownership interest. The Mustang seizure is unconstitutional, and the apparatus seizure may be.

PDP doctrines, however, disallow the seizure. The alternative is not to seize and wait for an IPJ lawsuit to conclude, then follow standard effectuation of judgments procedures. Under *Mathews*, Perrin's interest only in securing payment is weak (*Doehr*) and provides no reason for urgency (Tuon is unlikely to hide or waste her livelihood or primary means of transport) with no preexisting property right; the risk of an erroneous seizure is high (a testimony-needed tort dispute (*Doehr*), with no sworn affidavit required, so

## Question 5

If state law applies, and if SDNY was unhappy, EDCal will use the CA PI standard, and there is no preventable irreparable harm to Perrin; the accident has already occurred, so money is sufficient. There is no Erie conflict.

If SDNY was a happy court (see above), the EDCal will use NY procedural law including the NY PI standard. The BustYourYouKnowWhatBumpers is a constant public danger, so the PI may issue. There is an Erie conflict because if federal law applies, then just as with CA law, there is absolutely no irreparable harm, and because Tuon has a strong interest in continued access to the apparatus (her livelihood), rendering a PI unlikely. Under *York*, this difference is not outcome-determinative because merits litigation will continue regardless of the PI, use federal law. Under *Harlan*, easy-to-obtain PIs might make arguably dangerous business (amusement parks, surfing, big slides) reluctant to operate, but the connection is indirect, leaving the situation unclear. Under the *Hanna* majority, the federal law is FRCP 65, which does not abridge, enlarge, or modify a substantive right (§ 2072) because it really regulates procedure (*Sibbach*). It is applicable under the Scalia interpretation because it (under its judicial interpretation) specifies the four PI prerequisites/factors. A narrowing interpretation is hard to articulate, suggesting the same result under Ginsburg.

## Question 7

At 20 customers per day over multiple days, numerosity is present. Regarding commonality, almost any state's HCOL framework would result in CA law, and the dual threats of insufficient training and improper construction are the same for all customers. Regarding typicality and representativeness, Perrin was allegedly put in danger (and later hurt) by the propeller and the water levels. But water levels may vary day to day, as may propeller construction. In addition, the CA contrib defense makes each plaintiff's claim dependent on how that plaintiff operated their boat. Finally, we know of only one person actually hurt, Perrin. All this threatens commonality and typicality (*Wal-mart*). Perhaps all this may matter less because the class seeks an injunction only, invoking 23(b)(2). Certification thus depends on the court's view of the factual specificity of the circumstances of each plaintiff's claim and of the contrib defense; will the court focus only on the exposure to danger (all class members) or actual injuries?
