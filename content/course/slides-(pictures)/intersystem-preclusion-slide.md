## Text Extracted from Document

**Intersystem** — in intersystem preclusion we are asking which jurisdiction's definitions will govern the above elements.

- Only preclude party who lost in L1, or those in privity — always required (DPC = Due Process Clause, 14th Amendment): no matter how flexible preclusion doctrine becomes (especially with nonmutual preclusion), you can only bind someone who had a full and fair opportunity to litigate in L1. This is a constitutional floor, not just a doctrinal choice.
- Same claim — *e.g.* *Migra*
- A judgment that is final and on the merits — *e.g.* *Semtek*
- Necessary to the decision — *e.g.* *Rios*
- Mutuality — discussed in *Blonder-Tongue* and *Parklane Hosiery*
