## Description

This diagram poses a diversity jurisdiction problem as a preview to *Owen Equipment & Erection Co. v. Kroger*.

The plaintiff (State A) brings state law claims exceeding $75K against two corporate defendants:
- **Def. corp. 1** — incorporated in State B, principal place of business in State B → fully diverse from plaintiff
- **Def. corp. 2** — incorporated in State B, principal place of business in **State A** → shares citizenship with the plaintiff, **destroying complete diversity**

The question posed ("What Do We Do With This?") sets up the issue: can the plaintiff use supplemental jurisdiction to keep Def. corp. 2 in the case even though it destroys diversity? *Owen v. Kroger* answers this question.
