# Complete versus Minimal Diversity

> **Diagram type:** Two side-by-side arrow diagrams contrasting complete and minimal diversity scenarios.

**Bottom diagram (orange — complete diversity):**

- P2 (SC) → D2 (NC)

## Description

The diagram contrasts **complete diversity** and **minimal diversity** using two multi-party scenarios. Each diagram shows two plaintiffs (P1, P2) and two defendants (D1, D2), with arrows indicating the party pairings.

**Top diagram (green) — minimal diversity only (no complete diversity):**
- P1 and P2 are both from South Carolina (SC)
- D1 is from Virginia (VA); D2 is from South Carolina (SC)
- Because P2 (SC) and D2 (SC) share the same state citizenship, **complete diversity is destroyed** — § 1332 is not satisfied
- However, **minimal diversity exists** because at least one plaintiff (P1, SC) is diverse from at least one defendant (D1, VA)

**Bottom diagram (orange) — complete diversity:**
- P1 and P2 are both from South Carolina (SC)
- D1 is from Virginia (VA); D2 is from North Carolina (NC)
- No plaintiff shares citizenship with any defendant — **complete diversity is satisfied** and § 1332 is met
- Minimal diversity also exists (it is a lower bar, always met when complete diversity is met)

**Key rule:** § 1332 requires **complete diversity** — every plaintiff must be diverse from every defendant. The Constitution only requires **minimal diversity** — at least one plaintiff diverse from at least one defendant.
