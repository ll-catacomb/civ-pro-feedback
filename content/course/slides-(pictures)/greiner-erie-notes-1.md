## Page 2 — Service of Process (Understanding *Hanna* and *Walker*)

To understand what's going on in *Hanna* and *Walker*:

Two things required to get a lawsuit rolling:
1. File the complaint with the Court
2. Serve process on the defendant (a way of giving the defendant notice of the suit)

First one (filing) is easy — if plaintiff doesn't file with the court, obviously no lawsuit.

Second one: Service of process — rules/law say service must be done in certain ways; these vary federal/state, state to state.

**Two ways to serve that were at issue in *Hanna*:**
- "in hand" = tag, hand complaint to defendant
  - Fed ct: permissible but not required, see FRCP 4(e)(2)(A)
  - Some states (e.g., state ct in *Hanna*): only way
- Leave complaint at defendant's house
  - Fed ct: permissible, FRCP 4(e)(2)(B)
  - Some states: no good

**Note:** If a lawsuit is filed with the court, but the defendant is not served properly then the court can dismiss on its own, or a defendant can move to dismiss (even though to do so, the defendant had to have known about the lawsuit).

## Page 3 — Time Limits and Statutes of Limitations

**Time limits** — Biggest one: the statute of limitations

Timeline diagram:
- Triggering event → Limitations period → "commencement" of lawsuit
- *e.g.* accident / breach of contract
- What is required to "commence" a lawsuit? Filing? Service?

Most state courts and federal courts follow this scheme:
- Triggering event
- Limitations Period
- Limitations period expires → Grace period → Plaintiff files → Plaintiff serves

Rules:
- Plaintiff can file at any time after the triggering event
- Once filed, must serve within a "grace period"
- So this is permissible

**States and federal "grace period" lengths differ:**
- Federal court: Fed R Civ P 4(m): 120 days
- OK (state at issue in *Walker*): 60 days

## Page 4 — *Hanna* & *Walker* Comparison

**Hanna** (left):
- State law: must serve defendant by hand = tag
- Federal law: fine to leave complaint at defendant's home, FRCP 4(e)(2)(B)
- SCOTUS: federal law governs, lawsuit **lives on**

Timeline:
- Complaint filed
- Def. served by leaving complaint at home
- Def. never tagged
- → Limitations period expires → State grace period ends (within dashed box)

**Walker** (right):
- State law: 60-day grace period
- Federal law: 120-day grace period
- SCOTUS: state law governs, lawsuit **dismissed**

Timeline:
- Complaint filed
- → Limitations period expires → State grace period → Federal grace period
- Def. served (in some way that complies with both state and federal law)
