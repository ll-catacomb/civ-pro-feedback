## Description

This diagram illustrates a scenario involving removal, a counterclaim, and impleader.

**Original action:** The General contractor (NC) sues the Buyer (DE) for breach of contract — a state law claim exceeding $75K. Diversity jurisdiction exists (NC vs. DE, > $75K).

**Removal:** The Buyer removes the case to federal court in North Carolina.

**Post-removal developments:**
1. **Counterclaim** — The Buyer asserts a breach of contract counterclaim against the General contractor (state law, > $75K). This is a compulsory or permissive counterclaim within the same action.
2. **Impleader (third-party claim)** — The General contractor impleads the Subcontractor (NC, NC), arguing: "if I have to pay the Buyer, the Subcontractor has to pay me because it was their fault." This is a state law claim exceeding $75K.

The impleader raises a supplemental jurisdiction question: the General contractor and Subcontractor are both from NC, so there is no independent diversity basis for that claim. Whether § 1367 supplemental jurisdiction covers this impleader claim is the issue.
