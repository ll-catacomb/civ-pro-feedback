## Description

This diagram illustrates the facts of *Owen Equipment & Erection Co. v. Kroger*.

1. **Original claim:** The plaintiff (IA) sues the Power company (NE) for negligence — state law, > $75K. Diversity jurisdiction exists (IA vs. NE).

2. **Impleader:** The Power company impleads the Crane operator, arguing: "if I have to pay, you should pay because it was your fault." At the time of impleader, the Crane operator is listed as (NE, NE) — still diverse from the plaintiff.

3. **Citizenship change:** The Crane operator's citizenship shifts to (NE, **IA**) — the same state as the plaintiff. This destroys complete diversity between the plaintiff and the third-party defendant.

4. **Plaintiff's claim against third-party defendant:** The plaintiff then asserts a direct negligence claim against the Crane operator (now IA). Because the Crane operator shares Iowa citizenship with the plaintiff, there is no independent diversity jurisdiction over this claim.

The Supreme Court held that the plaintiff could **not** use supplemental jurisdiction to bring a direct claim against a third-party defendant when doing so would destroy complete diversity — § 1367(b) later codified this limitation.
