## Description

The diagram shows two parallel court hierarchies — state and federal — both ultimately subject to review by the **Supreme Court of the United States** at the top.

**State system (left):**
- The **State Trial Court System** is at the base, divided into specialized divisions: Family, Criminal, and Housing
- Appeals go up to the **State Intermediate Appellate Court**
- Further appeals go to the **State High Court**
- From there, cases raising federal questions may be appealed (dashed line) to the U.S. Supreme Court

**Federal system (right):**
- Trial-level cases are heard in the **United States District Court** (blanks indicate a specific district, e.g., "Eastern District of New York")
- Appeals go to the **United States Court of Appeals for the ___ Circuit** (blank indicates circuit number, e.g., Second Circuit)
- Further appeals go to the **Supreme Court of the United States**

The dashed pink lines represent discretionary or certiorari-based review paths to the Supreme Court, while solid lines represent the standard appellate route within each system.
