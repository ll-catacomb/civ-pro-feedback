## Description

This diagram illustrates the facts of *Aldinger v. Howard*, a supplemental jurisdiction case involving a county employee who brought two claims arising from the same events:

1. A **§ 1983 federal civil rights claim** (federal law) against the supervisor individually
2. **State tort claims based on vicarious liability** against the County itself

The issue is whether the federal court has supplemental jurisdiction over the state law claim against the County. At the time, counties were excluded from § 1983 liability (*Monell* had not yet been decided), meaning the County could not be sued under the federal claim — and Congress had implicitly signaled it did not want federal jurisdiction to extend to such entities. The Supreme Court held that supplemental jurisdiction could not reach the County, reasoning that when Congress limits a party's federal liability, courts should not use supplemental jurisdiction to pull that party into federal court anyway. *Aldinger* raised the broader question of whether supplemental jurisdiction could extend to additional parties (not just additional claims), which Congress later addressed in 28 U.S.C. § 1367.
