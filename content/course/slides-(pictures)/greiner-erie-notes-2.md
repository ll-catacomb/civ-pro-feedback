# Erie Tests — Three (Plus One on the Way)

<!-- page: 1 -->

> **Diagram type:** Structured outline comparing four Erie tests/approaches.

## Text Extracted from Document

### *Guaranty Trust* — Outcome Determinative?
- Yes → state law
- No → federal law
- This almost always leads to state law

### *Hanna* Majority — Valid + Applicable (Scalia interpretation)
- **Constitutional provision:** presume valid, ask whether it applies; courts very unlikely to construe narrowly
- **Statute:** Valid? Find constitutional provision support enactment. Applicable? Courts unlikely to construe narrowly
- **FRCP:** Valid? Cannot abridge, enlarge, or modify state substantive right. But this always met. Applicable? Possible narrow construction (*Walker*)
- **Federal Common Law:** Twin aims of *Erie* (forum shopping, inequitable administration of laws) — *This is York!!* Almost always leads to state law

### Harlan in *Hanna* (concurrence) — Primary Conduct Test
- Would applying federal law cause alteration in the primary conduct of the parties?

### On the Way — Ginsburg in *Shady Grove*
- Precursors: *Walker!* But for federalism reasons, not preservation of precedent
- Same as *Hanna* Majority (Scalia), except more likely to interpret federal law narrowly to avoid a conflict with state law, particularly FRCP
