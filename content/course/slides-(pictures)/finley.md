## Description

This diagram illustrates the facts of *Finley v. United States*, a supplemental jurisdiction case involving a plane crash that killed a man and his two children.

The plaintiff brought:
1. A **Federal Tort Claims Act** claim against the **FAA** — federal law, with **exclusive federal jurisdiction** (cannot be heard in state court)
2. **State negligence claims** against the **City of San Diego** (CA) and a **Utility company** (CA) — state law claims exceeding $75K

The problem: the state law defendants (City and Utility) share California citizenship with the plaintiff, so there is no diversity jurisdiction over those claims. The only basis for federal jurisdiction would be supplemental jurisdiction. The Supreme Court in *Finley* held that supplemental jurisdiction did not extend to additional parties, a decision Congress later overruled by enacting 28 U.S.C. § 1367.
